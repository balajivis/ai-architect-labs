# Agent Capabilities Spectrum

Not every "AI agent" is equally autonomous. The capabilities spectrum is a way to place systems on a scale from simple, fixed assistants to highly autonomous agents that plan, use tools, and self-correct over many steps.

## Key ideas
- At the low end: a chatbot that answers from a prompt with no actions or memory.
- In the middle: assistants that call a few tools or follow scripted workflows.
- At the high end: agents that plan multi-step tasks, choose tools dynamically, and adapt.
- More autonomy brings more value but also more need for guardrails and oversight.
- Choosing the right point on the spectrum is a product decision, not a default.
