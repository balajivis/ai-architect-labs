# AI UX Patterns and Flows

(Merged: "AI UX Patterns" + "AI UX Flows" — near-duplicate topics combined.) AI-powered products introduce new interaction patterns — chat, autocomplete, suggestions, agents — and new end-to-end flows where the system may be uncertain or wrong. Designing these well means setting expectations, handling errors gracefully, and keeping users in control.

## Key ideas
- Common AI UX patterns include conversational interfaces, inline suggestions, and human-in-the-loop review.
- Flows should account for uncertainty: confidence cues, easy correction, and clear undo.
- Transparency matters — users should understand when AI is involved and how to override it.
- Latency, streaming responses, and graceful failure shape how trustworthy an AI feature feels.
