# Citations and Grounding

Grounding means an answer is backed by retrieved evidence rather than the model's imagination; citations expose *which* sources support each claim. Together they make RAG answers trustworthy and verifiable.

## Key ideas
- Grounding ties generated claims to retrieved source material.
- Citations let users check where an answer came from.
- Reduces hallucination and builds user trust.
- Requires tracking which passages informed which parts of the answer.
- Strong grounding is essential for high-stakes or regulated domains.
