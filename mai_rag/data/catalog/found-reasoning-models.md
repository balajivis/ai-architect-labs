# Reasoning Models

Reasoning models are designed to work through multi-step problems more deliberately before answering, rather than responding in a single quick pass. They tend to do better on math, logic, planning, and complex coding tasks where intermediate steps matter.

## Key ideas
- They favor deliberate, multi-step problem solving over instant responses.
- They typically excel at math, logic, and structured planning tasks.
- The trade-off is usually higher latency and cost per answer.
- Not every task needs them; simple lookups are often better served by faster models.
- Choosing when to use them is a practical product and cost decision.
