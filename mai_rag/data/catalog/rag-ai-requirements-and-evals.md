# AI Requirements and Evals

Before measuring quality you must define it: AI requirements turn fuzzy goals into testable criteria, and evals are the repeatable tests that check whether a system meets them. Together they turn "seems fine" into evidence.

## Key ideas
- Requirements translate goals into concrete, testable expectations.
- Evals are repeatable tests run as the system changes.
- Catch regressions before they reach users.
- Cover correctness, safety, and quality, not just accuracy.
- A prerequisite for trustworthy, iterative AI development.

MAI emphasizes defining requirements and building evals across the Practitioner and AI Architect tracks, at an overview level.
