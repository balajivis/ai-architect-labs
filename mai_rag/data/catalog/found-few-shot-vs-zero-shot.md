# Few-shot vs Zero-shot

Zero-shot prompting asks a model to do a task with no examples, while few-shot prompting includes a handful of examples to guide it. Few-shot often improves consistency, especially for specific formats or nuanced tasks.

## Key ideas
- Zero-shot relies on instructions alone.
- Few-shot adds examples to steer behavior.
- Examples help lock in format and style.
- Few-shot costs more tokens than zero-shot.
- The right choice depends on task difficulty and consistency needs.
