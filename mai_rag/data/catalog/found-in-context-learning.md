# In-Context Learning

In-context learning is a model's ability to pick up a new task from examples or instructions placed directly in the prompt, without changing its weights. It is why you can teach a model a pattern just by showing a few examples.

## Key ideas
- The model adapts from the prompt, not from retraining.
- Examples in the prompt can steer the task and format.
- It is fast and flexible, requiring no training run.
- It is the basis of few-shot prompting.
- Effectiveness depends on clear, relevant examples.
