# LLM vs SLM

Large Language Models (LLMs) are big, broadly capable models, while Small Language Models (SLMs) are compact models tuned for efficiency and narrower use. The choice between them is a practical trade-off between capability, cost, and where the model can run.

## Key ideas
- LLMs offer broad capability; SLMs offer speed, lower cost, and smaller footprint.
- SLMs can run on more constrained or local/edge hardware.
- Many real systems mix both, routing easy tasks to smaller models.
- Bigger is not always better for a given, well-scoped task.
- Privacy and offline needs often push toward smaller, local models.
