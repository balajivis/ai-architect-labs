# Foundation Models

Foundation models are large AI models trained on broad, diverse data so they can be adapted to many downstream tasks rather than a single narrow one. They form the base layer that most modern AI products build on, including chat assistants, coding tools, and search.

## Key ideas
- A single general-purpose model can be reused across many tasks via prompting or light adaptation.
- They are trained once at large scale, then specialized for specific uses.
- "Foundation" captures their role as shared infrastructure for downstream applications.
- They span text, images, audio, and code, not just language.
- Their broad capability is also why behavior must be evaluated and guarded in production.
