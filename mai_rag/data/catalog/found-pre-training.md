# Pre-Training

Pre-training is the initial, large-scale phase where a model learns general language patterns from vast text before any task-specific tuning. It is the expensive foundation step that produces a broadly capable base model.

## Key ideas
- It builds general capability from large, diverse data.
- It is the most compute-intensive phase of model building.
- It produces a base model that later steps refine.
- It happens before instruction tuning and alignment.
- Most organizations adapt pre-trained models rather than build new ones.
