## Scope — this plan tier (Silver)

This document defines the service-level commitments that Northwind Technologies extends to customers enrolled in the Silver support plan. It is the authoritative reference for the response, coverage, restoration, channel, and availability commitments that apply under this plan. The commitments described here apply only to active subscriptions in good standing and supersede any verbal representation made during the sales process.

The terms below are written to mirror the structure of Northwind's other support plans so that customers comparing tiers can read the same sections in the same order. Because the structure is shared, the meaningful differences between plans live in the specific numeric values stated in each section rather than in the surrounding prose. Customers should read the numbers carefully. Where a value is stated, it governs; where this document is silent, the Northwind Master Services Agreement governs.

Eligibility for the commitments in this plan begins on the subscription start date and continues for the duration of the paid term. Renewal does not reset accrued history. Northwind reserves the right to revise this published plan with thirty days' notice, with revisions taking effect at the next renewal.

## When you will first hear back

A first reply is the first substantive human message from a Northwind support engineer that acknowledges the reported issue, confirms its recorded severity, and states the next action. Automated ticket-receipt confirmations do not count. The clock for this commitment starts when a properly classified ticket is submitted through an approved channel and stops when the first substantive reply is sent.

For the most severe class of incident — a complete outage or a critical loss of function affecting production with no reasonable workaround, recorded at the top severity rung — the engineer's first substantive reply is targeted within **4 business hours**. That figure is measured against the window described in the next section; time accrued outside that window does not count toward it. The three lower-severity rungs carry progressively longer reply targets, which are maintained in the customer portal and are not restated here.

Severity is assigned by Northwind at intake based on the reported business impact. Customers may request a review of that assignment, and Northwind will adjust the classification if the evidence supports it. The figure above is a target for the first reply only, not a guarantee of resolution; the time to return service is addressed separately in the section below.

## The hours during which we work your tickets

Service under this plan is delivered during extended weekday hours: **twelve hours a day, on the five days from Monday through Friday**, excluding Northwind-observed holidays — i.e., sixty serviced hours across each ordinary week. Each serviced day runs from **7:00 a.m. to 7:00 p.m.** in the customer's designated primary support time zone, which is recorded at onboarding and may be changed once per contract year.

Every clock in this document — both the time-to-first-reply and the time-to-return-service — accrues only inside these serviced hours. An incident reported one hour before a day's hours close will have the remainder of its first-reply allowance carried into the following business day. Saturdays, Sundays, and holidays fall outside this plan's serviced hours and do not accrue against any target. Customers needing attention outside these hours should consult the higher published plan, which serves a broader span.

## When service will be brought back

To restore is to return the affected production functionality to normal operation, whether through a permanent fix or a stable workaround that removes the critical business impact. This is distinct from the first reply; meeting the first-reply target does not by itself satisfy the objective described here.

For the top-severity class of incident, the targeted time to return production service to normal under this plan is **2 business days**, measured inside the serviced hours. Northwind commits commercially reasonable, continuous effort during those hours to meet this objective. Where a root-cause fix requires a scheduled release, Northwind will provide and maintain an interim workaround so that the production impact is removed within this window even if the permanent correction follows later.

The equivalent objectives for lower severities are longer and are published in the portal. They assume the customer provides timely access, diagnostic information, and a reachable technical contact; delays attributable to the customer pause the clock described here.

## How to reach us, and who may do so

Customers on this plan may open and manage support requests through **three** routes: by email, through the customer support portal, and by telephone. The portal is the system of record for every ticket regardless of where it originates; items opened by email or phone are mirrored into it so that history, severity, and status remain consistent in one place. The telephone route is staffed for live triage during the serviced hours and is recommended for the most severe incidents to ensure prompt classification.

This plan authorizes up to **5** designated individuals on the customer's side who are entitled to open tickets, request severity reviews, and receive SLA communications. They are recorded at onboarding and may be updated through the portal. Requests originating from people outside that designated set may be accepted at Northwind's discretion but are not covered by the commitments in this document.

## When availability shortfalls earn you something back

Northwind measures monthly availability for the production service and reports it through the portal. Under this plan, a customer becomes eligible for a service credit when measured monthly availability falls **below 99.7%**. Availability is calculated across the calendar month, excluding scheduled maintenance announced in advance and excluding interruptions attributable to customer systems, third-party networks, or force-majeure events.

To claim this remedy, an eligible customer must submit a request through the portal within thirty days of the close of the affected month, citing the relevant availability report. Approved amounts are applied against a future invoice and are the sole and exclusive remedy for availability shortfalls under this plan. They do not accrue cash value and are capped per the Master Services Agreement. Repeated months below the eligibility threshold may, at the customer's option, trigger the contractual review process described in the MSA.
