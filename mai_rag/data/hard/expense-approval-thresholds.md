# Northwind Expense Approval Authority Matrix

This matrix defines the spending-authority bands that govern who may approve a given expense at Northwind Technologies. It is the authoritative source for approval routing in the expense system and supersedes any conflicting guidance circulated by individual departments. Finance owns this document and reviews it quarterly.

## Scope — all employee and contractor expenses

This policy applies to **every** expense incurred on behalf of Northwind Technologies, regardless of who incurs it or how it is paid. That includes:

- Full-time and part-time employees in every department.
- Contractors, temporary staff, and agency personnel operating under a Northwind statement of work.
- Corporate-card purchases, out-of-pocket reimbursements, and direct vendor invoices.
- Recurring subscriptions and one-time purchases alike.

The bands below are evaluated against a **single expense** — one transaction, one invoice line, or one reimbursement request. Deliberately splitting a purchase into smaller transactions to stay under a band ("structuring") is a policy violation and will be routed to Finance for review regardless of the individual amounts. When in doubt about whether two charges count as one expense, treat the aggregate as the expense and apply the band for the combined amount.

## Approval Bands by Amount

All figures are in **USD** and refer to the total value of a single expense. The approver listed is the **minimum** authority required; higher authorities may always approve a lower-band expense.

| Band | Single-expense amount (USD) | Required approver |
|------|------------------------------|-------------------|
| 1 | $0 – $999 | Self-approve |
| 2 | $1,000 – $4,999 | Manager |
| 3 | $5,000 – $24,999 | Director |
| 4 | $25,000 – $99,999 | VP |
| 5 | $100,000 and above | CFO + board notification |

A few points that are routinely misread:

- **$5,000 is a Director-band expense, not a Manager-band expense.** The Manager band ends at $4,999. The moment an expense reaches $5,000 it crosses into the Director band. A $4,999 dinner is a Manager approval; a $5,000 dinner is a Director approval. There is no rounding and no grace zone between the bands.
- **$1,000 is the first dollar that leaves self-approve.** Anything at $999 or below is self-approved; $1,000 exactly requires Manager sign-off.
- **$25,000 is a VP expense, not a Director expense.** The Director band ends at $24,999, so a $24,999 expense is a Director approval and a $25,000 expense is a VP approval.
- **$100,000 and above requires CFO approval and a formal notification to the board.** The board notification is in addition to, not a substitute for, CFO approval.

Self-approval still requires a valid business purpose and an accurate expense category. Self-approve does not mean unreviewed — Finance audits a sample of self-approved expenses every month.

## Out-of-Policy Exceptions

Some expenses require elevated sign-off **regardless of amount**. The most common is travel that falls outside the Northwind Travel Policy — for example, business-class airfare on a route under the policy threshold, a hotel above the nightly cap, or a non-preferred airline booked without justification.

**Out-of-policy travel requires Director sign-off regardless of the dollar amount.** A $300 out-of-policy hotel upgrade still needs a Director, even though $300 would normally be self-approved. The out-of-policy rule overrides the amount band upward; it never lowers the required authority. If an expense is both out-of-policy *and* in a band above Director (for example a $40,000 out-of-policy event, which falls in the VP band), the higher band authority applies — you cannot satisfy a VP-band expense with only a Director's signature.

## Receipt Requirements ($2,500 itemized)

Documentation requirements are tied to payment method and amount:

- **Corporate-card transactions of $2,500 or more require itemized receipts.** A summary charge slip or a card statement line is not sufficient — the receipt must show line items, the merchant, the date, and applicable tax. A $2,500 corporate-card charge exactly is at the boundary and requires the itemized receipt; a $2,499 charge does not.
- Out-of-pocket reimbursement requests require an itemized receipt for any amount being claimed.
- Where an itemized receipt is genuinely unavailable, the employee must file a missing-receipt affidavit, which is reviewed by the same approver in the relevant band.

Receipts must be attached at submission. Expenses submitted without required documentation are returned and do not start the reimbursement clock until complete.

## Reimbursement Timing (10 business days)

Approved reimbursements are **processed within 10 business days of approval.** The clock starts at the moment the final required approver signs off — not at submission, and not when the expense was incurred. If an expense is returned for missing documentation, the 10-business-day window restarts from the date of the eventual approval.

"Processed" means the payment is released to payroll or accounts payable for disbursement; the actual deposit date may vary by a day or two depending on the employee's bank. Reimbursements that exceed 10 business days without disbursement should be escalated to the Finance shared inbox.

## Cross-Reference: Vendor Onboarding

Approval authority is necessary but not always sufficient. Some expenses trigger additional review under other policies, and approvers must check both.

Per the Vendor Onboarding Policy, **any new vendor expected to exceed $25,000 in annual spend requires a Security review** before the first purchase order is issued. This is a separate gate from the approval band.

Worked example — combine the band rule and the vendor rule:

> A team wants to engage a brand-new SaaS vendor for a single $30,000 purchase that is also the start of an ongoing $30,000/year relationship.
>
> - **Band rule:** $30,000 falls in the **VP** band ($25,000–$99,999), so VP approval is required.
> - **Vendor rule:** because the new vendor's expected annual spend exceeds $25,000, a **Security review** is also required before the PO is issued.
>
> Both must be satisfied. VP approval alone does not clear the Security gate, and a completed Security review does not substitute for the required VP approval.

When the two rules disagree about timing, the Security review must complete **before** the expense is approved, because Security findings can block or reshape the purchase. Approvers should confirm the vendor's status in the vendor registry before signing off on any first-time engagement near or above the $25,000 annual-spend line.
