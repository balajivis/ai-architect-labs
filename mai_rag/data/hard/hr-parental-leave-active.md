# Northwind Parental Leave Policy

Northwind Technologies recognizes that welcoming a new child is one of the most significant events in an employee's life. This policy describes the paid parental leave available to eligible employees, the conditions that govern its use, and the process for requesting it. People Operations maintains this policy and reviews it annually to ensure it remains competitive and compliant with applicable regulations across the jurisdictions in which Northwind operates. This version takes effect on 1 May 2026 and supersedes the prior policy; the entitlements stated below are the current entitlements as of that effective date.

## Scope — all benefits-eligible Northwind employees

This policy applies to all benefits-eligible Northwind employees, including full-time and qualifying part-time staff across every department — engineering, customer success, finance, IT security, data privacy, and the broader business organization. Contractors, interns, and temporary staff engaged through agencies are not covered by this policy and should refer to the terms of their individual engagement agreements. Where a local statutory entitlement is more generous than the provisions described here, the statutory entitlement applies and Northwind's policy supplements it rather than reducing it.

Parental leave under this policy may be used following the birth of an employee's child, the placement of a child with the employee for adoption, or the placement of a child with the employee for foster care that is intended to lead to adoption. The policy applies to births and adoptions equally; there is no distinction in entitlement, pay, or scheduling between a child who joins the family by birth and a child who joins by adoption.

## Primary Caregiver Entitlement (18 weeks)

The primary caregiver is entitled to 18 weeks of paid leave at 100% of base pay. The primary caregiver is the parent who has primary responsibility for the day-to-day care of the child during the leave period. In most cases this is self-declared at the time of the request; where two Northwind employees are welcoming the same child, only one may be designated the primary caregiver, and the designation cannot be split or transferred mid-leave.

Because the entitlement is paid at 100% of base pay, no salary top-up arrangement is required. Employees continue to accrue standard benefits during the leave, and their position is protected for the duration. Bonuses, equity vesting, and other compensation elements continue to be treated as if the employee were actively at work, subject to the terms of the relevant plan documents. The 18-week primary-caregiver entitlement applies to births and adoptions occurring on or after the 1 May 2026 effective date of this policy; for qualifying events before that date, the superseded policy's entitlement applies.

## Secondary Caregiver Entitlement (8 weeks)

The secondary caregiver is entitled to 8 weeks of paid leave at 100% of base pay. The secondary caregiver is the parent who is not the designated primary caregiver. As with the primary caregiver entitlement, the secondary caregiver leave is paid at 100% of base pay, so no top-up is necessary, and benefits continue to accrue throughout the leave period.

Where both parents are Northwind employees, one may take the primary caregiver entitlement of 18 weeks and the other may take the secondary caregiver entitlement of 8 weeks for the same child. The two entitlements are independent and may overlap, run consecutively, or be separated, subject to the scheduling rules below.

## Eligibility (90 days continuous)

To be eligible for paid parental leave, an employee must have completed 90 days of continuous employment with Northwind as of the date the leave is scheduled to begin. Continuous employment means an unbroken period of active service; approved short absences such as standard PTO do not reset the count. Employees who have not yet reached the 90-day threshold at the start of a planned leave should contact People Operations to discuss unpaid options and timing.

Eligibility is determined once per qualifying event. An employee who has met the 90-day requirement for one child does not need to re-establish eligibility for a subsequent qualifying event, provided employment has remained continuous.

## Scheduling and Blocks (within 12 months, up to 3 blocks)

Leave must be taken within 12 months of the date of birth or the date of placement for adoption or foster-to-adopt. Any portion of the entitlement not used within that 12-month window is forfeited and does not carry over or convert to pay.

To support flexibility, the entitlement may be taken in up to 3 separate blocks rather than as a single continuous absence. For example, a primary caregiver might take an initial block immediately following the birth, return to work, and take the remaining weeks in one or two later blocks within the 12-month window. Each block should be requested and approved in advance so that teams can plan coverage. The minimum length of a single block is one full week.

## Interaction with PTO Accrual (see hr-pto-accrual-active)

Paid parental leave is a distinct entitlement and is separate from an employee's paid time off. Employees are not required to exhaust PTO before or after taking parental leave. PTO continues to accrue during parental leave according to the employee's tenure band. The tenure bands that govern accrual rates, and how a leave period interplays with accrual caps and carryover, are defined in the PTO accrual policy (see hr-pto-accrual-active). Refer to that document for the authoritative accrual schedule; this policy does not restate accrual rates to avoid divergence between the two documents.

## Requesting Leave

To request parental leave, submit a request through the People Operations portal at least 30 days before the intended start date where the timing is foreseeable. For unforeseeable events, notify People Operations as soon as practicable. The request should indicate the expected date of birth or placement, the caregiver designation (primary or secondary), and the proposed block structure. People Operations will confirm eligibility, document the approved blocks, and coordinate with the employee's manager on coverage. Questions about this policy should be directed to People Operations.
