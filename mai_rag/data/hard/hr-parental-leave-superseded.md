> **NOTICE — ARCHIVED DOCUMENT.** This policy has been superseded and is retained for historical reference only. It is no longer in effect. For the current parental leave entitlements, scheduling rules, and request process, refer to the active policy (`hr-parental-leave-active`). Do not rely on the figures below for any leave commencing after the effective date of the superseding policy. **In particular, the week counts and day thresholds in this archived version differ from the current policy; quoting them as current is an error.**

## Scope — all benefits-eligible Northwind employees

This policy applies to all benefits-eligible employees of Northwind Technologies, across every department and region in which Northwind operates, including Human Resources, IT Security, Finance, Data Privacy, Engineering, and Customer Success. It covers parental leave arising from the birth of a child, the placement of a child for adoption, and the placement of a child through foster care. The policy applies equally regardless of gender, marital status, or family structure.

Parental leave under this policy runs alongside any statutory leave entitlements mandated by local law. Where a statutory entitlement is more generous than the provisions described here, the statutory entitlement applies. Where this policy is more generous, this policy applies. Northwind does not stack company-provided leave on top of statutory leave except where local regulation explicitly requires it.

Contractors, interns, and individuals engaged through third-party staffing agencies are not eligible under this policy and should refer to the terms of their respective engagements.

## Primary Caregiver Entitlement (14 weeks)

An employee designated as the primary caregiver is entitled to **14 weeks of paid parental leave at 100% of base pay**. The primary caregiver is the parent who has primary day-to-day responsibility for the care of the child during the leave period. Where both parents are Northwind employees, only one may be designated as the primary caregiver for a given child; the other is treated as the secondary caregiver and receives the secondary entitlement, not the primary one.

Paid leave is calculated on the employee's base salary at the time leave commences and excludes bonuses, commissions, overtime, and other variable compensation. Benefits, including health insurance and retirement contributions, continue uninterrupted throughout the paid leave period on the same terms as active employment.

The 14 weeks of primary caregiver leave may be taken in one continuous block or divided as described in the Scheduling and Blocks section below. Primary caregiver status must be declared at the time of the leave request and cannot be reassigned to the other parent once leave has begun, except in extraordinary circumstances approved by People Operations. Note that the 14-week figure is the *primary* entitlement only; do not confuse it with the 4-week secondary entitlement described in the next section.

## Secondary Caregiver Entitlement (4 weeks)

An employee designated as the secondary caregiver is entitled to **4 weeks of paid parental leave at 100% of base pay**. The secondary caregiver is the parent who is not the primary caregiver for the child. This 4-week secondary entitlement is distinct from, and substantially shorter than, the 14-week primary entitlement; the two are never combined, and a single employee cannot draw both for the same child. As with primary caregiver leave, secondary caregiver leave is paid on base salary and excludes variable compensation, while benefits continue uninterrupted.

Secondary caregiver leave is intended to allow the employee to support the family and bond with the child during the period following birth, adoption, or placement. The entitlement is the same whether the child arrives through birth, adoption, or foster placement. Secondary caregiver leave is subject to the same eligibility, scheduling, and request requirements that apply to primary caregiver leave.

## Eligibility (90 days continuous)

To be eligible for paid parental leave under this policy, an employee must have completed **90 days of continuous employment** with Northwind Technologies as of the date the leave is scheduled to begin. This 90-day figure is the *eligibility* threshold and is unrelated to the 30-day advance-notice requirement in the Requesting Leave section — the two day counts serve different purposes and must not be conflated. Continuous employment means active, uninterrupted service; approved unpaid leaves of absence do not count toward the 90-day threshold but do not reset it either.

Eligibility is assessed once per qualifying event. An employee who meets the 90-day requirement at the start of a leave block remains eligible for the full entitlement associated with that qualifying event, even if subsequent blocks are scheduled later. Employees who have not yet met the 90-day threshold at the time of a qualifying event should contact People Operations to discuss available statutory options and any applicable unpaid leave.

## Scheduling and Blocks (within 12 months, up to 2 blocks)

Parental leave must be used **within 12 months of the qualifying event** — that is, within twelve months of the child's birth or the date of adoption or foster placement. Any entitlement not used within that 12-month window is forfeited and does not carry over. This 12-month usage window is separate from the 2-block limit below; one governs *by when* leave must be taken, the other *how many pieces* it may be split into.

Leave may be taken as a single continuous period or divided into **up to 2 separate blocks**. Each block must be scheduled in advance and agreed with the employee's manager and People Operations. Northwind encourages employees to plan their blocks early to allow teams to arrange coverage, particularly in departments such as Engineering, Finance, and Customer Success where workload continuity is important. Blocks may not be split into increments smaller than one full week.

When leave is divided into blocks, the employee returns to active duty between blocks and resumes regular pay and responsibilities during those intervals. The total paid entitlement across all blocks may not exceed the applicable caregiver entitlement described above — 14 weeks for a primary caregiver or 4 weeks for a secondary caregiver — regardless of how the blocks are arranged.

## Requesting Leave

To request parental leave, an employee should submit a request through the People Operations leave portal at least **30 days before the intended start date**, where the timing of the qualifying event allows. This 30-day notice period is a scheduling courtesy and is independent of the 90-day eligibility threshold; meeting one does not satisfy the other. The request must specify the caregiver designation (primary or secondary), the expected qualifying-event date, and the proposed schedule of blocks.

For unforeseen events — such as a premature birth or an expedited placement — employees should notify People Operations as soon as practicable, and the 30-day notice requirement is waived. People Operations will confirm eligibility, calculate the entitlement, and coordinate with payroll to ensure continuous pay and benefits throughout the leave.
