# Health & Safety Policy

## 1. Purpose & Scope

Northwind Technologies commits to providing a safe, healthy, and compliant work environment for all employees, contractors, and visitors. This policy meets OSHA federal standards, Texas state regulations, and company values. It covers physical workplace hazards, ergonomics, mental health, substance abuse, and emergency preparedness.

**Scope**: All Northwind facilities (Austin HQ, satellite offices), employees, contractors, and temporary workers.

## 2. Core Principles

1. **Zero-harm goal**: No workplace injuries or illnesses are acceptable; continuous improvement in safety culture
2. **Legal compliance**: Full adherence to OSHA, Texas Occupational Safety & Health Administration (TOSHA), ADA, and any local ordinances
3. **Shared responsibility**: Management provides infrastructure and training; employees practice safe behaviors; contractors sign safety waivers
4. **Transparency**: All incidents reported (see Section 5); no retaliation for safety-related concerns or reports
5. **Data-driven**: Safety metrics (lost-time injuries, near-misses, hazards resolved) reviewed quarterly with VP People and Board

## 3. Workplace Hazards & Risk Management

| Hazard Category | Northwind Context | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Ergonomic (Office)** | Prolonged computer work; poor posture | Standing desk option; monitor risers; annual ergonomic assessments (optional) |
| **Environmental** | Electrical equipment, HVAC, lighting | Regular maintenance by Facilities; temperature 68–74°F monitored; backup power (see Emergency Response Plan) |
| **Chemical** | Cleaning supplies, HVAC refrigerants, occasional lab work | MSDS sheets posted; storage in locked cabinets; employees trained per OSHA Hazard Communication standard |
| **Biological** | Pandemic risk, infectious illness | Paid sick leave; remote-work accommodation; vaccination policy (see below) |
| **Psychological** | Work stress, long hours, remote isolation | EAP (Employee Assistance Program) available 24/7; mental health benefits; flexible work options per Leave & Time-Off Policy |

## 4. Vaccination & Communicable Illness Policy

### 4.1 COVID-19 & Influenza
- **Vaccination**: Strongly encouraged; not mandatory (unless required by customer contracts or government mandate)
- **Disclosure**: Employees voluntarily report vaccination status to HR; used for pandemic response planning only; no discrimination based on status
- **Illness**: Employees experiencing symptoms (fever, cough, shortness of breath) must:
  1. Notify manager immediately
  2. Work remotely if possible; if on-site, use provided rapid test
  3. If positive or symptomatic, remain home until 24 hours fever-free without medication
  4. Do not come to office; use PTO or unpaid leave as needed (no penalty)

### 4.2 General Communicable Illness
- **Exclusion**: Employees with active contagious illness (measles, strep, norovirus) must stay home
- **Paid sick leave**: Accrues per Leave & Time-Off Policy; no questions asked for health absence

## 5. Incident Reporting & Investigation

### 5.1 What Constitutes a Reportable Incident
- **Injury**: Cut, burn, fracture, strain, or any pain requiring medical attention
- **Near-miss**: Incident that did not result in injury but had potential (e.g., trip hazard, electrical spark)
- **Illness**: Work-related onset (e.g., occupational asthma, repetitive strain)
- **Environmental hazard**: Spill, electrical issue, equipment malfunction, air quality concern

### 5.2 Reporting Procedure
1. **Immediate**: Employee or witness notifies manager or Facilities within 4 hours
2. **First aid**: If needed, employee or colleague provides care; serious injury requires 911 call
3. **Incident form**: Employee completes form (Google Form or paper); Facilities scans into incident log
4. **Investigation**: Facilities & Safety Committee investigates within 5 business days; interview employee, identify root cause
5. **Corrective action**: Document preventive measures (e.g., repair, training, procedure change)
6. **Follow-up**: Within 30 days, Facilities confirms corrective measures implemented and effective

### 5.3 OSHA Reporting Obligations
- **Work-related injury or illness** resulting in lost time (>3 consecutive days) or requiring restricted duty **must be reported to OSHA** within 24 hours
- **Responsibility**: HR (with Facilities confirmation) submits OSHA Form 301 (Injury/Illness Log)
- **Post-incident**: General Counsel reviews if litigation risk apparent; may engage workers' compensation insurance

### 5.4 No Retaliation
Employees who report safety concerns, hazards, or incidents are protected from retaliation. Violation of this protection may result in disciplinary action up to termination.

## 6. Training & Competency

### 6.1 Mandatory Training
- **All employees**: Safety orientation (1 hour, part of Onboarding Day 1); annual refresher
- **Content**: Hazard recognition, emergency procedures, incident reporting, ergonomics, communicable illness protocols
- **Certification**: Tracked in HR system; lapsed training triggers mandatory re-completion

### 6.2 Role-Specific Training
- **Facilities staff**: OSHA 10-hour card; quarterly toolbox talks on hazard awareness
- **First Aiders**: CPR / First Aid certification (American Red Cross or equivalent); renewed annually
- **Lab/engineering personnel** (if applicable): Chemical safety, electrical safety, equipment operation

### 6.3 Ergonomic Assessment
- **Available to**: Any employee requesting assessment (free; no cost to employee)
- **Scope**: Workstation review, monitor/keyboard positioning, chair adjustment, recommendations for standing desk or monitor arm
- **Schedule**: Conducted by Facilities or external consultant; follow-up in 30 days

## 7. Mental Health & Psychological Safety

### 7.1 Employee Assistance Program (EAP)
- **Benefit**: Confidential counseling, 4 free sessions/year; covered 100% by company
- **Availability**: 24/7 phone line and online portal; mental health professionals (therapists, counselors)
- **Promotion**: Advertised during Onboarding, quarterly via email, and in break-room posters
- **Privacy**: EAP-disclosed information not shared with employer; does not impact employment

### 7.2 Stress & Burnout Mitigation
- **Flexible work**: Remote work options per Remote Access/VPN Guide; manager may authorize 4-day work week during crunch periods
- **Workload management**: Managers responsible for preventing chronic overtime; escalate to VP People if team consistently exceeds 50 hours/week
- **Peer support**: "Safety champion" nominated per department; trained to recognize and respond to peer distress

## 8. Safety Committee & Continuous Improvement

### 8.1 Safety Committee
- **Members**: Facilities Director (chair), VP People, IT Security, 1–2 employee representatives (rotated annually)
- **Frequency**: Monthly meeting; review incident trends, hazards, training updates
- **Authority**: Recommends policy changes and corrective actions to VP People

### 8.2 Safety Metrics & Reporting
- **Monthly**: Incident count, near-miss count, hazards opened/closed, training completion rate
- **Quarterly**: Trend analysis; Board briefing if serious incident occurred
- **Annual**: Comprehensive safety audit; updated risk register

**Target**: Zero lost-time injuries annually; achieve 10% reduction in near-misses year-over-year.

## 9. Contractor & Visitor Safety Responsibilities

- **Contractors**: Must sign safety acknowledgment; orientation required before starting work
- **Visitors**: Escorted per Office Access & Visitor Policy; briefed on emergency exits and hazards in visited areas
- **Hazard communication**: Contractors informed of site-specific hazards; Northwind responsible for their safe work conduct while on premises

## 10. Incident Examples & Response

**Example 1: Paper cut during document filing**
- Report via form; minor, no lost time
- Root cause: Sharp file cabinet edge
- Corrective action: Add edge guards to cabinets
- OSHA report: Not required (no lost time)

**Example 2: Employee slips on wet floor near break-room**
- Report within 4 hours; sprains ankle; misses 5 days (lost time >3 days)
- Root cause: Water spill; no "wet floor" sign posted
- Corrective action: Install permanent drain mat; post signs; train Facilities on immediate spill response
- OSHA report: Required within 24 hours
