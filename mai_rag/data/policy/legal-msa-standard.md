# Master Services Agreement (MSA) Standard

## Purpose
This document defines the standard terms and conditions for all Master Services Agreements (MSAs) executed by Northwind Technologies with vendors, customers, and partners. All MSAs must conform to this standard unless Board-approved exceptions are granted through the **Contract Approval Policy**.

## 1. Core Contract Elements

All Northwind MSAs must include:

1. **Parties & Effective Date** — Legal entity names, title, and commencement date
2. **Services & Scope** — Clear statement of deliverables, SOW incorporation, and change order process
3. **Term & Termination** — Initial term (typically 1–3 years), auto-renewal unless 90-day notice given, and termination for cause/convenience clauses
4. **Fees & Payment** — Pricing schedule, invoicing frequency (monthly standard), payment terms (Net 30 default), and price escalation clauses capped at 5% annually
5. **Confidentiality** — Incorporation of **NDA Policy** terms; mutual non-disclosure obligations; exceptions for legally compelled disclosure
6. **Intellectual Property** — Northwind retains all IP in deliverables and improvements; vendor retains IP in pre-existing materials; clear work-for-hire language
7. **Data Protection & DPA** — If vendor will access Confidential or Restricted data, a Data Processing Agreement (DPA per **Data Classification & Retention Policy**) is attached as Exhibit A
8. **Warranties & Disclaimers** — Vendor warrants it has authority to execute; services will be provided professionally; products free of malware and compliance with applicable law
9. **Limitation of Liability** — Capped at the greater of (a) fees paid in the prior 12 months or (b) $100,000, except for indemnification and data breaches (uncapped)
10. **Indemnification** — Vendor indemnifies Northwind for IP infringement claims; Northwind indemnifies vendor for Northwind's IP infringement claims in vendor deliverables
11. **Insurance & Risk** — Vendor maintains general liability ($1M minimum) and cyber insurance ($2M minimum for data processors); proof of coverage on demand
12. **Dispute Resolution** — Governing law: **State of Texas**. Escalation: negotiation (30 days) → mediation (30 days) → **binding arbitration in Austin, TX** per AAA Commercial Arbitration Rules. No class actions.
13. **Compliance & Regulations** — Vendor warrants compliance with applicable laws; Northwind may audit for export control, sanctions, and data handling (see **Anti-Bribery & Corruption Policy** for OFAC screening)
14. **Compliance with Acceptable Use** — If vendor manages Northwind user accounts or systems, vendor agrees to **Acceptable Use Policy** terms (no unauthorized access, malware, harassment, etc.)

## 2. Standard Liability & Indemnification Table

| Scenario | Capped Liability | Indemnification | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Service failure, uptime SLA breach | 12-month fees or $100K, whichever greater | Not applicable | Failure limited to service credits |
| Data breach or unauthorized access | **Uncapped** | Vendor indemnifies | Data handling triggers heightened coverage |
| IP infringement (vendor code) | **Uncapped** | Vendor indemnifies | Vendor assumes full defense |
| Northwind use of vendor deliverable | Capped at 12-month fees | Northwind indemnifies | Vendor retains pre-existing IP |
| Third-party claim vs. vendor | Capped at 12-month fees | N/A (vendor's insurance covers) | Vendor maintains insurance per §7 |

## 3. Confidentiality & IP Alignment

- All services delivered under this MSA are considered "Northwind Confidential" per the **NDA Policy** unless otherwise marked
- Vendor may not disclose Northwind data, customer lists, or architectural details without written consent
- Northwind retains ownership of all derivative works, customizations, and improvements to vendor services created during the engagement
- Vendor may use general learnings and methodologies (not Northwind-specific data) for future clients after engagement ends

## 4. Data Protection & DPA Trigger

If vendor will access, process, or store data classified as **Confidential** or **Restricted** under the **Data Classification & Retention Policy**, a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is required as Exhibit A. DPA terms include:
- Sub-processor notification and consent
- Data subject rights (access, correction, deletion)
- Security standards: encryption at rest and in transit, 90-day breach notification
- Audit rights and compliance attestations
- Immediate data return or deletion upon termination

## 5. Termination & Transition

- **Termination for Convenience** — Either party may terminate with 90 days' notice; vendor must return all Northwind Confidential data and systems access within 15 days
- **Termination for Cause** — Immediate termination if vendor materially breaches and fails to cure within 30 days, or if vendor becomes insolvent
- **Transition Assistance** — Vendor must cooperate in knowledge transfer and data migration at no additional cost for 30 days post-termination

## 6. Amendment & Escalation Process

Minor amendments (pricing within ±3%, scope clarifications) may be approved by VP Finance + General Counsel. Major amendments (new scope, material liability changes, new data access) require the same approval level as the original MSA (see **Contract Approval Policy**).
