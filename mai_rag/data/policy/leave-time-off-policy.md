# Leave & Time-Off Policy

## 1. Paid Time Off (PTO)

All full-time employees are entitled to paid time off. PTO can be used for vacation, sick leave, personal days, or other reasons.

### 1.1 Accrual Schedule

Employees accrue PTO at **1.5 days per month** (18 days per year).

- **New employees**: PTO accrues starting on Day 1 of employment
- **Accrual is pro-rated**: If you start mid-month, you accrue 0.75 days for that month
- **Accrual cliff**: PTO does NOT carry over between calendar years. Any unused balance is forfeited on December 31
- **Maximum accrual cap**: You may not carry over more than 5 days into the next calendar year. If you exceed 5 days at year-end, the excess is forfeited
- **Part-time employees (< 40 hours/week)**: Accrue PTO pro-rated based on hours worked

### 1.2 PTO Approval and Request Process

1. **Request timing**: Submit PTO requests at least 14 days in advance using Workday (HR system)
2. **Manager approval**: Your manager must approve the request within 3 business days
3. **Coverage plan**: For engineering roles with on-call duties (see **On-Call & Escalation Policy**), ensure your on-call shift is covered or transferred
4. **Blackout dates**: Northwind observes two blackout periods where non-essential vacation is discouraged (though not prohibited):
   - **Q4 push**: December 1–15 (during year-end financial close and product launch)
   - **Customer conference**: June 10–12 (annual customer summit)
   
   Requests during blackout dates may be approved if you have manager and VP sign-off

5. **Emergency PTO**: If you need urgent PTO (illness, emergency), notify your manager verbally immediately and submit request in Workday within 24 hours

### 1.3 Sick Leave

- Unscheduled PTO due to illness or medical appointments is covered by your PTO accrual
- No additional "sick leave bucket"; sickness uses your regular PTO balance
- If you are ill and unable to work, notify your manager before your shift starts
- For absences longer than 3 consecutive days, provide a medical note

### 1.4 Bereavement Leave

Employees are entitled to up to 5 days of paid bereavement leave in the event of death of a close family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling). Additional days may be approved by HR on a case-by-case basis. Bereavement leave does NOT count against your PTO balance.

## 2. Paid Holidays

Northwind observes the following paid holidays (US employees; international locations follow local holiday calendars):

| Holiday | Date |
|---------|------|
| New Year's Day | January 1 |
| Martin Luther King Jr. Day | Third Monday of January |
| Presidents' Day | Third Monday of February |
| Memorial Day | Last Monday of May |
| Independence Day | July 4 |
| Labor Day | First Monday of September |
| Thanksgiving | Fourth Thursday of November + Friday after |
| Christmas | December 25 |

**Floating holidays**: Employees are entitled to 2 additional paid days to observe holidays not listed above (e.g., Diwali, Lunar New Year, Eid). Coordinate with manager and HR.

If a holiday falls on a weekend, the observed holiday is the nearest weekday (typically the preceding Friday or following Monday).

## 3. Parental Leave

### 3.1 Maternity/Paternity Leave

- **Primary caregiver** (birth parent or adopting parent): 12 weeks of paid parental leave
- **Secondary caregiver** (non-birth parent or non-adopting parent): 4 weeks of paid parental leave
- **Usage timing**: Leave must be taken within 12 months of birth or adoption
- **Job guarantee**: Your position (or equivalent) is held for you; you return to the same role
- **Health insurance**: Continues during parental leave; employee continues paying normal premium share

### 3.2 Parental Leave Request Process

1. Notify HR at least 30 days before your planned leave date
2. Submit completed parental leave form (HR provides template)
3. HR confirms benefits continuation and return-to-work date
4. Coordinate with manager and team on coverage during your absence

## 4. Unpaid Leave of Absence

Employees may request unpaid leave of absence for personal reasons (sabbatical, family care, health recovery, education). Requests are evaluated case-by-case by HR and require VP approval.

- **Maximum duration**: 12 weeks unpaid per 3-year period
- **Benefits**: Health insurance may be available at employee cost (via COBRA or similar)
- **Job guarantee**: Position held for up to 12 weeks; beyond that, return is not guaranteed
- **Request process**: Submit 60 days in advance to HR with detailed request

## 5. Military Leave

Employees called to active military duty are entitled to unpaid leave in accordance with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA). Job position is held upon return from military service.

## 6. Jury Duty and Civic Obligations

Employees summoned for jury duty are excused from work without loss of pay for the duration of the trial or jury summons. Northwind does not require employees to request exemption from jury duty.

If you receive a jury summons, notify HR immediately. Provide HR with a copy of the summons and attend all required court dates.

## 7. PTO Payout Upon Termination

### 7.1 Resignation
Unused PTO is paid out on your final paycheck. The payout amount is calculated based on your current rate of pay.

### 7.2 Termination for Cause
Unused PTO is forfeited (not paid out) if employment is terminated for cause (e.g., breach of Information Security Policy, theft, violence).

### 7.3 Layoff or Reduction in Force
Unused PTO is paid out on your final paycheck.

### 7.4 Retirement
Unused PTO is paid out on your final paycheck.

## 8. Blackout Period Exceptions

In rare cases, employees may work during blackout periods if:
- Critical incident or outage requires on-call engineer (see **Incident Response Runbook** and **On-Call & Escalation Policy**)
- Customer contract or service level agreement (SLA) requires support

These exceptions are logged and may affect future bonus calculations. Employees who work during blackout dates may request a compensatory day off in the following quarter.

## 9. PTO Reporting and Compliance

- HR audits PTO usage quarterly to ensure compliance with accrual schedules
- Managers receive monthly reports of their team's PTO usage
- Significant overages or underutilization may trigger a conversation with HR

## 10. International Employees

Employees in non-US locations (UK, Canada, EU, etc.) follow local statutory leave requirements, which may exceed the above policy. HR confirms the applicable policy at hire; statutory minimums always apply.
