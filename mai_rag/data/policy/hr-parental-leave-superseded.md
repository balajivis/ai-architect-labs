# Parental Leave Policy (Superseded — Effective June 2024 through April 2026)

**NOTICE: This policy is no longer active. See the current Parental Leave Policy (Active) for updated benefits. This document is archived for reference only.**

## 1. Purpose

Northwind Technologies provides paid parental leave to support employees during the birth or adoption of a child. This policy was effective from June 15, 2024 through April 30, 2026.

## 2. Paid Parental Leave

**2.1 Eligibility**:
- All full-time employees (≥30 hours/week) who have completed 6 months of service
- Applies to biological parent (birth), adoptive parent, and same-sex spouse/domestic partner

**2.2 Leave Duration**:
- **8 weeks paid parental leave** (320 hours)
- Leave must be taken within 12 months of birth or adoption
- Leave is at 100% pay (not reduced)
- Accrued PTO and benefits continue during leave

**2.3 Leave Usage**:
- Leave may be taken as a continuous block (8 weeks) or split (e.g., 4 weeks at birth, 4 weeks at 6 months)
- Splitting must be approved by manager and HR before the first leave period
- Leave is available for both parents if both are employed by Northwind (each receives 8 weeks)

**2.4 Job Guarantee**:
- Employee's job or equivalent position is guaranteed upon return
- Salary, benefits, and job title do not change
- Return to same department/team if possible

## 3. Integration with Other Leave

Parental leave is **separate from** and **in addition to**:
- Accrued PTO (vacation, sick leave)
- Short-term disability (for birth complications)
- Unpaid FMLA leave (federal requirement; see Leave & Time-Off Policy)

Employees may use accrued PTO before or after parental leave to extend time off.

## 4. Notification & Scheduling

**4.1** Employees should notify People Operations of expected birth/adoption date as early as possible (ideally 8+ weeks in advance).

**4.2** Parental leave must be requested in Workday at least 4 weeks before the expected start date (with manager approval).

**4.3** HR confirms leave dates, benefits continuation, and return date.

## 5. Benefits During Leave

- **Health insurance**: Company-paid portion continues at same rate (employee continues to pay their share)
- **401(k)**: Contributions pause during leave; employee may resume upon return
- **Accrual of benefits**: PTO continues to accrue (1.5 days/month) during leave
- **Seniority & tenure**: Continuous; leave time counts toward tenure for vesting and other purposes

## 6. Unpaid Leave Options

If employees need more than 8 weeks, options include:
- **Accrued PTO**: use unused vacation/sick time
- **FMLA leave**: up to 12 weeks unpaid federal leave (see Leave & Time-Off Policy)
- **Unpaid personal leave**: negotiate with manager + HR approval (job security not guaranteed)

## 7. Return to Work

- Return date is specified in the leave request
- Employee contacts manager ~2 weeks before return to confirm
- Onboarding for any team changes is minimal (employee returns to same role)
- Flexibility for gradual return (e.g., part-time return week 1–2) may be negotiated

## 8. Second Parental Leave

- Employees may take parental leave for multiple children (each time working for Northwind and eligible)
- Same terms apply (8 weeks paid per child)
