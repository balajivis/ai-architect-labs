# Office Access & Visitor Policy

## 1. Purpose & Scope

This policy governs physical access to Northwind Technologies facilities (primary: Austin, TX HQ; secondary: any leased satellite offices). It ensures:
- **Security**: Unauthorized persons cannot access company premises or sensitive data
- **Compliance**: Audit trails for regulatory oversight (GDPR, SOC 2)
- **Safety**: Emergency protocols function correctly; occupancy tracked during crises
- **Professional environment**: Visitor management protects employee comfort and productivity

**Scope**: All employees, contractors, vendors, customers, and visitors.

## 2. Badge Access & Credentials

### 2.1 Employee Badge Issuance
- **Timing**: Issued on Day 1; part of Onboarding Day 1 checklist (mandatory security training must be completed first)
- **Technology**: RFID-enabled badge; activates electronic locks; scanned at entry points and throughout building
- **Access Levels**:
  - **Level 1 (General)**: Main entrance, common areas, kitchens, restrooms
  - **Level 2 (Department)**: Assigned department office, server room access (IT only), sensitive areas
  - **Level 3 (Executive)**: Executive suite, board room, vault with customer data backups

### 2.2 Contractor & Vendor Access
- **Approval Process**: Department manager requests temporary badge through IT Security; includes: contractor name, dates, access level, business justification
- **Onboarding**: Contractors receive abbreviated security briefing (30 min) covering: badge usage, visitor protocol, confidentiality agreement, incident reporting
- **Access Duration**: Temporary badge deactivated on last day of contract; physical badge returned to Facilities
- **Compliance**: Any contractor handling PII must sign Data Processing Agreement (DPA) per Information Security Policy

### 2.3 Badge Deactivation
- **Termination**: IT Security deactivates badge immediately upon exit interview
- **Lost Badge**: Employee reports to Facilities; replacement issued same day; old badge disabled within 1 hour
- **Access Review**: Quarterly audit of access logs; mismatches flagged by Security for investigation

## 3. Visitor Management

### 3.1 Visitor Types & Approval

| Visitor Type | Pre-Approval Required | Badge Type | Escort Requirement | Max Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Client/Customer** | Yes (host manager) | Temp badge (1–3 days) | Continuous escort during business hours | Up to 3 days |
| **Vendor / Sales Rep** | Yes (IT/Procurement) | Temp badge (1 day) | Continuous escort | 1 business day |
| **Delivery** | No (for main lobby drop-off) | Visitor badge (lobby only) | Optional (courier does not enter offices) | 15 minutes |
| **Interviewer / Contractor** | Yes (HR or host manager) | Temp badge (as-needed) | For first day; independent access on subsequent days if trained | Contract duration |
| **Family / Guest** | Yes (employee sponsor) | Visitor badge (common areas only) | Continuous escort required | 2 hours |

### 3.2 Visitor Check-In Procedure
1. **Entry**: Visitor presents ID at security desk; Facilities staff scans driver's license/passport
2. **Verification**: Confirm visitor name and host on approved list (Google Sheets shared with Facilities)
3. **Badge Issuance**: Print temp visitor badge; prominent "VISITOR" label; linked to host employee
4. **Waiver**: Visitor signs liability waiver & NDA (standard form; scanned into visitor log)
5. **Escort Brief**: Host employee or Facilities staff orients visitor to: emergency exits, restroom locations, confidential area restrictions, reporting an emergency protocol

### 3.3 Visitor Conduct & Restrictions
- **Allowed**: Common areas (lobby, kitchen, restrooms, conference rooms with host)
- **Restricted**: Server room, IT equipment, engineering lab without explicit approval from VP Engineering or IT Security
- **Photography**: Prohibited without written permission (IP/confidentiality risk); violators asked to leave immediately
- **Confidentiality**: All visitors briefed on NDA; breach reported to General Counsel
- **Unescorted roaming**: Not permitted; if visitor found unescorted, host alerted and escort assigned

### 3.4 Visitor Checkout
- Return visitor badge to Facilities desk
- Host employee confirms departure; badge deactivated
- Entry logged (arrival time, departure time, purpose, areas accessed)

## 4. Remote Access & VPN

While this policy covers physical facilities, remote employees require VPN access per Remote Access & VPN Guide:
- **MFA mandatory** for all VPN access (TOTP authenticator, hardware key, or SMS fallback)
- **Encryption required**: Device must be encrypted (BitLocker or FileVault) before VPN use
- **Access logs**: Reviewed monthly; anomalies escalated to VP Security

## 5. After-Hours & Keycard Access

### 5.1 Extended Hours / Weekend Work
- **Approval**: Manager must approve in writing (email sufficient); submitted to Facilities 24 hours in advance
- **Escort**: Employees working after-hours (after 7 PM) work in pairs or groups (safety policy); after-hours security patrol checks on group every 90 minutes
- **Logging**: Badge readers track entry/exit; anomalies flagged by Security

### 5.2 Keys & Physical Keys
- **Use**: Limited to Facilities staff and designated maintenance personnel
- **Audit**: Physical keys inventoried quarterly; missing keys reported to Facilities Director
- **Retirement**: All keys collected at termination

## 6. Facility Lockdown & Emergencies

### 6.1 Security Lockdown
If active threat or emergency declared (see Emergency Response Plan):
- **All doors locked**: Facilities controls electronic locks from central panel
- **No entry / exit**: Employees shelter in place or evacuate per protocol
- **Law enforcement override**: Responders provided building keys; IT Security assists with electronic override

### 6.2 Power Loss & Backup Systems
- **Battery backup**: Electronic locks revert to **fail-secure** (locked) in power outage; does not automatically unlock
- **Manual override**: Facilities staff can unlock from inside using manual release switch
- **Emergency release**: Fire alarm activation unlocks all doors (emergency egress requirement)

## 7. Facilities Audit & Review

- **Monthly**: Facilities reviews badge access logs; reports to VP Security any after-hours anomalies
- **Quarterly**: IT Security audits access levels against employee roles; flags and revokes inappropriate access
- **Annually**: Full access review; validate current employee list, contractor list, and access levels
- **Incident**: Any unauthorized access attempt, lost badge, or security concern reported to VP Security within 4 hours

## 8. Policy Compliance

**Violation Examples & Consequences**:
- Allowing visitor to roam unescorted: Verbal warning + retraining
- Sharing badge with another employee: Formal warning + disciplinary review (Code of Conduct)
- Permitting unauthorized contractor access: Immediate access revocation + HR escalation
- Refusing badge deactivation at termination: HR escalation; potential legal referral
