# Conflict of Interest Policy

## Purpose
This policy establishes Northwind Technologies' framework for identifying, disclosing, and managing conflicts of interest. Conflicts of interest can undermine trust, create legal liability, and compromise objective decision-making. All employees and officers must avoid and disclose conflicts.

## 1. Definition & Scope

**Conflict of Interest**: A situation where a person's personal, financial, or professional interest conflicts with or appears to conflict with their duty to act in Northwind's best interest.

**Applies to**:
- All employees, officers, and directors
- Independent contractors and consultants
- Board members
- Family members if they benefit from the conflict

**Types**:
1. **Personal financial interest** — Direct or indirect financial benefit to the employee or their family
2. **Outside employment** — Employment or consulting with a competitor, customer, or vendor
3. **Related-party transactions** — Transactions with family, friends, or entities the employee controls
4. **Gifts and entertainment** — Receipt of improper gifts or entertainment from vendors/customers
5. **Intellectual property** — Creation of IP that competes with Northwind or uses Northwind confidential information
6. **Hiring & promotion** — Decisions about relatives or close friends
7. **Board directorates** — Service on boards of competitors or significant vendors/customers
8. **Use of position** — Leveraging Northwind position for personal gain

## 2. Disclosure Requirements

**Who must disclose**:
- All employees (annual attestation)
- All officers and directors (annual + quarterly)
- All Board members (annual + before each Board decision)
- All employees involved in procurement, vendor selection, or hiring decisions (annual + per transaction)

**What to disclose**:
- Any outside employment, business ownership, or consulting relationships
- Any financial interest in Northwind vendors, customers, or competitors (direct or through family)
- Any family relationship between Northwind employees (particularly in reporting/approval chains)
- Any gifts, entertainment, or hospitality received from Northwind vendors/customers exceeding $50
- Any charitable or political affiliations that could affect objectivity in business decisions
- Any service on other boards or in other positions of responsibility

### Annual Disclosure Form
All employees must complete and sign an annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form (provided by HR) attesting:

```
I hereby disclose the following conflicts of interest, if any:
1. Outside employment/business:
2. Financial interests in vendors/customers:
3. Family relationships at Northwind:
4. Gifts received:
5. Board service or outside positions:
6. Other conflicts:

I certify that the information is true and complete. I understand that 
failure to disclose is grounds for disciplinary action.

[Signature] [Date]
```

**Deadline**: December 31 each year (due to HR by January 15).

## 3. Management & Mitigation

**Disclosure triggers review**:
1. **HR/General Counsel assessment** — Is the conflict material? Can it be mitigated?
2. **Mitigation plan** — If conflict exists, develop plan to manage it
3. **Escalation** — Material conflicts escalated to VP or CEO depending on severity
4. **Recusal** — Employee recuses themselves from related decisions

### Mitigation Strategies

| Conflict | Mitigation Strategy |
|---|---|
| **Outside employment (non-competing)** | Approved by manager; schedule permits no overlap with Northwind work |
| **Outside employment (competing)** | Not permitted; must cease or employee may be terminated |
| **Financial interest in vendor** | Recuse from vendor selection; disclose to procurement |
| **Family relationship in reporting chain** | One party recuses from performance reviews; escalate to HR |
| **Gifts from vendors** | Disclose; if over policy limits, must be returned or donated |
| **Board service** | Approved by CEO/Board; recuse from relevant business decisions |

**Board-level conflicts**:
- Director with material financial interest must recuse from vote
- Interested director must disclose interest before Board discussion
- Interested director's abstention is noted in Board minutes
- If recusal is not feasible, transaction cannot proceed without Board supermajority vote

## 4. Specific Conflict Scenarios

### Hiring & Promotion
**Conflict**: Employee influences hiring, compensation, or promotion decision for a relative or close friend.

**Policy**: 
- No employee may directly influence hiring/compensation/promotion of a direct report who is a relative
- If family member applies, employee must recuse; hiring manager coordinates with HR
- Relatives working at Northwind must have separate reporting chains (no direct reports)
- Failure to recuse may result in disciplinary action up to termination

**Example**: VP Engineering's sister applies for engineering role. VP Engineering must recuse; HR coordinates with different hiring manager.

### Vendor Selection & Procurement
**Conflict**: Employee influences vendor selection for a company owned by employee or their family.

**Policy**:
- Employee with financial interest in vendor cannot participate in RFP, evaluation, or approval
- Interest must be disclosed to Procurement and General Counsel
- Procurement conducts selection without the interested employee
- If vendor is selected, transaction is noted as related-party; requires Board approval if > $100K annual value

**Example**: CFO's brother-in-law operates a software development firm. CFO must recuse from IT infrastructure RFP that includes brother-in-law's firm.

### Customer & Competitor Relationships
**Conflict**: Employee has financial interest in Northwind customer or competitor; or employee is employed by/invested in competitor.

**Policy**:
- No outside employment with competitor permitted
- Significant financial interest (> $10,000 aggregate or > 1% of company) in customer/competitor must be disclosed and may require recusal
- Does not prohibit public market stock ownership (passive investment in publicly traded companies)
- Recusal from business decisions affecting the customer/competitor

**Example**: Employee owns 5% of startup that competes with Northwind Cloud. Must disclose; recuses from competitive positioning discussions.

### Use of Position for Personal Gain
**Conflict**: Employee uses Northwind position, authority, or information for personal financial benefit.

**Prohibited**:
- Negotiating personal services deals using information learned at Northwind
- Leveraging Northwind vendor relationships for personal business
- Using Northwind resources (equipment, software, staff time) for personal projects
- Disclosing Northwind confidential information for personal gain

**Example**: Employee learns from Northwind customer that they need a particular service; employee solicits the customer's business for a personal consulting entity. Prohibited.

### Board Service & Outside Directorates
**Conflict**: Employee or officer serves on board of external company.

**Policy**:
- Officers and Board members may serve on outside boards with CEO approval
- Board service at competitor is not permitted
- Board service at significant vendor (> $1M annual) or customer (> 10% of Northwind revenue) requires Board approval
- Outside board service must not consume material time or create confidentiality issues
- Director must recuse from Northwind Board decisions affecting the outside entity

**Example**: CEO sits on board of major customer's company. CEO recuses from Northwind pricing decisions affecting that customer.

## 5. Prohibited Activities

**Absolute prohibitions** (no mitigation):
1. **Bribery or kickbacks** — Any improper payment in exchange for favorable treatment (see **Anti-Bribery & Corruption Policy**)
2. **Theft of trade secrets** — Use of Northwind IP or confidential information for personal gain
3. **Fraud or misrepresentation** — Falsifying documents, hiding conflicts, or lying to management
4. **Unfair competition** — Soliciting Northwind customers on behalf of competing entity
5. **Simultaneous competing employment** — Working for competitor while employed by Northwind

**Violation consequences**: Immediate termination and referral to law enforcement if criminal.

## 6. Whistleblower Protections

Employees who report suspected conflicts in good faith are protected from retaliation:
- Reports may be made anonymously via Northwind Ethics Hotline
- Reports to General Counsel are confidential
- Retaliation is grounds for immediate termination of the retaliator
- External reports (SEC, DOJ) are protected by law

## 7. Annual Attestation

All employees attest annually to:
- Completion of conflict of interest training
- Truthfulness of their Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form
- Understanding of consequences for violations
- Commitment to disclose future conflicts promptly

**Non-attestation** is treated as non-compliance; escalated to manager and HR.

## 8. Review & Audit

**Annual review**:
- HR reviews all disclosure forms for completeness
- HR + General Counsel identify material conflicts requiring management
- Audit of procurement decisions to verify no unauthorized conflicts
- Audit of hiring decisions to verify family relationship disclosures

**Quarterly Board audit** (officer-level):
- CFO certifies no undisclosed financial conflicts in vendor payments or customer contracts
- CEO certifies no undisclosed conflicts in strategic decisions
- Board Audit Committee reviews officer conflicts

## 9. Recusal & Documentation

**How to recuse**:
1. Notify manager or decision-maker of conflict
2. State: "I have a conflict of interest in this matter and am recusing myself"
3. Do not participate in discussion, evaluation, or vote
4. Manager documents recusal in meeting notes

**Example documentation**:
> "Jane Smith recused herself from the Acme Corp vendor evaluation due to her family's financial interest in Acme Corp. Jane did not participate in the discussion or vote."

## 10. Exceptions & Waivers

Material conflicts that cannot be mitigated may be waived only by:
- CEO (for employee-level conflicts)
- Board Compensation Committee (for officer-level conflicts)
- Full Board (for Board member conflicts)

**Waiver requirements**:
- Written explanation of conflict and why it cannot be mitigated
- Board approval and documentation in Board minutes
- Periodic re-approval if conflict is ongoing
