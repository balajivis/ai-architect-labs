# Termination & Offboarding Procedure

## 1. Types of Termination

**1.1 Voluntary Resignation**:
- Employee initiates departure
- Standard notice: 2 weeks (management/senior roles may negotiate longer)
- Resignation may be by verbal statement to manager or written email to People Operations
- Effective date is employee's stated date (or 2 weeks from notice if not specified)

**1.2 Involuntary Termination for Cause**:
- Performance issues not resolved via Performance Improvement Plan
- Violation of Code of Conduct, harassment, theft, violence
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information
- See Code of Conduct & Professional Standards for examples

**1.3 Involuntary Termination Without Cause (Reduction in Force / RIF)**:
- Business need (restructuring, acquisition, downsizing)
- Role elimination (no performance issues)
- Offers severance and continuation of benefits (see section 4)

**1.4 Termination During Probation**:
- Employees hired may be terminated during 90-day probation with minimal notice
- No severance required unless state law mandates
- Severance offer may be extended for business reasons

## 2. At-Will Employment

All Northwind employees are employed on an at-will basis, meaning:
- Northwind may terminate employment for any legal reason or no reason
- Employee may resign at any time
- This relationship may not be modified except via written agreement signed by CEO and employee

Exceptions: written employment contracts, collective bargaining agreements (if applicable), or law-mandated rights (whistleblower protection, military leave, jury duty).

## 3. Termination for Cause Procedure

**3.1 Documentation & Escalation**:
1. Manager documents performance issues or misconduct with specific examples, dates, and impact
2. If possible, a Performance Improvement Plan is offered (see Performance Review & Feedback Process, section 5)
3. If PIP is not appropriate (egregious violation) or PIP fails, manager escalates to VP People and General Counsel
4. HR and Legal review facts and determine if termination is justified and legally sound

**3.2 Termination Meeting**:
- Conducted by manager + HR representative (General Counsel if legal exposure is high)
- Meeting is brief and factual: "As of today, your employment with Northwind is terminated"
- Employee is informed of final paycheck, benefits continuation (COBRA), and any severance
- Employee is asked to return company property (laptop, keys, badge)
- Final paycheck includes all accrued PTO (except in states where PTO is forfeited; see section 5)

**3.3 Post-Termination**:
- IT disables access to all systems (Okta, GitHub, AWS, Azure, email) within 2 hours
- Badge access revoked; office security notified
- Slack status changed to "former employee"; messages archived
- Personal effects (if any) are collected and returned or discarded

## 4. Involuntary Termination Without Cause (RIF / Layoff)

**4.1 Severance Package**:
| Tenure | Base Severance | Bonus Severance | Benefits |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|----------|
| <1 year | 2 weeks | None | 1 month COBRA |
| 1–3 years | 4 weeks | 25% target bonus | 2 months COBRA |
| 3–5 years | 8 weeks | 50% target bonus | 3 months COBRA + placement services |
| 5+ years | 12 weeks | 75% target bonus | 6 months COBRA + placement + career counseling |

*Severance is paid as a lump sum on final paycheck; bonus is pro-rated to termination date.*

**4.2 Severance Conditions**:
- Employee must sign a separation agreement (release of claims) to receive severance
- Separation agreement includes non-disparagement clause (employee agrees not to publicly criticize Northwind)
- Employee agrees to cooperate in transition (knowledge transfer, handoff meetings)
- Confidentiality obligations continue indefinitely post-termination
- Non-solicitation: employee may not recruit Northwind employees for 12 months

**4.3 Mass Layoffs** (>20% of function in 30 days):
- WARN Act notice (60 days) provided to affected employees, state labor dept, local authorities
- Same severance package applies
- HR meets with each employee individually

## 5. Final Paycheck & PTO Payout

**5.1 Timing**:
- Final paycheck is issued on next regular payroll date or within 5 business days (per state law)
- Includes base salary through final day, reimbursable expenses, accrued vacation
- Tax withholding still applies

**5.2 Unused PTO**:
- **California, New York, and other "use-it-or-lose-it" prohibited states**: All accrued PTO is paid out at final rate of pay
- **Other states**: Accrued PTO is forfeited unless company policy is more generous (Northwind's default: forfeited; exceptions may be made via severance agreement)
- Check state law for employee's work location

**5.3 Bonus & Incentives**:
- Annual bonus is forfeited unless employment extends through bonus payout date, or severance agreement specifies otherwise
- Commission (for Sales) is pro-rated through final day
- RSU grants that have not vested are forfeited (no acceleration unless severance agreement specifies)

## 6. Benefits Continuation

**6.1 COBRA** (Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act):
- Employees may continue health insurance for up to 18 months post-termination
- Employee pays full premium (employee + employer share) + 2% administrative fee
- COBRA notice is provided with final paycheck
- Enrollment deadline is 60 days from termination date

**6.2 401(k)**:
- Balance remains in 401(k) account; employee may roll over to IRA or new employer plan
- Vested balance is always available; unvested employer match is forfeited

**6.3 Life Insurance**:
- Company-provided term life ends on last day of employment
- Supplemental life may be convertible to individual term policy (employee-paid)

## 8. Exit Interviews

Departing employees (voluntary and involuntary) are invited to participate in a brief exit interview:
- Conducted by HR or third-party service (confidential)
- Questions cover: manager effectiveness, team culture, reasons for leaving, feedback for Northwind
- Responses are aggregated (anonymously) and reported to leadership quarterly
- Individual feedback is not shared with manager

## 9. References & Employment Verification

Post-termination:
- Northwind provides only factual employment verification (dates, job title, final salary—if requested in writing by third party with employee consent)
- No character references or performance judgments are provided
- Managers are instructed to decline informal reference requests ("I can't discuss that")

## 10. Return of Property & Data

**10.1** Returning employee property:
- Company laptop must be returned (or destroyed if damaged); data is wiped
- Phones, badges, keys, parking passes, and access cards are collected
- Employee is liable for lost/damaged equipment (may be deducted from final paycheck, per state law)

**10.2** Employee personal data**:
- Files on employee's Slack are archived but not deleted
- Google Drive / Microsoft OneDrive documents are transferred to manager (per retention policy)
- Personal photos/files (if any) are offered back to employee or discarded

**10.3** Confidentiality & Non-Compete**:
- Employees sign Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) at hire; obligation continues post-termination
- Non-compete agreements are NOT standard at Northwind (California prohibition)
- Non-solicitation of employees is enforceable for 12 months post-termination

## 11. Legal & Regulatory Compliance

**11.1** Termination documentation is kept in confidential personnel file (Workday) for 7 years per legal requirements.

**11.2** State-specific rules apply:
- California: at-will with enhanced severance, no enforceable non-competes
- New York: WARN Act notice if applicable, severance not required but if offered, certain legal language is required
- Texas: at-will, typical employment law

Specific state rules are reviewed by General Counsel and communicated to HR before termination.

**11.3** Discrimination & Retaliation:
- Termination may not be based on protected characteristics (race, gender, age, disability, etc.)
- Retaliation against whistleblowers (safety violations, legal/regulatory reporting) is prohibited
- Pattern of terminations of one gender/race triggers EEOC review if noticed
