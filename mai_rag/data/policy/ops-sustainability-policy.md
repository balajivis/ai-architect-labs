# Sustainability Policy

## 1. Purpose & Scope

Northwind Technologies commits to environmental sustainability as a core value and competitive advantage. This policy outlines company initiatives to reduce carbon footprint, minimize waste, conserve resources, and support a circular economy.

**Scope**: All Northwind operations—facilities, supply chain, employee behavior, and vendor relationships.

**Goal**: Achieve **carbon-neutral operations by 2030** (Scope 1 & 2 emissions); **80% waste reduction by 2028**.

## 2. Environmental Commitments

### 2.1 Carbon & Energy
- **Renewable energy**: Austin HQ powered by 100% renewable (wind/solar) via Green Mountain Energy contract; reviewed annually
- **Cloud computing**: AWS and Azure carbon-neutral certifications verified; choice of data regions for lower-carbon processing (US regions prioritized over distant international)
- **Commute reduction**: Remote-work policy (see Remote Access/VPN Guide) reduces employee commute emissions; targets 60% remote workforce (current: 45%, 2024 baseline)
- **Travel**: Prefer video conferencing; carbon offset program for mandatory air/vehicle travel via offset broker (verified carbon credits, retired quarterly)
- **Target**: 25% reduction in Scope 1 & 2 emissions by 2027 (baseline: 2024)

### 2.2 Waste Reduction & Recycling
- **Paper**: Double-sided printing default; digital-first document handling; target 50% reduction in paper use by 2027
- **Recycling**: Comprehensive recycling bins (compost, recyclable, landfill) in all break rooms and offices
- **E-waste**: All end-of-life electronics sent to certified e-waste recycler (R2 or e-Stewards certified); data destruction certificate retained per Equipment & Asset Management Policy
- **Packaging**: Preference for vendors using sustainable/minimal packaging; encourages reusable containers
- **Target**: Divert 80% of waste from landfill by 2028 (current: 35%, 2024 baseline)

### 2.3 Water Conservation
- **Fixtures**: Low-flow toilets, faucets, and showers in all facilities (retrofitted 2024); estimated 40% water reduction
- **Landscape**: Drought-resistant native plants around HQ (Austin pilot); reduces irrigation needs
- **Leak detection**: Facilities inspects for leaks quarterly; reports to VP Facilities
- **Target**: 30% reduction in water use by 2027 (baseline: 2024)

### 2.4 Responsible Procurement
- **Vendor selection**: Environmental practices considered in RFP evaluation; preference for suppliers with ISO 14001 or similar certification
- **Supply chain**: Northwind's Vendor Procurement & Third-Party Risk Policy extended to include environmental criteria
- **Local sourcing**: When feasible, prefer local suppliers (reduce transportation emissions)
- **Sustainable materials**: Office furniture sourced from manufacturers using FSC-certified wood or recycled materials

## 3. Employee Engagement & Accountability

### 3.1 Sustainability Champion Program
- **Nomination**: 1 champion per department; volunteer role
- **Responsibilities**: Champion organizes department-level sustainability initiatives (e.g., paperless weeks, carpool coordination, e-waste collection drives)
- **Meetings**: Monthly sustainability committee meeting (all champions + Facilities + ESG Committee)
- **Recognition**: Annual award for top sustainability champion (announced at all-hands); perks (e.g., $500 donation to environmental nonprofit of choice)

### 3.2 Sustainability Training
- **New hire**: 30-minute sustainability overview during Onboarding Day 1; covers recycling, travel policy, energy conservation
- **Annual**: Lunch-and-learn sessions (monthly); topics: carbon footprint calc, sustainable supply chains, climate science updates
- **Certification**: Optional: Carbon Literacy Project (3-hour course); company covers cost; 20+ employees certified as of 2026

### 3.3 Goal-Setting & Accountability
- **Individual objectives**: Managers encouraged to set 1–2 sustainability OKRs per employee (e.g., "Achieve zero single-use plastics in my team's office by Q4")
- **Team budgets**: Department sustainability improvements (e.g., plant-based options in break room) submitted for ESG Committee review; approved improvements funded from sustainability budget (capped at $50K/year)
- **Measurement**: Sustainability metrics tracked in company dashboard; results shared quarterly

### 4.1 Carbon Offset Programs
- **Mechanism**: Northwind purchases verified carbon credits for unavoidable Scope 1 & 2 emissions and mandatory travel
- **Standards**: Only Gold Standard, Verra (VCS), or Plan Vivo certified credits accepted; audited quarterly by Finance
- **Budget**: 0.5% of revenue allocated to offsets; reviewed annually by Board
- **Transparency**: Annual sustainability report published (public-facing); third-party audit conducted

## 5. Supplier & Vendor Expectations

### 5.1 Sustainability Criteria in Vendor Contracts
- **Clause**: Vendors expected to provide sustainability plan or certifications; gaps flagged for improvement within 12 months
- **Audit**: Finance/Procurement conducts annual sustainability check; low-performing vendors may be de-prioritized
- **Communication**: Vendor sustainability report requested annually; findings shared with VP Operations

### 5.2 Travel & Logistics Optimization
- **Video-first**: Meetings default to video conferencing per Remote Access/VPN Guide; business travel pre-approved by manager to justify carbon cost
- **Consolidation**: Logistics teams consolidate shipments to reduce vehicle miles; preferred carriers have carbon-reduction programs
- **Offset**: Mandatory air travel (>500 miles) automatically enrolled in carbon offset; employee selects offset type (reforestation, renewable energy, methane capture)

## 6. Measurement & Reporting

### 6.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- **Carbon emissions** (Scope 1, 2, 3): Measured annually; target: 25% reduction by 2027 vs. 2024 baseline
- **Waste diversion rate**: % of waste sent to landfill; target: 20% diversion by 2027
- **Water use** (gallons): Target: 30% reduction by 2027
- **Renewable energy %**: Target: 100% by 2027 (current: 100% HQ; expand to satellite offices)
- **Employee engagement**: % of employees participating in sustainability initiatives; target: 50%

### 6.2 Annual Sustainability Report
- **Scope**: Published each March; covers prior calendar year
- **Contents**: Carbon footprint analysis, waste metrics, water use, employee participation, vendor assessment, challenges, future roadmap
- **Audience**: Employees, customers, investors, public (website, PDF download)
- **Audit**: Third-party verification conducted; report submitted to ESG framework (CSRD, GRI, or TCFD)
- **Board review**: CFO presents findings to Board in Q2 meeting

## 7. Sustainability Committee

- **Chair**: VP Facilities (or ESG Director if hired)
- **Members**: Facilities Manager, IT Ops, HR, Finance, 2+ employee sustainability champions, 1 external advisor (environmental consultant)
- **Frequency**: Monthly meetings; quarterly Board update
- **Responsibilities**: Set targets, approve initiatives, track KPIs, oversee training, drive culture change

## 8. Policy Compliance & Consequences

| Violation | Example | Consequence |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **Deliberate waste** | Throwing recyclables in landfill bin | Verbal coaching; repeat → formal warning |
| **Excessive travel** | Unnecessary air travel instead of video call | Manager discussion; discourage; count carbon cost in project ROI |
| **Vendor non-compliance** | Vendor fails sustainability audit twice | 90-day improvement plan; if no progress, seek replacement vendor |
| **Misreporting emissions** | Finance provides inaccurate carbon data in report | Audit by external firm; corrected report published; process review |

## 9. Escalation & Accountability

If VP Facilities detects systemic sustainability violations (e.g., department bypassing recycling; supplier ignoring environmental standards):
1. **Discussion**: VP Facilities meets with department manager or Procurement to understand barriers
2. **Corrective action plan**: Agree on concrete steps; 30-day window to comply
3. **Escalation**: If not resolved, CEO and Board Sustainability Committee (if exists) notified
4. **Consequences**: Continued failure may affect performance reviews or supplier relationships
