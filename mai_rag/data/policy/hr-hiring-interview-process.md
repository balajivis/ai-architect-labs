# Hiring & Interview Process

## 1. Overview

This document describes Northwind Technologies' standardized hiring process, from job opening through offer acceptance. Our goal is to hire talented, diverse candidates in a timely and fair manner while ensuring legal compliance and minimizing bias.

## 2. Job Requisition & Planning

**2.1** All new hires require:
- Written business justification (org chart change, backfill, new scope)
- Budget approval from Finance
- Signed requisition from VP of the relevant function

**2.2** Hiring manager and HR collaborate on:
- Job title, level, and job description
- Compensation band (see Compensation Philosophy)
- Required vs. nice-to-have skills
- Candidate sourcing strategy (internal referral, recruiter, job boards)

**2.3** Job descriptions include:
- Essential functions and responsibilities
- Required qualifications (education, experience, certifications)
- Preferred qualifications
- Accommodation note: "Northwind welcomes applications from people with disabilities and is committed to providing reasonable accommodations.")

## 3. Sourcing & Outreach

**3.1** Primary sourcing channels:
1. **Internal referral**: Northwind employees refer qualified candidates; $2,000–$5,000 referral bonus if hired
2. **Recruiter**: External or in-house; passive candidate outreach
3. **Job boards**: LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor, niche boards (GitHub Jobs for engineering)
4. **University partnerships**: Campus recruiting for entry-level

**3.2** Blind resume screening:
- Candidate names, ages, or other identifying info may be redacted during initial screening to reduce bias
- Screening focuses on qualifications vs. job requirements

## 4. Interview Stages

| Stage | Duration | Participants | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------------|-------|
| **Phone Screen** | 15–20 min | Recruiter or Hiring Manager | Baseline qualifications, interest level, logistics |
| **Technical/Role Screen** | 45–60 min | Senior engineer / manager | Technical depth, problem-solving, domain knowledge |
| **System Design / Case Study** | 60–90 min | 2–3 senior staff | Architecture, scalability, analytical thinking |
| **Behavioral / Values** | 45–60 min | Manager + peer | Team fit, communication, alignment with values |
| **Executive Round** (optional) | 30–45 min | Director or VP | Strategic fit, leadership potential |
| **Reference Check** | 20–30 min | HR | Verification; prior performance and behavior |

**4.1 Phone Screen**:
- Confirm basic requirements (location, work authorization, availability)
- Assess communication and enthusiasm
- Explain Northwind culture and role
- Answer candidate questions

**4.2 Technical Round(s)**:
- Coding exercise, system design problem, or case study relevant to role
- Whiteboard, online IDE (HackerRank, LeetCode), or take-home exercise
- Interviewers assess depth, approach, communication, and ability to iterate

**4.3 Behavioral Round**:
- Competency-based questions (e.g., "Tell me about a time you resolved a conflict with a colleague")
- Questions on Code of Conduct understanding, inclusion, and values alignment
- Candidate questions about team, growth opportunities, work-life balance

**4.4 Reference Checks**:
- Contacted only after verbal offer acceptance (not before)
- HR speaks with 2–3 recent managers or senior colleagues
- Questions focus on performance, teamwork, and reliability
- Information is kept confidential; candidate is informed a reference was contacted but not specific feedback

## 5. Evaluation & Decision-Making

**5.1 Interview Debrief**:
- Each interviewer scores candidates on a 1–5 scale (1=no hire, 5=strong hire)
- Hiring committee meets to discuss scores, calibrate, and make recommendation
- Consensus is preferred; VP may override if needed

**5.2 Decision Factors**:
- **Qualifications**: Does candidate meet essential requirements?
- **Technical fit**: Can they do the job?
- **Growth potential**: Can they learn and advance?
- **Team fit**: Will they collaborate well?
- **Values alignment**: Do they embody Northwind principles (see Code of Conduct)?
- **Diversity**: Does candidate bring diverse perspective? Avoid homogeneous hiring.

**5.3 Offer Stage**:
- If reference check passes, HR extends verbal offer (base salary, bonus, equity, start date)
- Candidate accepts verbally; written offer follows
- Offer is conditional on background check (see Background Check Policy)

## 6. Offer & Acceptance

**6.1 Offer Letter** includes:
- Title, level, and reporting manager
- Base salary and target bonus
- RSU grant and vesting schedule
- Benefits eligibility date
- Start date
- At-will employment acknowledgment
- Confidentiality & non-solicitation terms (see Code of Conduct & Professional Standards)
- Acceptance deadline (typically 5 business days)

**6.2 Non-Disparate Treatment**:
- Offers are not made contingent on subjective factors (marital status, age, disability)
- Salary offers are based on role, experience, and market data (see Compensation Philosophy), not negotiation leverage

**6.3 Onboarding**:
- Once accepted, candidate is added to onboarding portal
- Day-1 mandatory training (3 hours: security awareness, MFA enrollment, Acceptable Use, data handling) is scheduled
- Equipment is shipped 3 days before start date
- First 90 days include regular manager 1:1s and formal review (see Employee Onboarding Guide)

## 7. Equal Opportunity & Accommodations

**7.1** Northwind is an Equal Opportunity Employer. We do not discriminate based on:
- Race, color, religion, national origin
- Gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation
- Age, disability, genetic information, veteran status

**7.2 Accommodations in Hiring**:
- Candidates with disabilities may request accommodations (modified interview format, extended time, remote interview)
- Requests are accommodated to the extent feasible
- Accommodations are kept confidential; not shared with hiring team

## 8. Diversity & Inclusion in Recruitment

**8.1** Northwind actively recruits from underrepresented groups:
- Partnerships with organizations supporting women, minorities, and underrepresented groups (Code2040, Rewriting the Code, Ascend Pan-Asian Leaders, etc.)
- Job descriptions use inclusive language
- Referral networks within team should reflect diversity

**8.2** Hiring metrics are tracked and reviewed quarterly:
- Applicant pipeline diversity (gender, race/ethnicity, age)
- Interview stage diversity (% advancing to next round)
- Offer diversity (% of offers to underrepresented groups)
- Disparities are discussed and addressed

See Diversity & Inclusion Policy for company-wide initiatives.

## 9. Candidate Feedback & Rejection

- Candidates who are rejected receive a brief, professional email thanking them for their time
- Specific feedback is rarely provided (liability concerns), but general themes (e.g., "stronger technical depth required") may be shared on request
- Rejected candidates are encouraged to reapply in 12+ months if interest remains
