# Diversity, Equity & Inclusion Policy

## 1. Purpose & Commitment

Northwind Technologies is committed to building a diverse workforce where every employee feels belonged, respected, and able to contribute their fullest potential. We believe that diversity in background, experience, identity, and perspective drives innovation, improves decision-making, and strengthens our culture.

This policy outlines our commitment to diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) and the mechanisms through which we implement and measure progress.

## 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Diversity** | Variety in race, ethnicity, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, disability, national origin, religion, veteran status, and socioeconomic background |
| **Equity** | Fair treatment and access to opportunities; removing systemic barriers; acknowledging that people start from different places |
| **Inclusion** | Creating a culture where all employees feel valued, heard, and empowered to be authentic |

## 3. DEI Governance & Accountability

**3.1 DEI Steering Committee**:
- Chaired by VP People
- Includes representatives from: Engineering, Product, Sales, Finance, Legal, and Employee Resource Groups (ERGs)
- Meets quarterly to review metrics, identify barriers, and approve initiatives
- Reports to CEO and Board annually

**3.2 Individual Accountability**:
- All managers are evaluated on DEI competencies:
  - Hiring: sourcing and interviewing diverse candidates (tracked via recruitment metrics)
  - Retention: equal performance ratings and advancement across groups
  - Inclusion: 360° feedback on psychological safety and belonging
- DEI competency is tied to manager bonus (up to 10% of discretionary bonus pool)

**3.3 Company Metrics**:
- Quarterly tracking of:
  - Applicant pipeline diversity (% women, underrepresented minorities, people with disabilities, etc.)
  - Hiring diversity (% of offers to underrepresented groups)
  - Advancement diversity (% promotion rate by demographic group)
  - Pay equity ($ gap analysis by gender, race, tenure, role)
  - Retention diversity (turnover rate by group; exit interview feedback)
- Metrics are published in annual DEI Report (shared with employees, Board, and public)

## 4. Recruitment & Hiring

**4.1 Sourcing**:
- Northwind partners with organizations supporting underrepresented groups:
  - **Women**: Women Who Code, Rewriting the Code, Ascend Pan-Asian Leaders, Ellevate
  - **Minorities**: Code2040, BLKCODECOLLECTIVE, LATISM, SWAGG Academy
  - **Disability**: DisabilityIN, National Organization on Disability
  - **LGBTQ+**: Out in Tech, Pride in STEM
- Campus recruiting emphasizes HBCUs, HSIs (Hispanic-serving institutions), and schools with strong underrepresented populations
- Job descriptions use inclusive language and avoid gendered pronouns

**4.2 Blind Resume Screening**:
- Candidate names, ages, and other identifying information may be redacted during initial screening (see Hiring & Interview Process)
- Reduces unconscious bias in resume evaluation
- Screening focuses on qualifications vs. job requirements

**4.3 Diverse Interview Panels**:
- Interview panels include women and underrepresented minorities (where available)
- Hiring managers receive unconscious bias training before interviewing
- Interview guides standardize questions to reduce variability and bias

**4.4 Diverse Candidate Slates**:
- Target: every job opening receives candidates from at least 2 underrepresented groups
- If slate is not diverse, recruiter is asked to extend search before moving to interview stage

## 5. Onboarding & Integration

**5.1 Belonging Initiatives**:
- New hire buddy program: assigned peer mentor (ideally from same or similar background) for first 30 days
- Affinity group introductions: connection with relevant ERG (Women@Northwind, Pride@Northwind, etc.)
- Manager training: onboarding best practices, cultural inclusion

**5.2 Employee Resource Groups (ERGs)**:
Northwind sponsors five active ERGs:

| ERG | Focus | Members | Activities |
|-----|-------|---------|-----------|
| **Women@Northwind** | Career development, mentorship, pay equity | 280 | Monthly speakers, networking, salary transparency workshops |
| **Pride@Northwind** | LGBTQ+ inclusion, allyship | 150 | Pride month events, benefits education, support groups |
| **Ascend** | Pan-Asian professional development | 95 | Cultural celebrations, networking, leadership pipeline |
| **BlackLeaders@Northwind** | Black employee support and advancement | 65 | Monthly meetings, career development, community outreach |
| **Parents@Northwind** | Work-life balance, parental leave, childcare | 110 | Peer support, benefits education (see Parental Leave Policy), referral network |

ERGs are volunteer-led and budget-supported (annual budget per ERG: $3,000–$5,000 for events and programming).

**5.3 Sponsorship Program**:
- Executive sponsors (VP+) are matched with high-potential employees from underrepresented groups
- Goal: accelerate career development and advancement
- Sponsors advocate for sponsee in promotion discussions

## 6. Retention & Career Development

**6.1 Mentorship & Sponsorship**:
- Formal mentorship program matches employees with mentors (same or cross-function)
- Mentors receive training on effective mentoring
- Goal: 60% of underrepresented employees have a mentor within 1 year of hire

**6.2 Leadership Development**:
- Northwind offers leadership development programs (Kellogg MBA, executive coaching, etc.)
- Target: 40%+ of program participants are women and underrepresented minorities
- Talent review process includes succession planning for diverse leadership pipeline

**6.3 Pay Equity Reviews**:
- Annual pay equity audit (see Compensation Philosophy, section 3.1)
- Unexplained gaps are remedied via adjustments or documented justifications
- Results are shared with leadership; trends are addressed through policy changes

## 7. Inclusive Workplace Culture

**7.1 Respectful Communication**:
- Employees are expected to use pronouns and names as each person specifies
- Assumptions about identity are avoided (e.g., "your spouse" vs. "your husband")
- Slurs, stereotypes, and offensive humor are prohibited (see Code of Conduct & Professional Standards)

**7.2 Accessible Facilities & Programs**:
- Austin HQ includes:
  - Gender-neutral and family bathrooms
  - Prayer/meditation quiet spaces
  - Lactation rooms for nursing parents
  - Wheelchair accessible entrances, elevators, restrooms
  - Accessible parking and shuttle service
  - ASL interpreters available for all-hands meetings
- All company events (lunches, socials, offsites) are alcohol-free or include non-alcoholic options
- Dietary accommodations (vegetarian, halal, kosher, gluten-free) are standard

**7.3 Flexible Work Arrangements**:
- Remote work (see Remote & Hybrid Work Policy) enables participation by employees with disabilities, caregiving obligations, or other needs
- Part-time work available by arrangement with manager + HR
- Schedule flexibility (core hours: 10am–3pm CT) accommodates religious observance, childcare, etc.

**7.4 Conflict Resolution & Support**:
- Grievance procedure (see Grievance Procedure) is available for all disputes, including bias/discrimination
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP) offers free counseling for work and personal issues
- Restorative justice / mediation offered as alternative to formal investigation (with consent)

## 8. Reporting & Accountability

**8.1 Annual DEI Report**:
- Published by February 28 each year (covers prior calendar year)
- Includes:
  - Diversity metrics (headcount by function, level, demographics)
  - Hiring metrics (applicants, offers, acceptance rate by demographics)
  - Pay equity findings (gaps by gender, race, aggregated)
  - Advancement/retention metrics
  - ERG membership and activities
  - Initiatives launched and outcomes
  - Goals for coming year

**8.2 Public Accountability**:
- DEI Report is published on Northwind's website (careers.northwind.tech)
- CEO letter discusses progress and commitments
- Board reviews DEI metrics quarterly and receives annual report

**8.3 Employee Feedback**:
- Annual engagement survey includes questions on inclusion, belonging, and psychological safety
- Results are reviewed by function; function leaders create action plans
- Anonymous feedback mechanisms (e.g., ERG surveys) are used to identify culture issues

## 9. Discrimination & Bias Complaints

**9.1** All discrimination complaints are treated seriously and investigated (see Anti-Harassment & Non-Discrimination Policy and Grievance Procedure).

**9.2** Systemic patterns (e.g., all complaints about one manager, or a pattern of bias in one function) trigger:
- Investigation
- Training for the team or individual
- Possible policy or process change
- Documentation in DEI Report (anonymized)

## 10. Procurement & Supplier Diversity

**10.1** Northwind procures from diverse suppliers:
- Vendors are evaluated partly on diversity practices (women-owned, minority-owned, LGBTQ-owned business certification)
- Supplier diversity program target: 15% of procurement spend to underrepresented-owned vendors by 2028

## 11. Community & Social Impact

**11.1** Northwind supports DEI in the broader tech community:
- Scholarship program: $50,000/year to underrepresented minorities in computer science
- Internship program: partners with Code2040, Year Up, and other pipelines
- Mentorship: employees volunteer as mentors for underrepresented students (supported with paid volunteer time)

## 12. Ongoing Learning & Accountability

**12.1 Training**:
- All employees: unconscious bias training annually (online, ~1 hour)
- Managers: inclusive leadership training (biennial, in-person, 4 hours)
- ERG leaders: community facilitation and conflict resolution training
- Hiring managers: structured interview and evaluation training

**12.2 Continuous Improvement**:
- DEI initiatives are reviewed annually; unsuccessful programs are discontinued or redesigned
- Feedback from employees is solicited and incorporated
- External DEI consultant conducts audit every 2 years
