# Travel Safety & Duty of Care Policy

## 1. Purpose & Scope

Northwind Technologies is committed to employee safety during business travel. This policy establishes protocols for travel authorization, risk assessment, security, and emergency support.

**Scope**: All employees, contractors, and authorized guests traveling on company business domestically or internationally.

**Duty of care**: Northwind assumes responsibility for employee wellbeing during authorized travel and provides appropriate support (insurance, communication, emergency assistance).

## 2. Travel Authorization & Pre-Travel Process

### 2.1 Approval Workflow
1. **Trip planning** (2–4 weeks before): Employee or manager initiates travel request in Concur or expense system
2. **Approval**: Manager approves; finance approves if >$5K budget or international; IT Security approves if taking Confidential/Restricted data
3. **Risk assessment**: VP Security or designee reviews trip (destination, dates, travel companions) for safety / political risk; flags high-risk areas (see Section 2.2)
4. **Trip details**: Finalized itinerary submitted (flights, hotel, ground transport, meetings, emergency contacts)

### 2.2 Risk Assessment & High-Risk Destinations
**Green** (low risk): US, Western Europe, Canada, Australia, Japan, Singapore, UAE
**Yellow** (moderate risk): Mexico, India, Eastern Europe, parts of Southeast Asia; requires manager + VP Security approval
**Red** (high risk): Syria, Yemen, parts of Nigeria, Venezuela, Afghanistan; VP Security approval required + mandatory travel insurance + security brief

**Assessment criteria**:
- **Health**: Zika, malaria, dengue, COVID-19 waves; vaccination requirements
- **Political**: Recent elections, protests, or civil unrest (check State Department travel advisories)
- **Crime**: Violent crime rates, theft, kidnapping risk; US State Dept. Overseas Security Policy Board (OSPB) reports
- **Infrastructure**: Hospital quality, emergency services availability, internet reliability

**If Red designation**: Northwind may prohibit travel or require:
- Enhanced travel insurance (kidnap & ransom, medical evacuation)
- In-country security contact (driver, fixer, local guide)
- Check-in protocol (daily call to VP People or on-call contact)
- Travel companion required (not solo travel)

## 3. Pre-Travel Preparation

### 3.1 Health & Vaccinations
- **Passport & visas**: Employee responsible; Northwind reimburses visa costs; manager verifies valid passport (6+ months remaining) before booking
- **Vaccinations**: Recommended vaccines per CDC/WHO for destination; company covers cost; documentation kept by HR
- **Travel insurance**: Purchased by Concur or Northwind travel agent for all international trips; covers medical evacuation, trip cancellation, emergency dental
- **Medications**: Prescription medications in original bottles; traveler advised to carry doctor's note for controlled substances

### 3.2 Device & Data Security
**Critical**: See Remote Access/VPN Guide for travel with company devices.

- **Encryption**: Device must have full-disk encryption (BitLocker, FileVault); verified before departure
- **Confidential data**: Minimize on-device; keep data backups in cloud (never unencrypted USB drive)
- **VPN mandatory**: All work access from hotel/airport/non-Northwind networks requires VPN connection
- **MFA**: All cloud logins (AWS, Azure, email, GitHub) use MFA (TOTP preferred; SMS fallback acceptable for travel)
- **Public WiFi**: Disabled unless on VPN; hotel WiFi strongly discouraged (high phishing risk)
- **Lost device**: Employee reports to IT immediately; remote wipe initiated; incident reported to VP Security
- **Biometric locking**: Laptop locked with password + biometric when left unattended

### 3.3 Emergency Contacts & Communication
- **Emergency contact**: Updated in HR system (next of kin, phone); manager has copy
- **Trip itinerary**: Provided to manager and emergency contact; includes flight numbers, hotel addresses, phone number
- **Check-in protocol**: For high-risk destinations (Red), employee calls or emails on-call manager daily (or per agreed schedule)
- **Communication plan**: Agree on contact method if needed (email, Slack, WhatsApp, SMS); note time zones

## 4. During Travel

### 4.1 Personal Safety & Situational Awareness
**While traveling, employee is responsible for personal safety:**
- **Stay aware**: Notice surroundings; avoid distraction (phone, headphones) in unfamiliar areas
- **Transportation**: Use registered taxis or ride-share (Uber, Grab, Lyft) in cities; avoid unmarked taxis
- **Valuables**: Keep passport, wallet, phone secure; do not display expensive items (jewelry, high-end camera)
- **Nightlife**: Travel with colleagues; avoid isolated areas or late-night outings in unfamiliar cities
- **Drugs/alcohol**: Avoid excessive drinking or recreational drugs; stay in control
- **Harassment/assault**: Report to manager + HR immediately; support offered (counseling, relocation, leave)

### 4.2 Health & Medical Care
- **Insurance**: Company travel insurance covers emergency medical; policy details provided pre-travel
- **Doctor/hospital**: If needed, contact insurance hotline (card number on policy); ask for English-speaking provider
- **Prescription refill**: Concur or travel agent can assist; reimbursed as travel expense
- **Mental health**: EAP available 24/7; call number provided; confidential counseling via phone

### 4.3 Work Continuity & Schedule
- **Availability**: Employees expected to maintain normal work hours (adjusted for time zone); communication with manager re: availability
- **VPN access**: Maintained throughout travel (see Section 3.2); bandwidth concerns communicated to team
- **Meetings**: If traveling across significant time zones, manager adjusts meeting schedules to minimize strain
- **Day off**: Employees may request 1 day "buffer" (travel day) for international trips (no work expected; uses PTO or unpaid leave per Leave & Time-Off Policy)

## 5. Expense & Reimbursement

See Expense & Travel Policy for detailed rules. **Summary**:
- **Approval threshold**: Trips >$5K require CFO approval; international trips flagged for VP People review
- **Receipts**: Itemized receipt required for all expenses >$75
- **Mileage**: Personal vehicle reimbursed at IRS rate ($0.655/mile, 2025); not recommended; rental car preferred
- **Per diem**: Meals covered in full (with receipts) or per diem rate (country-dependent); alcohol limited to wine/beer per meal
- **Ground transport**: Taxi, Uber, rental car, parking covered; personal convenience items not covered
- **Timeline**: Reimbursement submitted within 30 days of return; processed within 10 business days

## 6. Special Circumstances

### 6.1 International Travel with Confidential or Restricted Data
- **Approval**: Requires VP Security sign-off; business justification documented
- **Device security**: Full-disk encryption mandatory; no data exported to non-encrypted USB
- **Access controls**: VPN + MFA required for all work; device not left unattended in public
- **Backup**: Cloud backup of work data (never local-only); no data stored on device longer than necessary
- **Customs**: Device may be inspected at border (rare for US/EU travel); advise employee to expect this; IT Security provides written documentation if requested

### 6.2 Traveling with Family (Spouse/Children)
- **Approval**: Same as business travel; trip must have legitimate business purpose (not mixed leisure)
- **Cost**: Company covers employee travel + accommodation; does not cover family travel costs (exception: meal reimbursement if family joins for dinner)
- **Safety**: Family treated as "authorized visitor" for office access purposes (see Office Access & Visitor Policy); no access to confidential areas
- **Liability**: Northwind not responsible for family member injury; family member signs waiver

### 6.3 Extended Travel (4+ weeks) or Relocation
- **Work visa**: Employee responsible for obtaining (with company financial support); HR provides documentation (employment letter, salary confirmation)
- **Tax implications**: CFO consults with tax advisors; employee informed of tax consequences
- **Health insurance**: Verified for destination; supplemental insurance purchased if gaps
- **Continuing employment**: If travel >30 days, HR updates employment records; return date documented

## 7. Emergency Response & Evacuation

### 7.1 Crisis During Travel (Natural Disaster, Civil Unrest, Security Threat)
**Employee's responsibility:**
1. Get to safety (move away from hazard, shelter indoors, etc.)
2. Call manager or on-call contact immediately; provide location and status
3. If immediate danger, call local emergency (911 equivalent) first; then contact Northwind

**Northwind's responsibility** (VP People + VP Security):
1. Attempt to reach employee; confirm safety; provide guidance (shelter-in-place, evacuation, etc.)
2. Engage travel insurance crisis hotline if medical or evacuation needed
3. Monitor local situation (State Dept. alerts, news); offer support or relocation if condition worsens
4. Coordinate with embassy (if US citizen in foreign country) if help needed
5. Communicate with employee's family and manager; coordinate return trip
6. Provide counseling / EAP support post-incident; consider paid leave for recovery

### 7.2 Evacuation Assistance
- **Travel insurance hotline**: Covers medical evacuation, security evacuation, or trip interruption
- **On-call contact**: VP People on-call 24/7 during crisis; phone number provided with travel itinerary
- **Return travel**: Northwind arranges and pays for emergency return to US (or home country) if needed; reimbursement automatic

## 8. Post-Travel

### 8.1 Expense Reimbursement
- Submit within 30 days; all receipts attached; personal expenses clearly marked and deducted
- Reimbursement processed within 10 business days

### 8.2 Feedback & Trip Report (Optional)
- If international travel (duration >5 days) or high-risk destination: Employee may be asked to complete brief trip report (destination, key outcomes, lessons learned, safety/logistical notes)
- Used to improve future travel planning; shared with VP People and relevant business stakeholders

### 8.3 Health Check-In
- Employee notifies HR if illness or injury occurs during travel and requires follow-up care
- For Red-destination travel: debrief call with VP People (optional, but encouraged) to discuss safety, experiences, recommendations

## 9. Policy Compliance & Escalation

**Violations**:
| Violation | Example | Consequence |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **Unauthorized trip** | Travel without approval; no business justification | Reimbursement denied; manager discussion |
| **Security breach** | Accessing Confidential data via unencrypted device; no VPN | Formal warning; potential data incident notification |
| **High-risk destination without mitigation** | Travel to Red zone; no insurance, no check-in protocol | Trip cancelled (retroactively unapproved); reimbursement denied; escalation to CEO |
| **Non-compliance with pre-travel process** | Booked trip without VP Security sign-off for data | Reimbursement conditional on VP Security approval after-the-fact |
| **Failure to report emergency** | Medical incident during travel; not reported for 24+ hours | HR review; potential disciplinary action |

**Escalation pathway**:
- **Manager**: First stop for travel questions, reimbursement timing, schedule adjustments
- **VP People**: Authorization for extended travel, family travel, PTO coordination
- **VP Security**: High-risk destinations, data security, international compliance
- **CEO / Board**: Evacuation or major incident during travel (if media exposure or significant cost)
