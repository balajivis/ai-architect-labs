# Contract Approval Policy

## Purpose
This policy establishes the framework for reviewing, approving, and executing contracts at Northwind Technologies. All binding agreements with external parties must follow this approval workflow to mitigate legal, financial, and operational risk.

## 1. Scope & Applicability
This policy applies to all contracts, including but not limited to:
- Vendor and supplier agreements
- Customer service agreements and SLAs
- Employment contracts and independent contractor agreements
- Software licensing and open source commitments
- Master Services Agreements (MSAs) and Statement of Works (SOWs)
- Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs)
- Joint development and partnership agreements

**Exclusions**: routine purchase orders under $10,000 may bypass full approval if pre-negotiated terms are documented. All other contracts require approval.

## 2. Approval Authority Matrix

| Contract Type | Financial Threshold | Required Approvers | Review Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vendor/Supplier | < $50K | VP Finance + General Counsel | 5 business days |
| Vendor/Supplier | $50K–$250K | CFO + General Counsel + VP Procurement | 7 business days |
| Vendor/Supplier | > $250K | Board Finance Committee + General Counsel | 10 business days |
| Customer Sales | Any | General Counsel + VP Sales + Revenue Counsel | 5–10 business days |
| Employment/Contractor | Any | General Counsel + VP People | 3 business days |
| Licensing (proprietary) | Any | General Counsel + VP Engineering | 5 business days |
| Licensing (open source) | Any | General Counsel + VP Engineering + OSS Committee | 7 business days |
| NDA | < $1M exposure | VP of Sales + General Counsel | 2 business days |
| NDA | > $1M exposure | General Counsel + CEO | 3 business days |
| Partnership/Strategic | Any | CEO + General Counsel | 10 business days |

## 3. Approval Workflow

**Step 1: Submission** — Requestor submits contract draft via Legal portal with business justification, parties involved, and financial terms.

**Step 2: Legal Review** — General Counsel or designee reviews for compliance with MSA standard terms, NDA policy, and IP ownership. Legal may redline or request clarifications.

**Step 3: Risk Assessment** — Legal flags any deviations from standard terms or high-risk language (indemnification, liability caps, governing law, dispute resolution). See **Intellectual Property Policy** for IP ownership guardrails; see **Anti-Bribery & Corruption Policy** for export control and sanctions screening.

**Step 4: Functional Approval** — Finance, Engineering, Sales, or other stakeholders sign off on business terms.

**Step 5: Execution** — Only the General Counsel, CEO, or delegated signatory may execute on behalf of Northwind.

## 4. Standard Terms & Deviations

All vendor and customer contracts should begin from the **Master Services Agreement (MSA) Standard** template. Deviations require written justification and escalated approval:
- Liability caps below $100K require VP Finance approval
- Indemnification clauses extending beyond standard scope require CEO sign-off
- Exclusive dealing or non-compete terms require Board review
- Dispute resolution outside Texas (Northwind's home state) or arbitration clauses require CEO approval

## 5. Third-Party Risk Screening

Before execution, Legal conducts:
- **OFAC/Sanctions screening** against U.S. Department of Commerce lists
- **Know Your Customer (KYC)** due diligence for vendors over $500K annual commitment
- **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** review if vendor will access Confidential or Restricted data (see **Data Classification & Retention Policy**)

## 6. Record Keeping & Audit

All executed contracts are stored in the Legal contract management system with:
- Execution date, renewal date, termination date
- Approval chain (audit trail of all signatories)
- Any redlines or amendments
- DPA linkage (if applicable)

Annual audit of all active contracts ensures compliance with Northwind's evolving standards.

## 7. Escalation & Exceptions

Requests to waive this policy must be submitted to the General Counsel with CEO co-approval. Waivers are documented and reviewed quarterly.
