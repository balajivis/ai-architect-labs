# Grievance Procedure & Dispute Resolution

## 1. Purpose

This Grievance Procedure provides a fair, confidential process for employees to report and resolve concerns about workplace conduct, decisions, or conditions. All employees have the right to raise concerns without fear of retaliation.

This procedure applies to disputes involving:
- Compensation or benefits (see Compensation Philosophy, Benefits Overview)
- Discrimination or harassment (see Anti-Harassment & Non-Discrimination Policy)
- Performance reviews or termination decisions (see Performance Review & Feedback Process, Termination & Offboarding Procedure)
- Code of Conduct violations (see Code of Conduct & Professional Standards)
- Workplace safety or security issues
- Policy interpretation or application

## 2. Informal Resolution (Step 1)

**2.1** Employees are encouraged to resolve concerns directly with the person involved (manager, colleague, peer) before formal escalation:
- Describe the issue clearly and objectively
- Listen to the other person's perspective
- Work toward a mutually acceptable solution
- Document the conversation (date, participants, outcome) in writing to yourself

**2.2** If informal resolution is not feasible or unsuccessful:
- Employee moves to formal escalation (Step 2)
- Informal discussions do not waive the right to formal escalation
- Informal attempts are NOT a prerequisite to filing a formal grievance if the issue is serious (harassment, discrimination, safety)

## 3. Formal Grievance Filing (Step 2)

**3.1 Submitting the Grievance**:
- Employee submits a written grievance (email or form) to People Operations and/or General Counsel
- If the issue involves the direct manager, escalate directly to People Operations (skip manager notification)
- If the issue involves the VP People, escalate to General Counsel or CEO

**3.2 Content of Grievance**:
Grievance should include:
- Title: "Formal Grievance: [Issue Type]"
- Description: What happened, when (dates/timeline), location
- Parties involved: Who is involved (respondent, witnesses)
- Impact: How the issue affected you (emotional, professional, financial)
- Relief sought: What resolution do you request?
- Supporting evidence: Emails, messages, calendar invites, documentation

**Example**:
```
Subject: Formal Grievance: Compensation Discrepancy

I was hired as a Senior Engineer in January 2024 at $180K base salary. 
In May 2026, I learned that a peer in the same role, hired 2 months after 
me, was offered $195K. I have consistently exceeded performance 
expectations ("Exceeds" rating in 2024 and 2025). 

I request:
1. Written explanation of the salary difference
2. Adjustment to market rate if unjustified
3. Back pay for the differential

Evidence: Offer letters (attached), performance reviews, salary benchmarking 
(attached).
```

**3.3 Submission & Acknowledgment**:
- HR acknowledges receipt within 24 hours
- HR informs employee of next steps and expected timeline
- Grievance is entered into HR tracking system (confidential)

## 4. Investigation (Step 3)

**4.1 Investigation Lead**:
- For most grievances: HR conducts investigation
- For discrimination/harassment: HR + General Counsel
- For safety/security: HR + VP Security
- Investigator is impartial and has no direct involvement in the dispute

**4.2 Investigation Timeline**:
- Initial interviews (respondent, witnesses) within 5 business days
- Follow-up interviews as needed within 7 business days
- Document review and analysis within 3 business days
- Investigation concludes with written findings within 15 business days of grievance filing

**4.3 Investigation Process**:
1. **Notify respondent**: Respondent receives written description of allegation (sufficient detail to respond, but not the grievant's name if confidentiality is requested)
2. **Interview respondent**: Respondent provides statement and evidence
3. **Interview witnesses**: Neutral parties who have relevant information
4. **Collect evidence**: Emails, chat logs, documents, records
5. **Analyze**: Investigator weighs credibility, evidence consistency, company policy
6. **Determine facts**: What likely happened (preponderance of evidence standard)

**4.4 Confidentiality**:
- Investigation details are shared on a need-to-know basis
- Information is not disclosed to workplace gossip or non-essential parties
- Respondent and grievant are reminded of confidentiality obligations
- Violation of investigation confidentiality may result in discipline

## 5. Investigation Findings & Remedies (Step 4)

**5.1 Findings Report**:
- Written report documents alleged issue, evidence, findings, and reasoning
- Report is provided to grievant and respondent within 3 business days of investigation completion
- Report states one of three conclusions:
  - **Substantiated**: Alleged conduct did occur and violates policy
  - **Partially substantiated**: Some allegations are supported; others are not
  - **Unsubstantiated**: Insufficient evidence to conclude policy violation

**5.2 Remedies If Substantiated**:

| Issue Type | Possible Remedy |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Compensation discrepancy** | Salary adjustment + back pay + review of future raises |
| **Harassment** | Respondent disciplined (verbal warning → suspension → termination); Grievant offered counseling/support |
| **Discrimination** | Systemic review + bias training + decision reversal (if applicable); Respondent discipline |
| **Performance review dispute** | Review re-evaluated by HR + manager; adjustment if error found |
| **Retaliation** | Immediate cessation of adverse action + policy review |

**5.3 Remedies If Unsubstantiated**:
- No action taken against respondent
- Grievant is offered support (counseling, mediation, transfer to different team if working relationship is damaged)
- Policy refresher may be offered to both parties

## 6. Appeal (Step 5)

**6.1 Appeal Rights**:
- Grievant may appeal investigation findings or remedy if:
  - New evidence is discovered (was not available during investigation)
  - Process was materially unfair (investigator had conflict of interest, witness not interviewed despite request)
  - Remedy is insufficient (e.g., alleged harasser was verbally warned but grievant requested termination)

**6.2 Appeal Process**:
- Appeal is submitted to VP People or General Counsel within 10 business days of findings notification
- Appeal includes: specific ground for appeal, new evidence (if any), and requested outcome
- VP People or General Counsel reviews appeal and investigation file (de novo review—no automatic deference to original investigator)
- Appeal decision is provided within 10 business days

**6.3 Appeal Outcomes**:
- Findings are upheld (no change)
- Findings are modified (additional substantiation or exoneration)
- Remedy is modified or additional remedy is ordered
- Case is remanded for additional investigation if new evidence warrants

**6.4 Final Decision**:
- After appeal, the decision is final (no further internal appeal)
- Employee retains external legal remedies (labor board complaint, lawsuit) if disputes are not resolved

## 7. Non-Retaliation & Protection

**7.1** Retaliation is prohibited against any employee who:
- Files a grievance in good faith
- Participates in a grievance investigation
- Testifies or provides evidence in a grievance proceeding
- Requests accommodation or reports a policy violation

**7.2** Retaliation includes:
- Negative performance review or lowered rating
- Demotion or transfer to less desirable role
- Reduced hours or compensation
- Exclusion from meetings or projects
- Hostile treatment or "icing out"

**7.3** If retaliation occurs**:
- Employee may file a separate grievance for retaliation
- Retaliation grievances are prioritized and investigated immediately
- If substantiated, respondent (usually manager) faces immediate discipline

## 8. Documentation & Retention

All grievance documents—filing, investigation notes, findings, appeals—are maintained by HR in a confidential grievance file (separate from personnel file) for 7 years per legal requirements.

Documents are not released absent a valid subpoena or legal discovery request.

## 9. External Remedies

Employees retain the right to:
- File a charge with the EEOC (Equal Employment Opportunity Commission) if discrimination is alleged
- File a complaint with state labor board or OSHA (safety/health issues)
- Consult an employment attorney
- Pursue legal action

Using external remedies does not waive Northwind's internal grievance procedure, and vice versa.

## 10. Mediation Alternative

For disputes where both parties consent, Northwind may offer **mediation** as an alternative to formal investigation:
- A trained neutral mediator facilitates discussion between grievant and respondent
- Goal is mutual understanding and resolution
- Mediation is confidential and without prejudice (if mediation fails, either party may pursue formal grievance)
- Mediation must be agreed to by both parties; neither may be compelled
