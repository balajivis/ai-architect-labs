# Corporate Card Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

Northwind Technologies issues corporate credit cards to select employees to streamline business expenses and reduce personal out-of-pocket reimbursement. This policy establishes eligibility, spending limits, use guidelines, and monthly reconciliation procedures.

**Eligible roles:**
- Managers and above (implied travel/entertainment authority)
- Individual contributors with frequent business travel (≥4 trips/year)
- Finance and Procurement staff (vendor payments, conference registrations)
- Executive leadership (Board-approved)

## 2. Card Issuance and Limits

### 2.1 Eligibility and Application
1. **Manager approval**: Applicant's manager submits request to Finance (CFO or Finance Manager)
2. **Background check**: Company initiates background check if not recently completed
3. **Approval**: CFO or designated Finance Lead approves or denies within 5 business days
4. **Activation**: Finance orders card from Amex or Visa; card arrives in 7–10 business days

### 2.2 Spending Limits by Role

| Role | Monthly Limit | Single Transaction Max | Annual Cap |
|------|---------------|---|---|
| Individual Contributor (travel-heavy) | $3,000 | $1,500 | $30,000 |
| Manager | $5,000 | $2,500 | $60,000 |
| Director | $7,500 | $4,000 | $90,000 |
| VP / Executive | $15,000 | $7,500 | Unlimited |
| Finance / Procurement Staff | $8,000 (for vendor payments, registration fees) | $3,000 | $80,000 |

**Monthly limits reset** on the 1st of each month.

### 2.3 Temporary Limit Increases
Employees may request a **temporary limit increase** for:
- Large conference registration (>$3,000)
- Significant client entertainment event
- Capital equipment purchase (requires VP approval)

**Process**: Submit request to Finance at least 5 business days before need, with business justification. Limit increase is temporary (single transaction or 30-day window) and must be approved by CFO or VP Finance.

## 3. Authorized vs. Unauthorized Purchases

### 3.1 Authorized Purchases

**All personal reimbursement categories apply**, including:
- Business travel (flights, hotels, ground transportation, per diem)
- Meals and entertainment (with business purpose)
- Software and SaaS subscriptions
- Office equipment and supplies
- Professional development and conference fees
- Client gifts (up to $50/recipient/year)
- Vendor invoices and contractor payments

### 3.2 Prohibited Purchases

- Personal items (clothing, grooming, personal electronics)
- Gambling, alcohol (solo), tobacco
- Charitable donations (company makes these separately)
- Insurance, medical, dental personal expenses
- Commuting and personal vehicle expenses
- Duplicate expense claims (card + personal reimbursement)

### 3.3 Cash Advances
Cash advances are **not permitted** on corporate cards. Employees must use personal card for cash needs and submit for reimbursement per **Expense Reimbursement Policy**.

## 4. Monthly Reconciliation and Approval

### 4.1 Reconciliation Process
1. **Statement generation**: Card statement issued on the 15th of each month
2. **Employee reconciliation**: Cardholder matches each transaction to expense category and attaches receipts in Expensify within 5 business days
3. **Manager review**: Manager reviews transaction list and approves or flags suspicious charges within 3 business days
4. **Finance audit**: Finance spot-checks 20% of transactions and verifies receipt requirement compliance (≥$75 requires itemized receipt per **Expense Reimbursement Policy**)
5. **Payment**: Finance pays Amex/Visa invoice within 10 business days

### 4.2 Missing Receipt Process
If transaction lacks receipt and exceeds $75:
- Employee must provide affidavit or receipt within 3 business days
- If receipt cannot be provided, employee is liable (personal payment required)
- Manager and Finance are copied on the escalation

### 4.3 Disputed Transactions
If cardholder believes a transaction is fraudulent or erroneous:
1. Notify Finance and card issuer immediately (within 24 hours of discovery)
2. Card issuer handles chargeback and fraud investigation
3. Employee completes Incident Report per **Incident Response Runbook** if data security is involved (e.g., card skimming)

## 5. Card Security and Lost Cards

### 5.1 Card Safeguarding
- **Store securely**: Don't share card details; store card in secure wallet
- **Monitor statements**: Review monthly statement for unauthorized charges
- **PCI compliance**: Never write full card number on receipts or documentation; use last 4 digits

### 5.2 Lost or Stolen Card
1. **Notify card issuer immediately** (24-hour number on card back)
2. **Notify Finance** at finance@northwind.com and corporate-cards@northwind.com
3. **Affidavit**: File lost/stolen card affidavit with card issuer; company is not liable if reported promptly
4. **Replacement**: Finance orders replacement card within 5 business days

### 5.3 Card Compromise
If card details are compromised or you suspect fraudulent use:
1. Notify card issuer and Finance immediately
2. Finance cancels card and orders replacement
3. Employee reports to **Information Security** if PCI or data breach is suspected

## 6. Card Termination and Recovery

### 6.1 Voluntary Surrender
When an employee departs Northwind or no longer requires a card:
1. Employee returns physical card to Finance or HR
2. Finance cancels card with issuer
3. Final statement is reconciled and paid

### 6.2 Involuntary Termination
If employee is terminated or card is revoked:
- Finance immediately cancels card with issuer
- Final statement is reconciled
- Employee is liable for any unauthorized charges after termination date

## 7. Violations and Enforcement

### 7.1 Misuse Examples
- Expensing personal items (gym, entertainment, clothing)
- Using card after termination
- Falsifying receipt or business purpose
- Duplicate reimbursement (card + personal card claim)
- Exceeding monthly or single-transaction limits

### 7.2 Disciplinary Escalation
- **First violation**: Verbal warning; expense not reimbursed; card remains active
- **Second violation**: Written warning; card suspended for 30 days
- **Third violation**: Card canceled; employee escalated to HR; may result in termination for cause

## 8. Accounting and Reconciliation

Finance maintains all corporate card statements and reconciliation records for 7 years (IRS requirement). Transactions are coded to appropriate general ledger accounts (travel, entertainment, subscriptions, equipment, etc.) for monthly financial reporting.

Cards are never used for personal loans or cash equivalents, and all transactions must comply with IRS Section 162 (ordinary and necessary business expense).
