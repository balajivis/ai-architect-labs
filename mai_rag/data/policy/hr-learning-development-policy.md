# Learning & Development Policy

## 1. Purpose

Northwind Technologies is committed to continuous learning and employee growth. This policy outlines the learning and development opportunities available to all employees, funding mechanisms, and approval processes.

## 2. Learning Budget & Allocation

**2.1 Annual Learning Budget**:
| Function | Budget | Frequency |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| **Engineering (all levels)** | $2,500 | Per calendar year |
| **Product & Design** | $2,000 | Per calendar year |
| **Sales & Sales Engineering** | $2,000 | Per calendar year |
| **Finance, HR, Legal** | $1,500 | Per calendar year |
| **Other functions** | $1,000 | Per calendar year |

Budget is allocated per employee (non-cumulative; unused budget does not roll over).

**2.2 Types of Learning Covered**:
- Conferences and workshops (full-day or multi-day)
- Online courses and certifications (Coursera, Udacity, Pluralsight, etc.)
- Bootcamps and intensive programs
- Books and publications (up to $100/month)
- Training subscriptions (LinkedIn Learning, etc.)

**2.3 Out-of-Pocket Reimbursement**:
- Employees may pay for learning upfront and request reimbursement (preferred: submit for pre-approval)
- Reimbursement is requested via Expensify and must be submitted within 60 days of completion
- Reimbursement includes tuition only; travel and meals are covered separately if pre-approved

## 3. Professional Certifications

**3.1** Technical certifications (AWS, GCP, Kubernetes, CPA, etc.) directly improve job performance and are prioritized:
- **Exam fees**: 100% reimbursed (covered by learning budget)
- **Study materials** (courses, books, bootcamps): covered by learning budget
- **Exam retakes**: covered if first attempt is reasonable (e.g., not >2 retakes)
- **Exam pass incentive**: employees who pass certain high-value certifications (AWS Solutions Architect, CPA, CISSP) receive a $500 bonus (in addition to reimbursement)

**3.2** Certification maintenance:
- Recurring certification fees (renewals every 2–3 years) are covered in full
- Time off for exam prep (up to 1 week per year) may be approved by manager

## 4. Tuition Reimbursement (Higher Education)

**4.1 Eligibility**:
- Full-time employees with ≥6 months tenure are eligible
- Part-time employees may be eligible on pro-rata basis (check with HR)

**4.2 Approved Programs**:
- Bachelor's or Master's degree programs
- Professional certifications (MBA, CISSP, CPA, etc.)
- Bootcamps (software engineering, data science, product management, etc.)
- Job-related degree programs only (not hobby or personal enrichment)

**4.3 Reimbursement Terms**:
- **Amount**: up to $5,000 per calendar year; lifetime cap $25,000
- **Grade requirement**: minimum B (or equivalent) required; reimbursement is forfeited if less
- **Payback agreement**: 24-month employment commitment required after program completion; if employee leaves within 24 months, prorated repayment is due (25% per quarter worked)

**4.4 Process**:
1. Employee submits program details to HR for pre-approval (prior to enrollment)
2. HR reviews: is program job-relevant? Is employee eligible?
3. Manager approves (via Workday)
4. Employee enrolls and pays (if covering upfront) or provides billing info for direct payment
5. Upon completion, employee submits proof of completion (transcript, certificate) to HR
6. Reimbursement is processed within 30 days

## 5. Conference & Workshop Attendance

**5.1 Selection Criteria**:
- **Business relevance**: conference content aligns with job or career goal
- **Presenters/content**: reputable speakers; strong learning outcomes expected
- **Cost-effectiveness**: ROI is reasonable (e.g., $1,500 for 3-day conf vs. $5,000 for 5-day luxury event)
- **Frequency**: employee has not attended a conference within the last 12 months (unless special circumstances)

**5.2 Approval Process**:
1. Employee requests pre-approval from manager (2 months in advance if possible)
2. Manager reviews and approves in Workday
3. HR verifies budget availability
4. Attendee is marked in RSVP; registration is paid directly or reimbursed
5. Post-conference: employee submits brief summary (1 page) of learnings to team

**5.3 Coverage & Expenses**:
- Conference registration: covered from learning budget
- Travel (airfare, hotel, ground transport): covered from business travel budget (separate from learning budget)
- Meals, incidentals: per diem (see Expense Policy)
- Alcohol, entertainment: not reimbursed unless part of official conference event

**5.4 Attendance & Work**:
- Conference days are paid company time (not charged to PTO)
- Attending day 1–3 of a 4-day conference? Days attended are paid; day 4 (personal) may be charged to PTO or taken unpaid (manager flexibility)

## 6. Internal Learning Opportunities

**6.1 Brown Bag Sessions**:
- Employees present learnings on any work-related topic
- Scheduled during lunch (1 hour); lunch is provided
- Open to all employees; attendance is optional but encouraged
- At least 2 per month are scheduled (organized by HR or employee volunteers)

**6.2 Skill-Building Groups**:
- Cross-functional learning pods (e.g., "Kubernetes Fundamentals," "Product Strategy 101")
- Meet bi-weekly for 1 hour
- Led by internal expert (volunteer or assigned)
- Flexible attendance; recorded sessions available

**6.3 Mentorship Program**:
- Formal mentor matching: new hires and high-potential employees are matched with mentors
- Mentors receive training; 1:1 cadence is monthly (1 hour)
- Mentorship pairs are assigned for 6–12 months
- Mentors are recognized in annual review (counts toward performance evaluation)

**6.4 Lunch & Learns**:
- External speakers (industry leaders, recruiters, diversity advocates) present during lunch
- At least 1 per month; topics vary (careers, industry trends, personal development)
- Recorded and shared for asynchronous viewing

## 7. Manager Responsibilities

**7.1** Managers are responsible for:
- Discussing learning goals with direct reports during 1:1s and performance reviews
- Approving learning requests in a timely manner (within 1 week)
- Supporting attendance (no "surprise" meeting scheduling during conference days)
- Requesting post-conference debrief (1:1 or team meeting)
- Advocating for skill development (e.g., "You should attend the AWS summit in November")

**7.2** Managers receive training on coaching and development (see Performance Review & Feedback Process, section 2.6).

## 8. Engineering & Technical Learning

**8.1 Code Review & Pair Programming**:
- Code review is a learning opportunity; reviewers provide constructive feedback
- Pair programming sessions (senior + junior engineer) are scheduled weekly for knowledge transfer
- Reverse pairing (junior teaches senior) is encouraged

**8.2 Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)**:
- Engineers document complex design decisions
- Peer review of ADRs is a learning opportunity for the team
- ADRs are stored in GitHub (see governance standard in COMPANY_BIBLE.md)

**8.3 Internal Tech Talks**:
- Engineers present on new technologies, design patterns, or lessons learned
- Monthly tech talks (30 minutes) are scheduled
- Attendance is optional but encouraged for relevant engineers

## 9. Sales & Customer Success Learning

**9.1 Product Training**:
- New salespeople receive 2-week onboarding product training (see Employee Onboarding Guide)
- Quarterly product updates are delivered by Product team
- Sales engineers co-present at customer calls for knowledge transfer

**9.2 Sales Methodology**:
- Northwind endorses a sales methodology (e.g., Sandler, MEDDIC, or internal framework)
- New salespeople receive full training (2–3 days)
- Refresher training is offered annually (1 day)

**9.3 Customer Success Certifications**:
- CS team members are encouraged to pursue CCXP (Customer Experience Professional) certification
- Tuition reimbursement and exam prep time are provided

## 10. Accessibility & Inclusive Learning

**10.1** All learning opportunities are:
- Accessible to people with disabilities (ASL interpreters, captions, screen readers)
- Offered both in-person and remote when possible
- Inclusive of working parents (scheduling flexibility, childcare support may be available)

**10.2 Accommodations**:
- Employees may request accommodations for learning (extended exam time, alternative format, etc.)
- Contact HR and the learning provider; accommodations are provided without cost to employee

## 11. Measurement & Outcomes

**11.1** Learning is measured by:
- Budget utilization (% of allocated budget used per department)
- Certification rates (% of engineers with AWS certification, etc.)
- Advancement outcomes (% of sponsored employees who are promoted within 3 years)
- Engagement (survey questions on learning satisfaction and opportunity)
- Internal mobility (% of job openings filled by internal candidates, suggesting strong development)

**11.2** Managers provide feedback:
- Post-learning, managers assess: did learnings translate to job improvements? Any behavior changes observed?
- If not, manager discusses with employee how to apply learning
- Learning failure is not held against employee; the goal is growth

## 12. LinkedIn Learning & SkillUp

**12.1** All employees have complimentary access to LinkedIn Learning:
- Enroll via HR portal (onboarding email)
- Browse 16,000+ courses (business, tech, soft skills)
- Courses range from 5 minutes to 3+ hours
- Certificates of completion are available

**12.2 SkillUp** (Internal Learning LMS):
- Northwind maintains a learning management system (Workday Learning)
- Mandatory courses are assigned (e.g., security training, code of conduct)
- Optional courses are available by function (product discovery, data analysis, etc.)

## 13. Learning Leave & Sabbaticals

**13.1 Learning Leave**:
- Employees with ≥5 years tenure may request extended learning leave (unpaid, 1–3 months)
- Examples: bootcamp, language immersion, academic research
- Requires manager + VP approval
- Job security: employee's position is held; return date is specified in approval

**13.2 Sabbatical Program** (in pilot):
- Employees with ≥10 years tenure may be eligible for 3-month paid sabbatical (once per 10 years)
- Sabbatical must include a learning component (course, project, writing, etc.)
- Details and approval process are managed by People Operations
