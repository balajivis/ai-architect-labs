# GDPR Compliance Policy

## 1. Scope and Applicability

Northwind Technologies processes personal data of residents of the European Union (EU) and European Economic Area (EEA) through its Northwind Cloud platform and related services. This policy establishes mandatory compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for all employees, contractors, and authorized third parties.

**Applicability**: This policy applies whenever Northwind collects, processes, stores, or transfers personal data of any EU/EEA resident, regardless of where Northwind's systems are physically located.

## 2. Legal Basis for Processing

All personal data processing must be grounded in one of six lawful bases under GDPR Article 6:

1. **Consent** – The data subject has explicitly opted in (e.g., newsletter signup, feature analytics). Consent must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous.
2. **Contract** – Processing is necessary to fulfill a service agreement with the customer or employee.
3. **Legal Obligation** – Processing is required by EU or member-state law (e.g., tax records, financial reporting).
4. **Vital Interests** – Processing protects life-or-death interests of the data subject (rare; emergency response only).
5. **Public Task** – Processing is necessary for Northwind's public or official function (does not apply to private B2B SaaS).
6. **Legitimate Interest** – Northwind has a documented, proportionate business need that does not override the data subject's rights. A Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) must be completed and retained.

**Requirement**: Every data collection point and processing activity must document which basis applies. When in doubt, default to **Consent** or **Contract**.

## 3. Data Subject Rights

Under GDPR Articles 12–22, any EU/EEA resident has the following rights, which Northwind must honor within **30 days** (extendable by 60 days for complex requests):

### 3.1 Right of Access
The data subject may request a copy of all personal data Northwind holds about them, including sources, recipients, and retention timelines. Northwind must provide this free of charge.

### 3.2 Right of Rectification
The data subject may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data. Northwind must update records within 30 days and notify recipients where feasible.

### 3.3 Right of Erasure ("Right to Be Forgotten")
The data subject may request deletion of their personal data if: (a) it is no longer necessary for the original purpose; (b) they withdraw consent; (c) they object to processing; or (d) processing is unlawful. Northwind must delete records within 30 days **except** where legal obligations, contracts, or the **Data Classification & Retention Policy** require longer retention.

### 3.4 Right to Restrict Processing
The data subject may request that Northwind limit how it uses their data (e.g., "do not share with third parties, but you may store it"). Northwind must restrict processing and notify recipients.

### 3.5 Right to Data Portability
The data subject may request their personal data in a **structured, commonly used, machine-readable format** (e.g., CSV, JSON) suitable for transfer to a competing service. Northwind must provide this within 30 days at no charge.

### 3.6 Right to Object
The data subject may object to processing for marketing, profiling, or other purposes. Northwind must cease processing within 30 days unless it can demonstrate a compelling legitimate interest that overrides the objection.

### 3.7 Right Related to Automated Decision-Making
If Northwind makes a decision that significantly affects a data subject using only automated processing (e.g., an algorithm denying service), the data subject has the right to human review and appeal.

## 4. Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)

Before launching any new processing activity that is **high-risk** (e.g., automated decision-making, large-scale sensitive data processing, behavioral tracking), Northwind must conduct a **Data Protection Impact Assessment** documented in the **Privacy Impact Assessment Process**.

## 5. Data Processing Agreements (DPA)

Whenever Northwind engages a **Data Processor** (a third party that processes EU/EEA personal data on Northwind's behalf), a **Data Processing Agreement** compliant with GDPR Article 28 must be in place. See the **Data Processing Agreement (DPA) Standard** for requirements.

## 6. Cross-Border Data Transfers

Northwind's primary cloud infrastructure is in the United States (AWS regions: us-east-1, us-west-2). Transfers of EU/EEA personal data to the US require a legal mechanism. Current mechanism: **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)** as approved by the European Commission. See the **Cross-Border Data Transfer Policy** for additional safeguards.

## 7. Data Breach Notification

If a data breach involving EU/EEA personal data is confirmed, Northwind must:
- **Notify the relevant Data Protection Authority (DPA)** within **72 hours** of discovery (unless a risk assessment determines the breach poses no risk to rights/freedoms).
- **Notify affected data subjects** without undue delay if the breach poses high risk.
- **Document the breach** in the Breach Register (maintained by Chief Privacy Officer).

Details: See the **Data Breach Notification Procedure**.

## 8. Compliance Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|-----------------|
| **Chief Privacy Officer** | Policy owner; maintains Breach Register; approves Data Protection Impact Assessments |
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | Acts as primary contact for EU DPAs; monitors compliance; investigates data subject requests |
| **Department Heads** | Assign data owners; ensure teams understand lawful basis and retention requirements |
| **All Employees** | Complete GDPR training annually; report suspected breaches within 1 hour |

## 9. Annual Review and Audit

The Chief Privacy Officer audits GDPR compliance annually and reports findings to the Board of Directors. Non-compliance may trigger regulatory fines up to EUR 20 million or 4% of annual global revenue, whichever is higher.
