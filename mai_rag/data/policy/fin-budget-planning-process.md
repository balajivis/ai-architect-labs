# Annual Budget Planning Process

## 1. Purpose and Overview

This policy establishes the process for annual budget planning at Northwind Technologies. The budget planning cycle runs from July to September each year for the following fiscal year (January–December). All departments submit budget requests aligned with company strategic objectives; Finance consolidates and CFO presents to the Board for approval by November 30.

## 2. Budget Planning Timeline

| Date | Milestone | Owner |
|------|-----------|-------|
| July 1 | Finance releases budget templates and guidelines | CFO |
| July 15 | Department heads submit initial budget requests | Department Leads |
| August 1–15 | Finance and department heads align (1:1s, reviews) | CFO + Department Leads |
| August 20 | Department heads submit final budgets | Department Leads |
| September 1–15 | Finance consolidates; identifies gaps and risks | Finance Team |
| September 20 | CFO presents preliminary budget to CEO and Board | CFO |
| October 1–15 | Board reviews; requests revisions or approvals | Board of Directors |
| October 30 | Final budget approved by Board | Board of Directors |
| November 15 | Finance publishes approved budget by department | Finance |
| December 31 | Q4 budget vs. actual reconciliation | Finance Controller |

## 3. Budget Submission Requirements

### 3.1 Department Budget Request Contents

Each department submits a budget request that includes:

**Section A: Revenue and Headcount**
- Planned hiring (role, title, level, start date, salary range)
- Planned departures or reductions
- FTE (full-time equivalent) count by role level
- Salary and benefits cost per role (benefits = 28% of salary for allocation)

**Section B: Operating Expenses**
- Salaries and benefits (including contractors, consultants)
- Software and SaaS subscriptions (tools, licenses)
- Equipment and hardware
- Travel and training
- Professional services (legal, audit, contractors)
- Facilities and office costs (rent, utilities, furniture)
- Other departmental expenses

**Section C: Capital Expenditures (CapEx)**
- Any purchases over $50,000 (single item or project)
- Asset category (infrastructure, research, product development)
- Expected useful life and depreciation
- ROI justification (if applicable)

See **Capital Expenditure Policy** for CapEx approval thresholds.

**Section D: Justification Narrative**
- Business objectives this budget supports
- Key initiatives and expected outcomes
- Risk assessment (challenges, dependencies, mitigation)

### 3.2 Budget Constraints and Guidelines

**Inflation adjustment**: Finance provides inflation factor (typically 2–4% per prior year); base budgets start with inflation adjustment

**Headcount growth**: Hiring plans must align with overall company growth targets; CEO sets company-wide headcount ceiling

**Travel budget**: See **Expense & Travel Policy** for tier limits; individual travel budgets roll up to department

**Discretionary spending**: Non-essential travel, entertainment, and tools subject to Board approval if over departmental allocation

## 4. Budget Consolidation and Approval

### 4.1 Department-Level Review
Finance meets with each department head (1:1) to:
- Validate assumptions (salary increases, inflation, headcount growth)
- Challenge items not aligned with company strategy
- Identify inter-departmental dependencies (e.g., shared infrastructure costs)
- Discuss risk mitigation (contingency reserves)

### 4.2 Cross-Departmental Alignment
Finance identifies shared costs (cloud infrastructure, software licenses, office rent) and allocates to departments using agreed allocation bases (headcount, usage, department assignment).

**Common shared costs:**
- AWS and Azure cloud infrastructure (allocated by cost tag / usage)
- GitHub, Jira, Slack, and developer tools (allocated by headcount)
- Office space and utilities (allocated by headcount)
- Cyber insurance (allocated by headcount)

### 4.3 Company-Level Consolidation
CFO consolidates all departmental budgets into:
- Total operating budget (personnel + operating expenses)
- Total capital budget (CapEx)
- Revenue assumptions (sales pipeline, product pricing, churn)
- Profitability forecast for the year

CFO presents consolidated budget to Board with variance analysis against prior year and strategic priorities.

## 5. Budget Variance and Monitoring

### 5.1 Monthly Variance Report
Finance publishes monthly budget vs. actual report by department:
- **Actual spending** vs. budgeted amount
- **Variance percentage** (over/under budget)
- **Explanation** for variances > 10%

Department heads review and provide explanations; Finance escalates concerns to CFO.

### 5.2 Mid-Year Review (June)
At the 6-month mark, Finance and CFO conduct mid-year review:
- Are we on track to meet annual targets?
- Do we need to adjust headcount or spending due to market changes?
- Should we reallocate budget between departments?

Any significant reforecasting is approved by CEO and Board.

### 5.3 Year-End Reconciliation
At December 31, Finance closes out the fiscal year:
- Actual spending reconciled against budget for all departments
- Unused budget does not roll over to next year (hard close)
- Variance analysis presented to Board in January

## 6. Capital Expenditure (CapEx) in Budget Planning

Major capital projects are budgeted separately from operating expenses. Detailed procedures are in **Capital Expenditure Policy**; budget planning includes:

- **Expected CapEx**: Large IT infrastructure projects, R&D equipment, office renovations
- **Depreciation impact**: CapEx projects depreciate over useful life; annual depreciation affects operating budget in future years
- **ROI threshold**: CapEx projects over $100,000 require documented ROI business case

All CapEx requires Board approval if >$250,000 (per **Capital Expenditure Policy**).

## 7. Emergency Budget Adjustments

If unforeseen circumstances require budget adjustments during the year:

**Mid-year changes under $100,000**: CFO approves; reported in monthly variance report

**Changes $100,000 to $500,000**: CEO approval required; Board notified in next meeting

**Changes over $500,000**: Board approval required; may require emergency Board meeting

Examples: Major security incident requiring forensics, unplanned acquisition, significant market shift.

## 8. Budget Accountability

Department heads are accountable for:
- Staying within their approved budget (within 10% variance tolerance)
- Explaining variances over 10% to Finance
- Hiring within approved headcount plan
- Not committing to expenses without prior CapEx or budget allocation

Finance monitors spending and alerts department heads monthly. Departments that consistently exceed budget without approval may face spending freezes or headcount restrictions in the following year.
