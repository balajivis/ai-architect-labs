# NDA Policy

## Purpose
This policy establishes guidelines for negotiating, executing, and managing Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) on behalf of Northwind Technologies. NDAs protect Northwind's proprietary information and ensure clarity on confidentiality obligations across partnerships, customer relationships, and vendor engagements.

## 1. Types of NDAs

**Mutual NDA** — Both parties commit to keeping confidential information secret. Used for partnerships, joint evaluations, and strategic discussions.

**Unilateral NDA (Northwind receives)** — Vendor or partner discloses to Northwind; Northwind agrees to keep it confidential. Used for vendor proposals, customer case studies, and technical evaluations.

**Unilateral NDA (Northwind discloses)** — Northwind discloses; counterparty agrees to keep it confidential. Used for sales demonstrations, architecture briefings, and partnership proposals.

## 2. Standard Definitions

All Northwind NDAs incorporate the following definitions:

**"Confidential Information"** — Information marked as confidential, or identified as such at disclosure, including but not limited to:
- Business plans, financial data, customer lists, pricing
- Software code, architecture, algorithms, and designs
- Specifications, trade secrets, and know-how
- Internal documentation and communications
- Data classified as **Confidential** or **Restricted** per the **Data Classification & Retention Policy**

**"Permitted Disclosures"** — Exceptions to confidentiality:
- Legally required disclosure (court order, subpoena) with 10-day advance notice to the disclosing party to seek protective order
- Disclosure to employees, contractors, advisors with a need-to-know on a need-to-know basis, under written confidentiality obligations
- Publicly available information through no breach of this NDA
- Information independently developed without reference to Confidential Information

## 3. Standard Term & Scope

| Element | Standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Confidentiality Duration** | 3 years post-termination | Applies to information disclosed during the relationship and for 3 years after |
| **Trade Secrets Exception** | No expiration | Trade secrets remain protected indefinitely per applicable law |
| **Disclosure Timeframe** | Upon marked or identified as confidential | Or within 5 business days if retroactively identified |
| **Permitted Recipients** | Employees, contractors, legal counsel, auditors | Must be under written confidentiality obligations |
| **Return/Destruction** | 30 days post-termination | Disclosing party may require certification of destruction |
| **No License Grant** | Automatic | NDA does not grant license to use Confidential Information |
| **No Obligation to Disclose** | Automatic | Neither party is obligated to disclose any information |

## 4. Approval Authority for NDAs

| Scenario | Approver(s) | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Standard mutual NDA, any party | VP Sales + General Counsel | 2 business days |
| Unilateral (Northwind discloses < $1M potential exposure) | VP Sales + General Counsel | 1 business day |
| Unilateral (Northwind discloses > $1M potential exposure) | General Counsel + CEO | 2 business days |
| Customer/partner requests broad use of Northwind IP | General Counsel + VP Sales | 3 business days |
| Any NDA involving **Restricted** data | General Counsel + Chief Privacy Officer | 3 business days |
| NDA superseding standard mutual language | General Counsel + CEO | 5 business days |

## 5. Prohibited Provisions

The following provisions are **not acceptable** without escalated approval:

1. **Perpetual confidentiality** — Cap at 3 years post-termination except for trade secrets
2. **Blanket non-solicitation** — No restriction on hiring beyond current employees without specific consent
3. **Non-compete** — Not permissible in an NDA; must be separate employment agreement
4. **Broad indemnification** — Northwind will not indemnify counterparty for counterparty's own disclosure of Confidential Information
5. **Liability waivers** — Northwind will not waive liability for willful breach or gross negligence
6. **Governing law outside Texas** — Any exception requires CEO approval and must be arbitration in Texas or another mutually agreed neutral location
7. **Audit rights on demand** — Must be limited to reasonable frequency (annually or less) and reasonable notice (15+ days)

## 6. Northwind-Standard Mutual NDA

All mutual NDAs should conform to the template in Northwind's Legal portal. Key provisions:
- **Confidentiality duration**: 3 years post-termination
- **Trade secret carve-out**: Perpetual for trade secrets
- **Permitted disclosures**: Employees, contractors, legal counsel, auditors (with written obligations)
- **No license grant**: NDA does not grant any IP license
- **Return/destruction**: 30 days post-termination
- **Governing law & arbitration**: State of Texas; arbitration in Austin, TX
- **Entire agreement**: NDA supersedes prior discussions

## 7. Multi-Party & Consortium Agreements

If Northwind is party to a consortium NDA with multiple partners (joint ventures, industry groups), the General Counsel must:
1. Verify Northwind's interests are aligned with consortium mission
2. Ensure **Data Classification & Retention Policy** requirements are met for any shared data
3. Confirm that IP created by Northwind's employees retains Northwind ownership
4. Document consortium governance and exit terms

## 8. Breach & Remedies

- **Notice** — Suspected breach must be reported to Legal within 5 business days
- **Investigation** — Legal investigates severity and scope
- **Escalation** — Material breach (disclosure to third parties, competitive harm) is escalated to CEO and General Counsel
- **Remedies** — Northwind may seek injunctive relief, damages, or both via arbitration per the NDA terms
- **Notification** — Affected customers or partners are notified per **Incident Response Runbook** if Northwind Confidential data is exposed
