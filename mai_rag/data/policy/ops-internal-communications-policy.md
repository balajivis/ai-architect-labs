# Internal Communications Policy

## 1. Purpose & Scope

This policy establishes standards and channels for internal communications at Northwind Technologies, ensuring clarity, consistency, timeliness, and trust. It covers routine updates, urgent announcements, company events, and crisis communications.

**Scope**: All employees, contractors, managers, and communication channels (email, Slack, in-person, town halls).

**Objective**: Build a culture of transparency, ensure critical information reaches all employees, and maintain an informed, engaged workforce.

## 2. Communication Channels & Usage Guidelines

| Channel | Purpose | Audience | Frequency | Owner |
|---------|---------|----------|-----------|-------|
| **Slack** | Routine updates, team coordination, quick announcements | All employees in relevant workspace | Daily | Team leads / Comms |
| **All-hands email** | Critical announcements, policy changes, company milestones | All employees (required read) | Monthly or as needed | CEO / VP People |
| **Town hall (Zoom/in-person)** | Strategic updates, Q&A, celebration, culture-building | All employees (encouraged attendance) | Monthly (in-person: Q1, Q3) | CEO / VP People |
| **Department meetings** | Functional updates, OKRs, feedback | Department only | Weekly or bi-weekly | Department VP |
| **Leadership standup** | Executive alignment, cross-functional sync | VP+ only | Daily (15 min) | CEO / COO |
| **Wiki / Handbook** | Policy references, runbooks, evergreen documentation | All employees (self-serve) | Updated as-needed | Owners of each policy |
| **All-hands bulletin board** (HQ) | Facilities notices, safety alerts, upcoming events | On-site employees | Weekly | Facilities / HR |
| **Intranet / Dashboard** | Organizational news feed, benefits, announcements | All employees | Daily | Communications team |

## 3. Tone & Language

**Company voice**: Professional yet conversational. Avoid:
- Jargon unless explained
- Blame; use "we"; blameless problem-solving
- Euphemisms; call things by their name (e.g., "layoff" not "rightsizing"; "breach" not "security incident")
- All-caps (except headers); come across as shouting

**Required elements** in major announcements:
1. **What**: Clear statement of fact or decision
2. **Why**: Rationale (business, safety, compliance, values)
3. **Impact**: How this affects employees, customers, or operations
4. **Next steps**: What happens now; when; who to contact with questions

## 4. Types of Communications

### 4.1 Routine Updates (Weekly–Monthly)
**Examples**: New hire announcements, office closures, policy clarifications, product launches

**Process**:
1. Draft message; ensure factual accuracy
2. Identify appropriate channel (Slack, email, town hall)
3. Schedule for off-peak times (not Friday afternoon; not holiday week)
4. Post; monitor for questions; respond in 4 hours
5. Archive in wiki for reference (if policy-relevant)

**Escalation**: If >10 follow-up questions or confusion, VP People clarifies in all-hands email

### 4.2 Executive Updates (Monthly All-Hands Email)
**Examples**: Quarterly OKR results, CEO reflections, company performance, culture initiatives, personnel changes (at appropriate level of detail)

**Process**:
1. CEO or VP People drafts message (500–1,000 words)
2. Review: CFO (financial accuracy), General Counsel (legal/compliance), other execs (correctness)
3. Schedule: 1st Monday of month, 9 AM Austin time
4. Delivery: All-hands email list (managed by HR); Slack repost with archive link
5. Optional: CEO records 3-min video summary; posted in intranet

**Tone**: Honest (celebrate wins; acknowledge challenges); forward-looking; motivational but grounded

### 4.3 Crisis Communications
See Crisis Management Plan and Emergency Response Plan for detailed protocols. **Key principles**:
1. **Speed**: First message within 30 minutes of Level-1 incident
2. **Honesty**: State what you know; acknowledge unknowns
3. **Frequency**: Updates every 30–60 min during active incident; every 4 hours during recovery
4. **Channels**: All-hands email + Slack #incident-declared + on-screen alerts (if applicable)
5. **Spokesperson**: CEO (Level-1); VP of affected function (Level-2); functional manager (Level-3)

**Example crisis message**:
```
SUBJECT: [Incident Update] Service availability issue — Austin HQ impacted

Northwind is experiencing connectivity issues affecting office employees 
beginning 2 PM CT. Our team is investigating root cause. Remote employees 
are unaffected. We will update you every 60 minutes. Questions: #incident-slack-channel.
```

### 4.4 Personnel Announcements
**New hire**:
1. HR sends announcement to team (introduce name, role, start date, interests)
2. Hiring manager sends welcome message
3. Team organizes coffee chats / lunch (icebreaker)
4. Day 1 buddy assigned (peer mentor)

**Promotion**:
1. VP approves; HR schedules notification with employee (before announcement)
2. Announcement in department standup (manager) + all-hands email if >director level
3. New org chart updated in wiki
4. Slack reaction thread encourages congrats

**Termination / Layoff**:
1. **Timing**: Employees notified individually by manager before broad announcement
2. **Sensitivity**: No speculation; only approved facts stated
3. **Transparency**: CEO provides business rationale (e.g., restructuring, market change) + support offered (severance, outplacement, benefits extension)
4. **Escalation**: General Counsel reviews for legal/compliance; VP People coordinates benefits/transition
5. **See Crisis Management Plan** if mass layoff (Level-2 event)

### 4.5 Policy & Process Changes
**Examples**: New remote-work policy, updated benefits, security mandate

**Process**:
1. Draft: Owner (HR, IT Security, etc.) writes policy in wiki
2. Stakeholder review: 7-day review period; feedback incorporated
3. Announcement: Email + wiki link + optional Q&A session (post in Slack for async participation)
4. Training: If behavior change required, schedule training or link documentation
5. Enforcement: Set effective date; communicate transition expectations
6. See Information Security Policy and other domain-specific policies for required training/certification

### 4.6 Company Events (Town Halls, Offsites, Celebrations)
**Town halls** (monthly, 1 hour):
- CEO: 10 min opening + updates
- Functional VPs: 20 min (rotating: Engineering one month, Product next month, etc.)
- Q&A: 20 min; questions submitted via Google Form (anonymity option); CEO answers 5–10
- Recording: Posted to intranet 24 hours later (not real-time; async watchers)

**Offsites / Celebrations**:
- **All-hands offsite**: Annually (Q2 or Q3, 2 days); location TBD; optional attendance (remote option provided)
- **Department retreats**: Optional; manager coordinates; budget pre-approved by VP
- **Celebrations** (product launch, milestone): Ad-hoc; announced with date, time, location, RSVP process
- **Communications**: Invite email + Slack reminder; attendance tracked (optional)

## 5. Sensitive Topics & Escalation

| Topic | Approval Required | Escalation Path |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Layoff / RIF** | CEO + CFO | General Counsel; Board notification if >10% headcount |
| **Acquisition / Investment** | Board + CEO | Board approval; legal review; restricted communications until public |
| **Security breach** | CEO + VP Security + General Counsel | Incident Response Runbook; Customer notification; Regulatory (GDPR timeline) |
| **Legal dispute** | General Counsel + CEO | Confidential; no employee communication without legal review |
| **Executive changes** | Board + CEO | Board approval; General Counsel review (regulatory); announcement within 24h |
| **Suicide / Fatality** | HR + CEO + Counsel | Trauma support; employee counseling; limited factual statement only |

For sensitive topics, **coordinate with General Counsel before any employee communication**.

## 6. Feedback & Iteration

### 6.1 Pulse Surveys
- **Frequency**: Quarterly (2-min surveys via Slack; 5–10 questions)
- **Topics**: Communication effectiveness, trust in leadership, clarity of strategy
- **Analysis**: VP People shares aggregate results (anonymized); action items posted
- **Example question**: "I understand our company strategy and how my role supports it" (Likert scale 1–5)

### 6.2 Anonymous Feedback Channel
- **Mechanism**: Email to transparency@northwind.com (monitored by CEO + VP People)
- **Response**: Acknowledgment within 48 hours; resolution communicated to full company if relevant
- **Use case**: Concerns about miscommunication, transparency gaps, leadership credibility

### 6.3 Communication Retrospectives
- After major announcement or crisis event, conduct 30-min retrospective:
  - What worked? (timing, channel, tone)
  - What didn't? (gaps, confusion, slow response)
  - Improve next time? (process, training, template)

## 7. Accessibility & Inclusivity

- **Language**: Use plain English; avoid idioms and culture-specific references
- **Format**: Email + Slack + wiki; accommodate different reading preferences
- **Captions**: All town-hall videos captioned (automated + human review)
- **Translation**: For non-English-speaking employees (if >5% of population), key announcements translated
- **Neurodiversity**: Reduce cognitive load; summaries before detail; TL;DRs provided

## 8. Archival & Reference

- **Wiki**: All policies, evergreen communications stored in searchable wiki (GitHub Pages or Confluence)
- **Retention**: Keep all announcements for 2 years; older ones marked "archive"
- **Indexing**: Tag by topic (benefits, security, culture, etc.) for discoverability
- **Updates**: Policy owners mark "last updated" date; outdated info flagged with "superseded by [doc_id]"

## 9. Escalation: Ineffective or Contradictory Communications

If employees report confusion, contradictory messaging, or information gaps:
1. **Report**: Employee or manager emails transparency@northwind.com
2. **Triage**: VP People investigates within 5 business days
3. **Correction**: Clarification email sent if needed; root cause documented
4. **Process review**: If systemic issue (e.g., multiple departments sharing conflicting info), revise policy or add approval gate
