# Company Values & Strategy Overview

## 1. Purpose & Scope

This document articulates Northwind Technologies' foundational values, strategic direction, and principles that guide company decisions, culture, and employee behavior. It is the north star for all policy-making, hiring, and organizational design.

**Audience**: All employees, leadership, Board, and candidates.

**Intended effect**: Alignment across company on "why we exist" and "how we operate."

## 2. Mission, Vision & Values

### 2.1 Mission
**Northwind Technologies helps enterprises make sense of data, so they can compete in a changing world.**

We build data-integration software that democratizes access to insights. Our customers—from Fortune 500 to mid-market—use Northwind Cloud to unify data silos, automate analytics, and discover patterns that drive competitive advantage.

### 2.2 Vision (3–5 year)
**By 2029, Northwind Cloud will be the enterprise standard for data integration, trusted by 50% of Fortune 500 companies and 10,000+ mid-market businesses.**

### 2.3 Core Values

| Value | Definition | Behavioral Example |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Integrity** | Act with honesty, transparency, and accountability. Say what we mean; do what we say. | When a feature misses ship date, engineer tells customer truthfully (not with excuse); VP leads retrospective to prevent next time |
| **Customer obsession** | Relentlessly focus on solving customer problems, even when hard or costly. Customers' success = our success. | Support team stays late to unblock critical customer; product manager delays launch to fix usability gap |
| **Bias to action** | Move fast; iterate; learn from failure. Analysis-paralysis is the enemy. | Engineer ships feature flag to 5% of users to validate idea; collects feedback in 2 days |
| **Psychological safety** | Create environment where people speak up, challenge ideas, admit mistakes without fear. | Intern questions VP's strategy in meeting; VP says "great point"; team pivots. No retaliation. |
| **Excellence** | High standards; continuous improvement; do things well. Not perfectionism; done well beats done perfect. | Code reviewed for clarity + performance; design iterated on UX feedback; ops manual re-drafted for clarity every quarter |
| **Diversity & belonging** | We succeed when voices, perspectives, backgrounds, experiences are represented. Diverse teams make better decisions. | Hiring prioritizes diverse candidates; ERGs (engineering, women, LGBTQ+, BIPOC) meet monthly; leadership reads/reflects on inclusive leadership |

## 3. Strategic Pillars (2026–2030)

### Pillar 1: Product Excellence
- **Goal**: Northwind Cloud is 10x easier to integrate than competitors; ROI demonstrated in 30 days
- **Key initiatives**:
  - Simplify UX; reduce time-to-value (target: 48 hours setup; currently 2 weeks)
  - Add 50 new pre-built connectors (expand ecosystem)
  - AI-assisted data discovery & schema recommendation
- **Success metric**: NPS >70; customer churn <5% annual

### Pillar 2: Market Expansion
- **Goal**: Grow from $100M ARR (2025) to $500M ARR by 2030
- **Key initiatives**:
  - Vertical-specific packages (healthcare, financial services, retail)
  - International expansion (EU, APAC; 2027 target)
  - Mid-market segment growth (SMB partnerships)
  - Enterprise land-and-expand sales motion
- **Success metric**: $500M ARR; 50% YoY growth sustained through 2029

### Pillar 3: Operational Excellence & Scale
- **Goal**: Build infrastructure and processes to support 10x revenue growth while improving margins
- **Key initiatives**:
  - Hiring: 200 headcount (2025) to 800 (2030); focus on product, GTM, R&D
  - Technology: Microservices architecture; multi-region cloud deployment (HA/DR)
  - Systems: CRM, analytics, financial systems consolidated and automated
  - Culture: Maintain startup speed and autonomy while scaling rigor
- **Success metric**: Gross margin >75%; R&D headcount 40% of total

### Pillar 4: Trust & Governance
- **Goal**: Industry-leading security, compliance, and customer trust
- **Key initiatives**:
  - SOC 2 Type II certification (2026); ISO 27001 (2027)
  - GDPR & CCPA compliance; executive-level DPA review process
  - Transparent communication; blameless incident response; customer notification SLA <4 hours
  - Board oversight of security, compliance, and HR metrics
- **Success metric**: Zero material breaches; customer trust NPS (net trust score) >80

## 4. Principles Guiding Decision-Making

### 4.1 "Default to Transparency"
- Assume information should be public unless genuinely confidential (security, proprietary, GDPR-sensitive)
- Share quarterly results, OKRs, challenges openly in all-hands
- Admit mistakes; share lessons-learned in retrospectives
- **Exception**: Protect customer data, employee privacy, unannounced hires, legal/litigation strategy

### 4.2 "Customer Success Above Revenue"
- Short-term revenue never overrides long-term customer success
- If feature request harms customer, recommend alternative
- Offer refunds / credits if customer value not delivered (per contract)
- Build for customer use-cases, not for sales-team pressure
- **Example**: Sales pushes expensive feature customer doesn't need; Product declines; sales finds alternative

### 4.3 "Fail Fast; Learn Faster"
- Encourage experimentation; reasonable failures are learning opportunities (not grounds for blame)
- Small bets: ship feature flags, A/B tests, beta programs; collect data in days, not months
- Postmortems focus on process improvement, not punishment
- **Rule**: If same mistake happens twice, review process (hire, training, tool)

### 4.4 "Autonomy Within Guardrails"
- Teams have freedom to choose tools, architecture, and process
- Guardrails: Security, compliance, accessibility, financial prudence (capital spend approval), and code review standards
- VPs trust their reports; reports don't wait for permission to act (unless $$ or security implications)
- **Example**: Engineer can choose tech stack (Go vs. Python); cannot skip security review

### 4.5 "Diverse Perspectives Strengthen Decisions"
- Encourage respectful disagreement; best idea wins (not highest rank)
- Solicit input from frontline (support, customer success) before product decisions
- Build teams with different thinking styles; value introverts and extroverts equally
- Leadership reviews hiring diversity metrics quarterly; low representation flagged for action

## 5. How Values Show Up in Policies

| Policy | Aligned Values | How |
|--------|---|---|
| **Incident Response Runbook** | Integrity, Transparency | Rapid detection + customer notification (no cover-up); blameless post-mortem |
| **Information Security Policy** | Trust & Governance, Customer Obsession | Encryption, access controls, audits protect customer data; SOC 2 compliance |
| **Remote Access / VPN Guide** | Psychological Safety, Autonomy | Trust employees to work remotely; enforce security (MFA, encryption) without being intrusive |
| **Code Review Standards** | Excellence, Psychological Safety | High standards for code; safe space to ask questions; no blame |
| **Health & Safety Policy** | Integrity, Psychological Safety | Report incidents without retaliation; proactive safety culture |
| **Leave & Time-Off Policy** | Psychological Safety, Belonging | Generous PTO; burnout prevention; trust employees (not tracking hours) |
| **Expense Policy** | Integrity | Trust employees to expense honestly; simple reimbursement process (not penny-pinching) |

## 6. Anti-Values: What We Don't Do

- **Misrepresent** to customers or internally (no vanity metrics; real data only)
- **Sacrifice employee wellbeing** for growth; burnout is not a badge of honor
- **Discriminate** based on identity, background, or family status
- **Ignore safety** (physical or psychological); shortcuts on security for speed
- **Silently fail** (customers); transparency > surprises
- **Blame individuals** for systemic failures; fix the system

## 7. Leadership Accountability

The CEO, Board, and VP team are accountable for living these values:
- **Board**: Quarterly review of values alignment in key decisions (M&A, major policy change, incident response); escalate if deviation observed
- **CEO**: Models values in all-hands transparency, decision-making, and interpersonal interactions; holds VPs accountable
- **VPs**: Translate values into team norms; hire people aligned with values; address value misalignment in direct reports
- **All employees**: Call out value misalignment (peer or leadership) via manager, HR, or anonymous feedback channel (transparency@northwind.com)
