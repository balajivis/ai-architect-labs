# Procurement Thresholds & Competitive Bidding

## 1. Purpose and Overview

This policy establishes competitive bidding requirements for vendor selection and purchasing at Northwind Technologies. Competitive bidding ensures:
- **Best value** (lowest cost and acceptable quality)
- **Fairness** (multiple vendors evaluated equally)
- **Compliance** (audit trail for purchasing decisions)

**Related policy**: **Purchase Approval Matrix** defines *who* approves; this policy defines *how* to evaluate and *when* multiple quotes are required.

## 2. Procurement Thresholds and Bidding Requirements

### 2.1 Threshold Matrix

| Purchase Amount | Bidding Requirement | Min. Quotes | Evaluation Criteria | Documentation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **$0 – $999** | No competitive bid required | — | Manager discretion | Receipt/invoice |
| **$1,000 – $4,999** | Preferred: 1 quote (manager can approve single vendor) | 1 | Cost, quality, delivery | Procurement request + quote |
| **$5,000 – $24,999** | **Required: 2 quotes** | 2 | Cost, quality, delivery, support | 2 quotes + comparison matrix |
| **$25,000 – $99,999** | **Required: 3 quotes** | 3 | Cost, quality, delivery, support, vendor stability | 3 quotes + weighted evaluation |
| **$100,000 – $250,000** | **Formal RFP** (Request for Proposal) | 3+ | Comprehensive evaluation + vendor security review | RFP document + vendor presentations |
| **Over $250,000** | **Competitive RFP + Board approval** | 3+ | Strategic vendor partnerships; may involve trial/POC | Full due diligence + Board vote |

### 2.2 Sole-Vendor Justification

If only **one vendor** is capable of fulfilling the requirement (sole-source procurement):

1. **Document the justification** in Procurement system:
   - Why is this vendor the only option? (e.g., proprietary technology, exclusive license, unique expertise)
   - What efforts were made to find alternatives?
   - What is the risk of sole-source dependency?

2. **Approver review**: VP Procurement (or CFO if >$100K) approves sole-vendor waiver

3. **Approval authority**: Proceed with normal approval matrix (Finance, VP, CFO as required)

4. **Annual review**: Finance tracks sole-vendor purchases; works with department to reduce lock-in (find alternatives, negotiate favorable terms)

**Example**: Company standardizes on Salesforce CRM. Only Salesforce can provide the exact integration needed with internal systems. Sole-source justification: "Proprietary API integration specific to Northwind's architecture; no competitor offers equivalent functionality."

## 3. Competitive Bidding Process

### 3.1 Two-Quote Process ($5K–$24,999)

**Timeline**: 3–5 business days

1. **Requestor identifies** 2 potential vendors for the good/service
2. **Procurement team** (or manager) contacts vendors:
   - Email or phone: "We're evaluating vendors for [product/service]. Can you provide a quote by [date]?"
   - Include: Specifications, quantity, delivery date, support requirements
3. **Collect quotes**: Request written quotes (email, PDF, or vendor form)
4. **Comparison**: Document side-by-side comparison:
   - Unit price, total cost
   - Features or specifications (does each meet our needs?)
   - Delivery timeline
   - Support and warranty terms
5. **Recommendation**: Procurement recommends lowest cost if quality is equivalent; or explains if higher-cost vendor is preferred (e.g., better support, proven reliability)
6. **Manager approval**: Manager selects vendor and approves cost
7. **PO issued**: Procurement creates purchase order; vendor begins fulfillment

### 3.2 Three-Quote Process ($25K–$99,999)

**Timeline**: 1–2 weeks

1. **Procurement team** issues Request for Quote (RFQ):
   - Written document with detailed specifications
   - Required features and acceptance criteria
   - Evaluation criteria (cost, delivery, support, references)
   - Deadline for responses (typically 5–7 days)

2. **Vendor selection and outreach**:
   - Identify 3+ qualified vendors (can use vendor database, industry reputation, referrals)
   - Send RFQ to all; request written quote and any supporting materials

3. **Quote evaluation**:
   - **Lowest cost comparison**: Summarize total cost from each vendor
   - **Technical evaluation**: Does each vendor meet all specifications? Gaps?
   - **References**: Contact customer references; assess vendor reliability
   - **Payment terms**: Net 30, Net 60, early-pay discount?
   - **Scoring matrix** (weighted):
     - Cost: 40 points
     - Quality/compliance: 30 points
     - Delivery/timeline: 15 points
     - Support/warranty: 15 points
     - **Total**: Highest score is recommended

4. **Recommendation and approval**:
   - Procurement recommends vendor with highest score
   - If lowest-cost vendor is not selected, document justification (e.g., "Vendor B is $5K more but has 24/7 support and proven stability; worth premium")
   - VP (department) or CFO approves final selection

5. **Negotiation** (optional): Procurement may negotiate price with selected vendor (e.g., "Vendor A quoted $30K; can you match Vendor B's $28K?")

6. **PO issued**: Once approved, procurement creates and sends PO

### 3.3 RFP Process ($100K+)

**Timeline**: 2–4 weeks

1. **RFP document preparation**:
   - Business background and requirements (detailed)
   - Technical specifications and acceptance criteria
   - Evaluation criteria and weighting
   - Contract terms (payment, liability, SLAs, support levels)
   - Submission deadline and format

2. **Vendor outreach**:
   - Identify 3–5 qualified vendors
   - Send RFP to all; request formal proposal response
   - Optional: Pre-proposal meeting/webinar (allows vendors to ask clarifying questions)

3. **Proposal evaluation**:
   - Scoring matrix (cost, technical, support, vendor stability)
   - May include live vendor presentations (product demo, team introduction)
   - Reference checks and background research
   - **POC (Proof of Concept)**: For complex software, may require trial/pilot period

4. **Vendor selection**:
   - Highest-scoring vendor is recommended
   - CFO and VP (if departmental CapEx) present to CEO/Board for final approval
   - Board approval required if >$250K

5. **Negotiation and contract**:
   - Procurement and Legal negotiate final terms (pricing, SLAs, data processing, liability)
   - Contract approved by General Counsel and CFO
   - Contract signed by authorized signatory (CFO for <$1M; CEO for >$1M; Board Chairman for strategic partnerships >$5M)

6. **Onboarding**: Vendor is activated (see **Vendor Onboarding (Finance & Procurement)**)

## 4. Evaluation Scoring Matrices

### 4.1 Example Scoring Matrix ($25K–$99K Purchase)

| Criteria | Weight | Vendor A Score | Vendor B Score | Vendor C Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cost** | 40% | $30K (10/10) | $28K (9/10) | $35K (7/10) |
| **Quality/Compliance** | 30% | 9/10 | 8/10 | 9/10 |
| **Delivery/Timeline** | 15% | 8/10 (30 days) | 7/10 (45 days) | 9/10 (14 days) |
| **Support/Warranty** | 15% | 7/10 | 8/10 | 8/10 |
| **WEIGHTED TOTAL** | **100%** | **8.8/10** | **8.2/10** | **8.2/10** |

**Recommendation**: Vendor A (highest score, lowest cost)

### 4.2 Example Scoring Matrix ($100K+ RFP)

| Criteria | Weight | Score |
|---|---|---|
| Technical fit (does it solve our problem?) | 30% | 0–10 |
| Cost (total 3-year cost of ownership) | 25% | 0–10 |
| Vendor stability and reputation | 20% | 0–10 |
| Support and SLA commitments | 15% | 0–10 |
| Data security and compliance | 10% | 0–10 |
| **WEIGHTED TOTAL** | **100%** | **0–10** |

Scoring is often performed by a **selection committee** (e.g., CTO, VP Ops, VP Finance, General Counsel) who vote/score independently, then average scores.

## 5. Exceptions to Competitive Bidding

### 5.1 Emergency Purchases

**Emergency definition**: Business-critical need that cannot wait for normal procurement cycle (e.g., server failure, security incident requiring forensics).

**Process**:
1. **CFO or VP approval**: Verbal or email approval documenting emergency
2. **Procurement**: May approach single vendor if speed is critical
3. **Best-effort cost validation**: Confirm price is reasonable (call 2nd vendor if time allows)
4. **Documentation**: Retain email or memo explaining emergency

**Example**: Production database server fails; need replacement within 48 hours. Procurement calls primary vendor; if available, orders immediately without waiting for competitive bids.

### 5.2 Renewal Purchases

**Existing vendor renewals** (e.g., annual software license renewal, annual maintenance contract):

- **If price is ≤prior year + inflation (2–4%)**: Manager can approve renewal without competitive bid
- **If price increases >10% OR service significantly changes**: Procurement should re-bid (obtain competitive quotes)

**Example**: Salesforce CRM annual license $50K (prior year was $50K). 2% inflation accepted; no rebid required. But if vendor quotes $60K (20% increase), manager asks Procurement to get alternative quotes.

### 5.3 Strategic Partnerships

**Long-term vendor relationships** (preferred vendors with negotiated terms):

- Northwind may maintain a **preferred vendor list** for categories (e.g., office supplies, HR systems, travel booking)
- Preferred vendors are pre-qualified; lower approval thresholds may apply
- Periodically (every 2–3 years), preferred vendors compete for renewal

## 6. Vendor Management and Performance Tracking

### 6.1 Vendor Database

Procurement maintains a vendor database with:
- **Vendor name, contact, location**
- **Category** (software, hardware, services, etc.)
- **Certifications** (SOC 2, ISO, security clearances)
- **Performance history** (on-time delivery, quality, support responsiveness)
- **Preferred status** (if on preferred vendor list)

New vendors are added after first purchase; performance is tracked.

### 6.2 Vendor Performance Scorecard

Annually, Procurement reviews vendor performance:
- **On-time delivery**: % of orders delivered on schedule
- **Quality**: Defect rate, returns, rework
- **Support**: Response time, issue resolution
- **Cost**: Price competitiveness vs. market
- **Compliance**: Security, data handling, SLA adherence

**Scoring**: Vendors rated A (excellent, 90%+), B (good, 75–90%), C (acceptable, <75%). C-rated vendors may be removed from preferred list.

### 6.3 Alternative Vendors

To reduce single-vendor lock-in:
- Procurement maintains 2–3 qualified vendors per category
- If primary vendor performance declines, alternative vendors are available
- Contracts include data export rights and transition services

## 7. Record Retention and Audit

All procurement documentation is retained for 7 years:
- RFQs and RFPs issued
- Vendor quotes received
- Scoring matrices and evaluation notes
- POs issued and status
- Vendor performance records

Internal audit reviews procurement compliance quarterly; external auditors review during annual audit.
