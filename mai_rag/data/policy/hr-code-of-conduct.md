# Code of Conduct & Professional Standards

## 1. Purpose & Scope

This Code of Conduct establishes behavioral and professional standards for all Northwind Technologies employees and contractors. We are committed to fostering a workplace where every team member feels respected, safe, and empowered to do their best work. This policy applies globally and at all times, including during company events, remote work, and business travel.

All employees must acknowledge receipt of this policy as part of onboarding (see the Employee Onboarding Guide for integration with Day-1 mandatory training).

## 2. Core Values & Expected Behavior

| Value | Example Behavior |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Integrity** | Honest communication; admit mistakes; no falsification of records |
| **Respect** | Listen actively; value diverse perspectives; inclusive language |
| **Accountability** | Take ownership; follow through on commitments; escalate blockers |
| **Excellence** | Continuous improvement; high-quality work; professional appearance |

## 3. Workplace Conduct Standards

**3.1** Employees shall treat colleagues with courtesy and professionalism at all times. Bullying, intimidation, or deliberate exclusion is prohibited.

**3.2** Personal use of company resources (computers, phones, networks) must be minimal and aligned with the Acceptable Use Policy. See the Acceptable Use Policy for detailed guidance on email, messaging, and internet usage.

**3.3** Confidential company information—financial data, customer lists, technical roadmaps, M&A discussions—must be protected. Unauthorized disclosure breaches confidentiality and may result in immediate termination.

**3.4** Conflicts of interest must be disclosed to your manager or People Operations. Examples: a family member on staff, investments in competitors, or side consulting work without prior approval.

**3.5** Employees are expected to maintain punctuality and adherence to work schedules as documented in offer letters and the Remote & Hybrid Work Policy.

## 4. Violations & Escalation

Violations of this Code may result in corrective action, suspension, or termination depending on severity and history. Minor breaches may trigger a verbal warning; serious violations (fraud, violence, harassment) lead to immediate investigation and possible termination.

For concerns about misconduct, employees should:
1. Raise the issue with their direct manager.
2. If the manager is involved, escalate to People Operations or General Counsel.
3. For confidential reporting, contact the ethics hotline (details in onboarding materials).

See the Grievance Procedure for the formal complaint process and investigation timeline.

## 5. Acknowledgment

By accepting employment with Northwind Technologies, you acknowledge understanding and agreement to this Code. Annual re-acknowledgment is required during performance review cycles.
