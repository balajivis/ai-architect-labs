# Compensation Philosophy & Pay Equity

## 1. Guiding Principles

Northwind Technologies believes compensation should be:
- **Market-competitive**: aligned with peer SaaS/tech companies (Austin market baseline)
- **Equitable**: consistent across gender, race, and tenure for similar roles
- **Transparent**: employees understand how pay is determined
- **Performance-aligned**: tied to individual and company results

We conduct annual pay equity audits to identify and correct disparities.

## 2. Compensation Components

**2.1 Base Salary**: Fixed annual compensation determined by:
- Market data (benchmarking via Radford, Mercer surveys)
- Role level and scope
- Relevant experience and skills
- Geographic location (Austin HQ is baseline; other locations adjusted by cost-of-living index)

**2.2 Bonus**: Discretionary annual incentive (0–20% of base, depending on role and company profitability):
- Individual performance rating (from Performance Review & Feedback Process)
- Company financial performance (EBITDA or revenue growth target)
- Paid typically in Q1 following review cycle

**2.3 Equity (RSUs)**: Stock grants vest over four years (1-year cliff). Grants are:
- Offered at hire and refresh opportunities every 2–3 years
- Subject to board approval and 409A valuation
- Non-forfeitable upon vesting (held in restricted brokerage account)

**2.4 Benefits Package**: See Benefits Overview for health, retirement, paid time off, and wellness benefits.

## 3. Pay Equity & Transparency

**3.1 Audit Process**: Annually, HR and Finance analyze compensation across:
- Gender
- Race/ethnicity
- Age
- Tenure in role
- Reporting level

Any unexplained gap ≥10% triggers a remediation plan (adjustment or justification documentation).

**3.2 Individual Salary Transparency**: Upon request, employees may request a written explanation of how their salary was determined and how it compares to market rates (aggregate data only, no peer comparisons).

**3.3 Raise Philosophy**:
- Annual reviews include merit raise evaluation (see Performance Review & Feedback Process, section 7)
- Promotions typically include a 10–20% salary increase
- Market adjustments applied if benchmarking shows salary has fallen below 25th percentile for role

**3.4 Compensation Bands**: Each role has a defined band:
| Scope | Typical Band Width |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Individual Contributor (Level 1–3)** | $120K–$200K |
| **Senior / Lead (Level 4–5)** | $150K–$280K |
| **Manager / Principal (Level 6–7)** | $180K–$320K+ |

*Bands are adjusted annually based on market data; individual placement within band depends on experience and performance.*

## 4. Promotion & Compensation Changes

**4.1** Promotions are evaluated during the annual review cycle or on an ad-hoc basis if a new role opens. The hiring manager and HR determine the new salary band and specific offer.

**4.2** Employees changing roles within Northwind receive an offer letter documenting new salary, any equity refresh, and change effective date.

**4.3** Lateral moves (same level, different function) do not automatically trigger salary increases. Changes are evaluated case-by-case by Finance and the hiring manager.

## 5. Confidentiality & Non-Disparagement

Employees may discuss their own compensation, but may not demand disclosure of others' salaries or access personnel files of colleagues (unless authorized by HR for legitimate business purpose, e.g., payroll auditing).

Managers may not prohibit compensation discussion among employees. Retaliation for discussing pay is prohibited.

## 6. Special Compensation Arrangements

**6.1 Relocation Assistance**: Employees relocating for Northwind may receive:
- Moving allowance ($5,000–$25,000 depending on distance)
- Temporary housing (up to 90 days)
- Tax gross-up (capped at $10,000)

**6.2 Sign-On Bonus**: New hires may receive a one-time bonus ($10K–$100K) to offset forfeited incentives from previous employer. Typically subject to 12–24 month clawback.

**6.3 Retention Bonus**: Key employees may be offered retention incentives (lump-sum or equity) during critical projects or potential separation scenarios.

## 7. Pay Secrecy Prohibited

Northwind does not prohibit, restrict, or penalize employees for discussing wages, hours, or other terms and conditions of employment (except as required by law or court order).
