# Contractor & Consultant Payment Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy governs the engagement, payment, and tax compliance for independent contractors and consultants at Northwind Technologies. Contractors are non-employees who provide specialized services on a project or temporary basis.

**Related policies**:
- **Purchase Approval Matrix**: Approval thresholds for contractor engagement
- **Invoice Processing & Accounts Payable**: Contractor invoice processing and 1099 reporting
- **Vendor Onboarding (Finance & Procurement)**: Contractor vendor setup

## 2. Contractor vs. Employee Classification

### 2.1 Independent Contractor Definition

An **independent contractor** is a worker who is **not an employee**. Key distinguishing factors:

| Factor | Employee | Contractor |
|---|---|---|
| **Control** | Employer controls how, when, where work is done | Contractor controls work methods; only results matter |
| **Integration** | Works as part of company team; integrated operations | Works independently; separate from company |
| **Tools/Equipment** | Company provides tools, office space, equipment | Contractor provides own tools |
| **Duration** | Ongoing/indefinite | Project-based, finite duration |
| **Benefits** | Eligible for health insurance, PTO, 401(k) | No benefits |
| **Taxes** | Company withholds income tax, FICA | Contractor pays self-employment tax |
| **Multiple clients** | Typically exclusive to one employer | May work for multiple clients |
| **Intellectual property** | Company owns IP created | May retain some IP rights (unless contracted) |

### 2.2 IRS Classification Standards

The **IRS uses 20 common-law factors** to determine if worker is employee or contractor:

**Indicators of employee status**: Set schedule, works on company premises, trained by company, follows company policies, integrated into company operations, receives benefits.

**Indicators of contractor status**: Works with own tools, sets own schedule, can work for competitors, free to hire substitutes, paid by project/deliverable, no exclusive relationship.

**Critical**: Misclassifying an employee as contractor can result in:
- Back taxes and penalties to IRS
- Lawsuits from workers for unpaid benefits/overtime
- HR sanctions

**Finance must work with HR and Legal** to properly classify workers before engagement.

## 3. Contractor Engagement and Approval

### 3.1 Contractor Approval Process

1. **Department requests contractor engagement**: Manager submits to HR with:
   - Name of contractor or job description (if sourcing)
   - Role and responsibilities
   - Duration (start date, end date or open-ended)
   - Estimated cost (hourly rate × hours OR fixed project price)
   - Business justification (why contractor vs. hire employee?)

2. **HR review**: HR confirms:
   - Is this really a contractor (not de facto employee)?
   - Is there an existing employee who could do this work?
   - Compliance with company policies (security, NDA, background check)

3. **Finance approval**:
   - **Under $10,000**: Department Manager approves
   - **$10,000–$50,000**: Department VP approves
   - **$50,000–$100,000**: CFO approves
   - **Over $100,000**: CFO + CEO approval (per **Purchase Approval Matrix**)

4. **Legal review**: General Counsel prepares or reviews contractor agreement

5. **Execution**: Both parties sign agreement before work begins

### 3.2 Contractor Agreement (SOW)

All contractor engagements ≥$5,000 require a written **Statement of Work (SOW)** or **Independent Contractor Agreement** signed by:
- Contractor (or contractor's company)
- Manager or VP (on behalf of Northwind)
- Legal witness or General Counsel (for agreements >$25K)

**SOW must include**:
- **Scope of work**: Deliverables and acceptance criteria
- **Compensation**: Rate (hourly or fixed price), payment schedule, expenses covered
- **Duration**: Start date, end date, or term
- **Intellectual property (IP)**: Who owns IP created (typically Northwind owns all)
- **Confidentiality and NDA**: Standard terms (contractor agrees not to disclose confidential information)
- **Insurance and liability**: If applicable (contractor liable for own errors)
- **Independent contractor status**: Contractor confirms no employee relationship
- **Tax compliance**: Contractor responsible for self-employment tax
- **Termination clause**: Either party can terminate with X days notice (typically 7–30 days)

## 4. Payment Terms and Methods

### 4.1 Compensation Structure

| Structure | Details | Common Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Hourly rate** | Contractor invoices for hours worked; Northwind pays per time sheet | Temporary staff, augmentation |
| **Fixed price** | Contractor quotes fixed price for project; paid upon completion/milestones | Project-based, deliverable-driven |
| **Monthly retainer** | Contractor is paid flat monthly fee; hours are flexible | Ongoing fractional services (CFO, counsel, advisor) |
| **Milestone-based** | Contractor is paid upon completion of predefined milestones | Large projects (software development, implementation) |

### 4.2 Payment Terms

- **Hourly/T&M**: Contractor invoices weekly or bi-weekly; Northwind pays within Net 30
- **Fixed price**: Typically 50% upfront (upon signing), 50% on completion; may include milestone payments
- **Retainer**: Paid monthly in advance; adjusted annually or per contract term

### 4.3 Expense Reimbursement

Contractor expenses (travel, software, supplies) are handled per contract:
- **Inclusive rate**: Contractor's rate includes expenses (most common for short-term contractors)
- **Separate reimbursement**: Contractor is reimbursed for actual expenses (requires receipts per **Expense Reimbursement Policy**)
- **Estimate and approval**: Large expenses (e.g., $500 software purchase) require pre-approval by manager before contractor incurs

## 5. Time Tracking and Invoicing (Hourly Contractors)

### 5.1 Timesheet Requirements

**For hourly or time-and-materials contractors:**

1. **Timesheet submission**: Contractor submits weekly or bi-weekly timesheet showing:
   - Dates worked
   - Hours per day
   - Deliverables or tasks completed
   - Any travel or expenses incurred

2. **Manager approval**: Manager reviews timesheet and approves (confirms hours are accurate and deliverables completed)

3. **Invoice submission**: Contractor invoices Northwind based on approved timesheet:
   - Invoice number and date
   - Contractor name and tax ID (EIN or SSN)
   - Hourly rate and total hours
   - Total due and payment terms
   - W-9 form on file (see section below)

### 5.2 Invoice Processing

Contractor invoices are processed like vendor invoices (see **Invoice Processing & Accounts Payable**):
1. **Three-way match**: Invoice matches timesheet, approved PO, and contractor rate agreement
2. **Manager approval**: Manager confirms hours and deliverables in Expensify
3. **Finance approval**: Finance verifies W-9 on file, rate is within SOW, and budget is available
4. **Payment**: Paid within Net 30 per contract terms

## 6. Tax Compliance: W-9 and 1099 Reporting

### 6.1 W-9 Form (Before First Payment)

**Before paying any contractor**, Finance must obtain:

- **W-9 form** (Form W-9: Request for Taxpayer Identification Number and Certification)
- **Signed** by contractor (or contractor's company owner if LLC/S-Corp)
- **TIN** (Tax Identification Number): Social Security Number (SSN) for sole proprietor, or EIN for LLC/S-Corp

**Finance retains W-9 on file** in contractor's record; stored securely (separate from email, protected from unauthorized access).

### 6.2 1099-NEC Reporting

At year-end, Finance reports contractor payments to IRS and contractor:

**1099-NEC form** (Form 1099-NEC: Nonemployee Compensation) is issued if:
- Contractor was paid **≥$600 in a calendar year**
- Contractor is a US person (SSN or ITIN)

**Finance must**:
1. Report total payments made to contractor (sum of all invoices for Jan–Dec)
2. Provide copies to contractor by **January 31** following year-end
3. File with IRS by **February 28** (March 31 if filed electronically)
4. Retain copy for 7-year tax record

**Example**: Contractor X invoices $4,000 in January, $3,500 in February, $2,800 in March = $10,300 total for Q1. If no additional invoices, Q1 total is $10,300 (1099-NEC issued for $10,300). If contractor invoices again in Q2, annual total would be tracked and 1099-NEC issued for full-year amount in Jan following year.

### 6.3 Payments Under $600 (No 1099)

Contractors paid <$600 in a year do **not require 1099 reporting**. However:
- W-9 still must be on file (for documentation and future reference)
- IRS guidance suggests W-9 is good practice even for <$600 payments

## 7. Contractor Compliance and Restrictions

### 7.1 Worker Status and Benefits

Contractors are **not eligible** for:
- Health insurance or other benefits
- Paid time off (PTO) or sick leave
- 401(k) or retirement plan
- Workers' compensation (in most cases)
- Unemployment insurance
- Stock options or equity grants

**Exception**: Board advisors or long-term strategic consultants may negotiate benefits (e.g., equity grant); these are rare and require Board approval.

### 7.2 Exclusive vs. Non-Exclusive Work

**Non-exclusive contractor** (typical):
- May work for other clients during engagement
- No restriction on competing work (unless explicitly stated in SOW)
- No exclusive relationship with Northwind

**Exclusive contractor** (rare):
- Works only for Northwind during engagement period
- May not work for competitors or other clients
- Requires higher compensation premium

Exclusive arrangements must be clearly stated in SOW; Finance flags for management (unusual and costly).

### 7.3 Contractor vs. Employee "Creep"

**Risk**: Contractor gradually becomes de facto employee (works on-site, attends meetings, reports to manager, works full-time indefinitely).

**Prevention**:
- Enforce end dates: Contractor agreement expires on specified date
- No indefinite renewal: If contractor needs to continue, manager submits new SOW (Finance re-evaluates engagement)
- Monitor tenure: Contractor relationships lasting >2 years are reviewed by HR (may need to convert to employee)
- Limit control: Contractor should have autonomy (not micromanaged like employee)

## 8. Intellectual Property and Confidentiality

### 8.1 IP Ownership

Standard Northwind contractor agreement includes:

**"All intellectual property created during the engagement, including code, designs, documentation, and processes, is owned by Northwind Technologies. Contractor waives all rights to IP created."**

**Exception**: Contractor may retain pre-existing IP or third-party software (if clearly identified in SOW). Any such exceptions must be documented.

### 8.2 Confidentiality and NDA

All contractors sign **Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)**, which includes:
- Confidentiality obligations (don't disclose company information)
- Non-solicitation (don't recruit Northwind employees after engagement ends)
- Non-compete (if applicable; varies by state and role)
- Duration of obligations (typically 2–3 years post-engagement)

## 9. Contractor Termination

### 9.1 Termination Process

**If Northwind terminates contractor:**
1. Manager notifies contractor and Finance
2. **Final date**: Contractor's access is terminated (email, systems, facilities)
3. **Equipment return**: Contractor returns any company equipment (laptop, keys, documents)
4. **Final invoice**: Contractor submits final invoice for work completed through termination date
5. **Payment**: Northwind pays final invoice within Net 30

**If contractor terminates:**
- Contractor provides notice per SOW (typically 7–30 days)
- Finance notes contract end date
- Final invoice processed as normal

### 9.2 Severance (Rare)

Unlike employees, contractors typically receive **no severance** if terminated early. SOW should specify:
- Can either party terminate for cause? (e.g., unsatisfactory work, breach of NDA)
- Is there an early termination fee? (rare; typically 0)
- What happens to incomplete work? (contractor delivers partial deliverables; Northwind pays prorated)

**Exception**: If Northwind terminates without cause before project completion, may offer early termination payment (negotiated; not required by law).

## 10. Contractor Record Retention

Finance maintains contractor records for 7 years:
- SOW/contractor agreement
- W-9 form
- Invoices and payment records
- Timesheets (if hourly)
- 1099 forms issued (copy for Northwind's records)

These records are reviewed during annual audit and in case of any IRS inquiry.
