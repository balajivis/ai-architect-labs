# Emergency Response Plan

## 1. Purpose & Scope

The Emergency Response Plan (ERP) defines immediate response procedures for physical emergencies at Northwind facilities (HQ in Austin, TX, and any leased office space). This plan prioritizes employee safety, law enforcement coordination, and business recovery. It complements the Business Continuity Plan (BCP) and Crisis Management Plan (CMP) by adding on-site safety protocols and facility-level decision-making.

**Scope**: All employees, contractors, visitors, and facilities management personnel.

## 2. Emergency Classification & Activation

| Emergency Type | Activation Trigger | Initial Responder | Escalation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fire** | Smoke/flames detected; fire alarm activated | Facilities Manager + Security | Call 911; evacuate per protocol |
| **Medical Emergency** | Injury, collapse, chest pain, unconsciousness | Any employee; notify Security | Call 911; activate AED if trained |
| **Workplace Violence / Active Threat** | Armed individual, credible threat, hostile incident | Any employee; call 911 immediately | Lockdown; notify Facilities + Security |
| **Severe Weather (Tornado, Flash Flood)** | NOAA warning issued; visible conditions deteriorate | Security monitors alerts; pre-notification to staff | Shelter in place (interior rooms, away from windows) |
| **Environmental Hazard (Chem Spill, Gas Leak)** | Unusual odor, visible vapor, equipment malfunction | Facilities; DO NOT investigate | Evacuate; call 911; await hazmat response |
| **Loss of Critical Utilities (Power, Water)** | Outage >15 min; affects life safety or operations | Facilities assesses; IT logs recovery | Shift to remote; activate BCP if >1 hour |

## 3. On-Site Emergency Roles & Procedures

### 3.1 Emergency Response Team (ERT)
- **Emergency Coordinator** (Security Manager or Facilities Director): Commands scene; interfaces with 911 responders
- **Safety Wardens** (1 per floor): Account for personnel; manage evacuation routes
- **First Aiders**: Any employee with First Aid/CPR certification; coordinates with 911 dispatch
- **IT/Backup Lead**: Ensures data security during evacuation; activates BCP recovery if needed

### 3.2 Evacuation & Assembly
1. **Alarm/Alert Activation**: Fire alarm, PA system, or Slack #emergency channel for non-immediate threats
2. **Evacuation**: Use nearest marked exit; no elevators; move quickly but calmly to designated assembly point (southeast parking lot, away from building)
3. **Accountability**: Safety Warden takes headcount and reports to Emergency Coordinator via radio
4. **Shelter-in-Place** (if evacuation unsafe, e.g., active shooter nearby): Move to interior room, lock door, silence phones, await all-clear

### 3.3 Reunification (post-emergency)
- Family contact information pre-registered via HR system
- HR releases employees only with ID verification
- IT coordinates remote-work setup if facility remains closed

## 4. Specific Emergency Procedures

### 4.1 Fire Emergency
1. **Detection**: Employee activates nearest pull-station; alerts others verbally
2. **Evacuation**: All personnel leave via nearest exit; do not use elevators
3. **Assembly**: Meet at southeast parking lot; Safety Wardens account for personnel
4. **Security/Facilities**: Confirm fire department notified (Security does this immediately); meet at main entrance to direct responders
5. **Re-entry**: Only after fire department clears building; Emergency Coordinator authorizes
6. **Drills**: Quarterly evacuation drills; all personnel participate; feedback recorded

### 4.2 Medical Emergency
1. **Response**: Nearest employee calls 911 immediately; provide victim's location and condition
2. **First Aid**: If certified responder nearby, provide CPR/bleeding control per training; do not move victim unless life-threatening hazard
3. **AED**: If available and trained personnel present, deploy AED per device instructions
4. **Privacy**: Clear area of non-essential personnel; do not photograph or record victim
5. **Incident Report**: HR documents emergency; records shared with General Counsel (liability review)

### 4.3 Active Threat / Workplace Violence
1. **Immediate**: Any employee witnessing threat calls 911 first, then Security hotline (+1-844-NORTHWIND-SEC)
2. **Lockdown**: Do not confront; move to interior room, lock door, turn off lights, silence phone, await law enforcement all-clear
3. **Law Enforcement**: Security coordinates with police; provides building layout and real-time updates if possible
4. **Post-incident**: Trauma support offered; voluntary counseling available; mandatory threat assessment before return-to-work (coordinated by VP People and Security)
5. **See Crisis Management Plan** for executive notification and public communication steps

### 4.4 Severe Weather (Tornado, Flooding)
1. **Alert**: Security monitors NOAA alerts; sends Slack alert if tornado watch/warning or flash-flood warning issued
2. **Shelter**: Instruct personnel to move to interior rooms on lowest floor, away from windows, doors, and exterior walls
3. **Assembly**: Do not evacuate during tornado; remain sheltered until all-clear from NOAA/National Weather Service
4. **Recovery**: Once threat passes, assess facility for damage; contact Facilities for utilities check before resuming normal operations
5. **BCP Activation**: If facility sustains structural damage or utilities compromised >1 hour, shift to 100% remote per BCP

### 4.5 Loss of Critical Utilities
1. **Power Outage**: Facilities assesses estimated duration. If >15 min, announce to staff: "Shift to remote work; VPN access available."
2. **Water/Restrooms**: Evacuate facility if emergency eyewash/safety systems affected; activates BCP.
3. **IT Response**: Ensure cloud services (AWS/Azure) functional; on-call engineer verifies Datadog monitoring; if data-center power lost, failover to secondary cloud.
4. **Return Timeline**: Facilities provides hourly updates; employees may return once utilities restored.

## 5. Emergency Communication

### 5.1 During Emergency (0–60 minutes)
- **Primary**: PA system announcements; text alerts to all-employee group
- **Fallback**: Slack #emergency channel; phone tree activated by Safety Wardens
- **External**: Facilities/Security coordinates with local emergency services; no social media posts until incident command okays

### 5.2 Post-Emergency (after all-clear)
- **All-hands email**: CEO or VP People summarizes incident, any injuries, next steps, and support resources
- **See Internal Communications Policy** for detailed messaging requirements
- **Trauma support**: EAP (Employee Assistance Program) offered; email and phone lines promoted

## 6. Training & Drills

- **All employees**: Mandatory emergency-response training (1 hour) during Onboarding Day 1; annual refresher
- **Evacuation drill**: Quarterly; coordinated by Facilities; attendance recorded
- **First Aid / CPR**: Annual certification required for designated First Aiders; recertification managed by HR
- **Emergency Coordinator refresher**: Annually; led by Security Manager and Facilities Director

**Last Drill**: March 2026 (tornado shelter-in-place scenario). Evacuation time avg 4 minutes. **Next drill**: June 2026.

## 7. Post-Emergency Actions

1. **Incident Documentation**: Security completes form (date, time, type, injuries, law enforcement involvement)
2. **Regulatory Notification**: HR informs OSHA of any work-related injury within 24 hours (federal requirement)
3. **Post-Mortem**: Manager and affected team debrief within 48 hours; feedback captured
4. **Plan Update**: Security reviews plan for gaps; updates made if procedure failed or environment changed
5. **Board Notification**: If incident resulted in injury or major facility damage, VP Operations briefs Board within 7 days
