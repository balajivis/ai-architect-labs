# Whistleblower Policy

## 1. Purpose & Scope

Northwind Technologies is committed to the highest standards of ethical conduct and legal compliance. This Whistleblower Policy encourages employees, contractors, and third parties to report suspected violations of law, company policy, or ethical standards—without fear of retaliation.

**Scope**: All employees, contractors, vendors, customers, and other stakeholders who become aware of potential misconduct.

**Protected activities**: Reporting in good faith; participating in investigations; assisting law enforcement.

## 2. What Should Be Reported

### 2.1 Reportable Conduct
Employees should report suspected violations, including but not limited to:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Financial Misconduct** | Fraudulent expense reports, falsified financial records, embezzlement, bribery, unauthorized spending, accounting irregularities |
| **Legal Violations** | Antitrust violations, tax evasion, sanctions/export control breaches, labor law violations, discrimination, harassment |
| **Data & Security Breaches** | Unauthorized access to customer data, internal data exfiltration, security policy violations, breach cover-up |
| **Product & Quality** | Knowingly shipping defective product, concealing safety risks, falsifying test results, misrepresenting features to customers |
| **Environmental & Health/Safety** | OSHA violations, unsafe working conditions, environmental violations, failure to report workplace injuries (see Health & Safety Policy) |
| **Ethical Violations** | Conflicts of interest not disclosed, bribery or kickbacks, discrimination/harassment, retaliation against employees, abuse of authority |
| **Regulatory Compliance** | GDPR violations, HIPAA/healthcare regulation violations, SOX/SEC violations, export control breaches |

### 2.2 Good Faith Reporting
- **Good faith**: Reporting party believes information is true; reasonable basis for concern
- **Protections apply**: Even if investigation concludes no violation occurred (as long as report was made in good faith)
- **False accusations**: Deliberately false reports intended to harm another employee are NOT protected; may result in disciplinary action

### 2.3 NOT Reportable (Handle via Normal Channels)
- Personal disputes with colleague (use HR or manager)
- Performance management issues (use manager or HR)
- Disagreement with business strategy (use manager or leadership)
- Rumors without factual basis (not actionable)

## 3. Reporting Channels & Procedures

### 3.1 Primary Channel: Anonymous Hotline & Email
**Northwind Whistleblower Hotline** (24/7, operated by third-party service):
- **Phone**: +1-844-NORTH-ETHICS (1-844-667-8438)
- **Email**: whistleblower@northwind.com (forwarded to external advisor; not monitored by HR or management)
- **Web portal**: ethics.northwind.com (secure submission; tracks case ID)
- **Language**: English, Spanish, Mandarin available; interpreter service on request
- **Confidentiality**: Report is anonymous unless reporter opts to disclose identity; third-party hotline does not retain reporter identification

### 3.2 Secondary Channels (Non-Anonymous)
If employee prefers to report with identity:
- **HR**: equity@northwind.com; report forwarded to VP People and General Counsel
- **General Counsel**: legal@northwind.com; forward to General Counsel + Board Audit Committee
- **CEO**: (for reports about General Counsel or other executives) directly to CEO; General Counsel recused

### 3.3 Protected Activity: External Reporting
Employees may report suspected violations to:
- **Law enforcement** (FBI, DOJ, IRS, SEC, OSHA, state attorney general, etc.)
- **Regulatory agencies** (GDPR authority, HIPAA investigator, etc.)
- **Authorized government agents**: Full cooperation in subpoena, investigation, or legal proceedings

**Northwind does not prohibit external reporting** and does not retaliate against employees who report to authorities (see Section 4).

## 4. Anti-Retaliation & Protections

### 4.1 Zero-Tolerance Retaliation Policy
**Northwind strictly prohibits retaliation** against any person who:
- Reports suspected misconduct in good faith (via any channel)
- Participates in an investigation
- Objects to conduct the person reasonably believes is illegal or unethical
- Assists law enforcement or regulatory agencies

### 4.2 Retaliation Defined
Retaliation includes, but is not limited to:
- **Termination, demotion, suspension** of employment
- **Reduction in pay, benefits, or PTO**
- **Reassignment to undesirable location or role** (intentional punishment)
- **Exclusion from meetings, information, opportunities** (intentional isolation)
- **Negative performance review** linked to report (retaliation not performance)
- **Hostile work environment** (coworkers shunning, spreading rumors)
- **Threat or intimidation** (implicit or explicit)
- **Blacklisting** (preventing future employment)

### 4.3 Duration of Protection
- **During investigation**: Full protection
- **Post-investigation** (if violation not found): Protected if report was in good faith
- **Long-term**: Retaliation protection extends indefinitely; if retaliation occurs >6 months after report, still presumed linked to whistleblowing

### 4.4 If Retaliation Occurs
1. **Report retaliation** to HR or General Counsel immediately (within 48 hours preferred)
2. **Investigation**: Separate investigation launched; interim protections considered (e.g., change of supervisor, temp reassignment)
3. **Remedy**: If retaliation confirmed, corrective action taken (discipline, termination, reinstatement, backpay, apology, removal from employee record)
4. **Legal recourse**: Employee may also file complaint with OSHA, EEOC, SEC, or other agencies (retaliation protections exist under Sarbanes-Oxley, Dodd-Frank, environmental laws, etc.)

## 5. Investigation Process

### 5.1 Receipt & Acknowledgment
1. **Report received**: Hotline or email received and logged
2. **Case ID assigned**: Unique tracking number provided to reporter (if non-anonymous)
3. **Acknowledgment**: Within 24 hours, reporter (if identifiable) receives acknowledgment; expected timeline for investigation provided
4. **Confidentiality**: Reporter identity protected; limited to those with need to know (typically: General Counsel, investigator, Audit Committee)

### 5.2 Investigation Team & Conflict Resolution
| Scenario | Investigator |
|----------|---|
| Employee-on-employee misconduct | HR + External investigator (retained by company) |
| Manager misconduct | General Counsel + External investigator |
| Executive (VP+) misconduct | Board Audit Committee + External counsel (third-party firm) |
| Financial/fraud allegations | CFO + External forensic accountant + General Counsel |
| GDPR/data breach allegations | VP Security + External security firm + General Counsel |

**Conflict management**: If General Counsel implicated, Board Audit Committee assumes oversight; CEO recused if implicated.

### 5.3 Investigation Timeline
- **Scope-setting**: Within 5 business days; investigator meets reporter (if identifiable) to understand allegations, gather initial documents
- **Interviews**: Within 2 weeks; investigator interviews witnesses, subject of allegation (if appropriate), relevant stakeholders
- **Document review**: Contemporaneous; email, records, financial docs, communications reviewed
- **Findings**: Within 30 days (may extend to 60 days for complex investigations); investigator prepares draft report
- **Corrective action**: If violation confirmed, corrective measures implemented within 14 days

### 5.4 Investigation Standards
- **Impartiality**: Investigator has no relationship to parties involved; conflict of interest questionnaire completed
- **Due process**: Subject of allegation is informed of allegations and given opportunity to respond (unless investigation purpose defeated by notice)
- **Confidentiality**: Information limited to need-to-know basis; investigation subject may not retaliate based on knowledge of investigation
- **Documentation**: All interviews, documents, findings recorded and retained per Records & Document Control Policy

### 5.5 Findings & Remediation
**If violation confirmed:**
1. **Discipline**: Subject receives corrective action proportionate to violation (verbal warning, termination, criminal referral, etc.)
2. **Preventive measures**: Root-cause analysis conducted; system improvements implemented (process change, training, monitoring)
3. **Reporter notification** (if identifiable): Outcome communicated to reporter (confidential; details withheld if necessary)
4. **Documentation**: Case file maintained; subject to audit trail

**If insufficient evidence:**
1. **Closure**: Case closed; no violation found based on available evidence
2. **No finding**: Reporter informed that investigation closed; retaliation protections still apply (no adverse action permitted)
3. **Retention**: File retained for 7 years (standard records retention)

**If report was bad faith (deliberately false):**
1. **Corrective action**: Reporter may face discipline up to termination
2. **Appeal**: Reporter has right to appeal within 14 days to Audit Committee (non-retaliation still applies during appeal)

## 6. Board & Audit Committee Oversight

### 6.1 Quarterly Reporting
- **Audit Committee** (subset of Board, independent directors) receives quarterly report:
  - Number of reports received (by channel, category)
  - Status of investigations (open, closed, findings)
  - Discipline imposed; follow-up status
  - Trends and systemic issues identified
  - Retaliation allegations; resolutions

### 6.2 Annual Assessment
- **Board**: Reviews whistleblower program effectiveness; assesses if policies/procedures adequate
- **Policy updates**: Changes to procedures or hotline services approved by Board

## 7. Confidentiality & Record Management

- **Reporter identity**: Confidential; disclosed only with reporter consent or if legally compelled
- **Investigation records**: Maintained by General Counsel in secure location; access limited
- **Retention**: Case files retained 7 years minimum (longer if active dispute/litigation)
- **Litigation hold**: If investigation becomes subject of legal action, case file subject to litigation hold (see Records & Document Control Policy)
- **Privilege**: Investigation files may be privileged (attorney-client or attorney work product); not discoverable in civil litigation without waiver

## 8. Communication & Training

### 8.1 Whistleblower Policy Distribution
- **New hires**: Policy provided during Onboarding Day 1; hotline number on employee badge
- **Annual reminder**: Email + poster campaign promoting hotline and protections
- **Managers**: Training on handling reports, retaliation prohibition, investigation process
- **Website**: Public-facing whistleblower page for vendors, customers, job candidates

### 8.2 Preventing Retaliation (Manager Training)
Managers trained annually on:
- What constitutes retaliation
- Avoiding implicit retaliation (tone, exclusion, assignment choices)
- Reporting retaliation if they become aware
- Responding to employee questions about investigations (refer to HR/General Counsel; do not discuss)

## 9. Example Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Expense Fraud Report**
- Employee reports manager submitted falsified expense report (alcohol marked as "meals")
- Report made via anonymous hotline
- General Counsel engages external accountant; reviews expense data, manager's card statements, receipt images
- **Finding**: Fraud confirmed; 12 alcohol purchases mislabeled
- **Action**: Manager terminated; amount reimbursed to company; case referred to law enforcement
- **Timeline**: Reported Jan 15; investigation complete Feb 28; closure Mar 10

**Scenario 2: GDPR Violation Report**
- Data analyst reports manager instructed deletion of customer data without legal hold review
- Report made via HR (identifiable)
- VP Security investigates; reviews deletion logs, manager communications
- **Finding**: Data deleted prematurely; potential GDPR violation (customer may have right to data)
- **Action**: Backups restored (if available); customer notified; process revised; manager receives written warning
- **Timeline**: Reported May 1; investigation complete June 15; customer notification July 1

**Scenario 3: Sexual Harassment Report**
- Employee reports senior manager created hostile work environment (unwanted comments, exclusion)
- Report via HR (identifiable)
- HR + external investigator conduct interviews; documentation reviewed
- **Finding**: Substantiated harassment; pattern across multiple employees
- **Action**: Manager terminated; all-company training required; support offered to affected employees
- **Timeline**: Reported April 10; investigation complete May 25; termination May 31
