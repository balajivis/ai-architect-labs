# Anti-Bribery & Corruption Policy

## Purpose
This policy establishes Northwind Technologies' zero-tolerance approach to bribery, corruption, and improper payments. Northwind complies with the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), the UK Bribery Act 2010, and other applicable anti-corruption laws.

## 1. Applicability & Scope

This policy applies to:
- All Northwind employees (full-time, part-time, contractors)
- All directors and officers
- All agents, distributors, and resellers acting on Northwind's behalf
- All business activities, worldwide, including:
  - Customer and vendor relationships
  - Government interactions and public sector sales
  - Joint ventures and partnerships
  - Hiring and employment decisions

**Geographic scope**: Applies to all jurisdictions where Northwind operates or has customers/vendors, including high-risk jurisdictions (FCPA countries, corruption perceptions index-ranked countries).

## 2. Prohibited Conduct

### Bribery of Government Officials
**Prohibited**: Offering, promising, or giving anything of value to any government official, employee, or party official to:
- Obtain or retain business
- Secure any improper advantage
- Influence an official act or decision

**Government officials include**:
- Federal, state, and local government employees
- Officials of foreign governments and their agencies
- State-owned enterprise employees (if performing government functions)
- Political party officials and candidates
- Any family member of the above if they are acting on the official's behalf

**Examples of violations**:
- Offering a government procurement officer a free vacation to win a contract
- Paying a foreign tax official's "unofficial fee" to speed up a business license
- Funding a political candidate's campaign in exchange for favorable regulatory treatment
- Paying bribes through third parties (agents, distributors) to obscure the payment

### Commercial Bribery
**Prohibited**: Offering anything of value to a commercial customer, competitor, or supplier to:
- Induce them to breach a duty or agreement with a third party
- Secure improper competitive advantage
- Influence a business decision in Northwind's favor

**Examples**:
- Paying a competitor's employee to disclose confidential information
- Offering a customer's procurement officer a personal loan to win a deal
- Giving a vendor kick-back in exchange for inflated pricing that Northwind exploits

### Facilitation Payments
**Prohibited**: "Facilitation payments" (small bribes to government officials to perform routine duties) are **not permitted** under Northwind policy, even in jurisdictions where they may not be FCPA violations.

**Example of violation**: Paying a customs official $500 to expedite cargo clearance.

**Exception**: Emergency safety or security situations may warrant exception approval from General Counsel.

## 3. Permissible Business Gifts & Hospitality

Northwind employees may provide modest gifts and hospitality to business partners and government officials if they:
1. Are **not** intended to improperly influence a business decision
2. Comply with all applicable laws and the recipient's policies
3. Are properly documented and disclosed
4. Are of **modest value** (under $100 per item; aggregated limits per recipient per year: $500 commercial, $250 government official)

| Activity | Commercial Partner | Government Official | Limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Meal** | ✅ Yes (work-related) | ✅ Yes (modest, in office setting) | <$100 per meal |
| **Ticket to event** | ✅ Yes (industry conference, sporting event) | ❌ No (unless publicly available) | <$100 |
| **Gift (branded merchandise)** | ✅ Yes (pen, mug, t-shirt) | ✅ Yes (if modest & branded) | <$50 |
| **Holiday gift** | ✅ Yes (gift basket) | ❌ No (unless part of client entertainment) | <$100 |
| **Travel** | ✅ Yes (if business purpose) | ❌ No (highly scrutinized) | N/A |
| **Cash or equivalents** | ❌ No | ❌ No | N/A |

**Documentation**: All gifts over $50 must be recorded in the Northwind Gifts Register (maintained by HR).

## 4. Due Diligence on High-Risk Transactions

Before engaging a vendor, distributor, reseller, or joint venture partner in a high-risk jurisdiction or high-value transaction, Northwind conducts:

**Sanctions Screening** (OFAC/UN/EU):
- Check vendor against U.S. Department of Commerce Denied Parties List (DDPL), OFAC list, UN Consolidated List, and EU Consolidated List
- Annual refresh for existing vendors
- Automatic rejection if entity or individual appears on any list

**Know Your Customer (KYC)**:
- Verify legal identity and beneficial ownership (for entities over $500K annual commitment)
- Check corporate registration and legitimacy
- Verify absence of director/officer involvement in corruption scandals
- For distributors: verify sales strategy and customer list to ensure no improper payments

**Corruption Perception Ranking**:
- Transactions in countries ranked below 50 on Transparency International Corruption Perceptions Index receive enhanced due diligence
- Particularly scrutinized: government sales, consulting arrangements, and agent relationships

**Third-Party Compliance Agreement**:
- All high-risk vendors and resellers sign Anti-Corruption Compliance Agreement affirming:
  - No illegal payments or facilitation payments
  - Compliance with FCPA, UK Bribery Act, and local law
  - Right for Northwind to audit and terminate for violations

## 5. Business Courtesies & Hospitality Review

All entertainment, gifts, and travel expenses must comply with:
1. **This policy** (modest, documented, not improper influence)
2. **Applicable law** (FCPA, UK Bribery Act, local law)
3. **Recipient's company policies** (many organizations prohibit accepting gifts)
4. **Northwind's Expense & Travel Policy** (receipts, business purpose)

**Red flags** requiring escalation to General Counsel:
- Unusual requests (cash payments, travel to non-business locations, gifts to relatives)
- Requests from government officials or state-owned enterprises
- Requests from vendors in high-corruption jurisdictions
- Requests inconsistent with business purpose
- Requests to hide or mischaracterize the expense

## 6. Raising Concerns & Reporting

**If you suspect bribery, corruption, or improper payment**:

1. **Report to General Counsel** — Email or call (confidential)
2. **Report to CEO** — If concern involves General Counsel
3. **Report to Northwind Ethics Hotline** — Anonymous, third-party managed line (details in employee handbook)
4. **External reporting** — If internal reporting is ineffective, may report to SEC (Whistleblower Program), DOJ, or applicable government agency

**Protection against retaliation**:
- Northwind prohibits retaliation against employees who report in good faith
- Retaliation is grounds for immediate termination of the retaliator
- Reports are investigated confidentially

## 7. Consequences of Violation

**Employee violations** result in:
- First violation (minor, non-intentional): Documented warning + mandatory FCPA retraining
- Second violation or intentional violation: Suspension + legal review
- Material or criminal violation: Immediate termination + referral to law enforcement

**Company consequences**:
- Civil penalties up to twice the benefit sought (FCPA civil penalty)
- Criminal penalties up to $2 million per violation
- Debarment from government contracts
- Reputational harm and loss of customer confidence

## 8. FCPA Training & Certification

**Annual training**:
- All employees must complete FCPA training (30 minutes) annually
- Training covers: overview of FCPA/UK Bribery Act, real-world examples, red flags, reporting procedures
- 95% completion rate expected; non-completion escalated to VP People

**Certification**:
- All employees attest annually to compliance with this policy
- Senior management (VP+) and employees in high-risk roles certify quarterly
- Attestation: "I have completed FCPA training, understand the policy, and commit to compliance."

## 9. Government & Public Sector Sales

**Special considerations** for sales to government agencies (federal, state, foreign, SOE):

1. **Pre-bid due diligence** — Verify transaction legitimacy and absence of improper payments
2. **Proposal compliance** — Ensure representations are truthful and not misleading (False Claims Act risk)
3. **No facilitation payments** — Do not make any payments to speed up contract awards or decisions
4. **Export control review** — If selling overseas, verify product is not controlled under EAR/ITAR
5. **Sanctions screening** — Customer entity and all beneficial owners must clear sanctions checks

**Red flags in government sales**:
- Request to pay "consultation fees" before contract is awarded
- Requirement to use a specific agent or intermediary without clear business purpose
- Request to make payments to offshore accounts
- Unusual contract terms or pricing

## 10. Monitoring & Enforcement

**General Counsel monitors**:
- Expense reports flagged for unusual gifts or entertainment
- Third-party vendor screening results
- Employee reports of suspected violations
- Regulatory changes (new FCPA guidance, country-specific restrictions)

**Periodic audits**:
- Random audit of vendor relationships and due diligence files (annual)
- Expense report audit for suspicious patterns (quarterly)
- Gifts Register review (quarterly)

**Board reporting**:
- Annual report on FCPA/anti-corruption compliance to Audit Committee
- Material violations reported immediately to CEO + Board
