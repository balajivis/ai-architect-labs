# Parental Leave Policy

## 1. Purpose & Commitment

Northwind Technologies believes that supporting employees during major life transitions—birth, adoption, surrogacy, and fostering—strengthens our culture and enables employees to be fully present for their families and work. This policy provides generous paid parental leave and flexible return-to-work options.

## 2. Paid Parental Leave

**2.1 Eligibility**:
- All full-time employees (≥30 hours/week) are eligible
- No tenure requirement (eligible from day 1 of employment)
- Applies to:
  - Biological parent (birth)
  - Adoptive parent
  - Spouse/domestic partner of birthing parent
  - Same-sex spouse or same-sex domestic partner
  - Foster parent (extended fostering arrangements; see section 5)

**2.2 Leave Duration — UPDATED EFFECTIVE MAY 1, 2026**:
- **16 weeks paid parental leave** (640 hours at 100% salary)
  - This is an increase from the prior 8-week policy (superseded as of May 1, 2026)
  - Covers full salary, bonus pro-rata, and health benefits
  - Leave must be taken within 18 months of birth, adoption, or placement

**2.3 Leave for Birthing Parents**:
- Receive 16 weeks paid leave
- May extend with 4 weeks additional partially-paid leave (60% salary) if needed
- Eligible for short-term disability (60% salary) if medical complications extend beyond 8 weeks postpartum

**2.4 Leave for Non-Birthing Parents & Partners**:
- Receive 16 weeks paid leave
- Same terms as birthing parent

**2.5 Shared Parental Leave**:
- If both partners are Northwind employees, each receives 16 weeks independently
- Leave periods may overlap or stagger (e.g., one parent takes weeks 1–8, other parent takes weeks 9–16)
- Splitting is flexible; coordinate with managers and HR

## 3. Integration with Other Leave

**3.1** Parental leave is **separate from** and **in addition to**:
- Accrued PTO (vacation, sick, floating holidays)
- Short-term disability (for birth-related complications)
- Unpaid FMLA leave (federal requirement)

**3.2** Employees may combine:
- Parental leave (16 weeks paid) + accrued PTO (if desired)
- Parental leave + short-term disability (if medically necessary)
- Total time off may reach 20+ weeks if combining all

**3.3** PTO does NOT get charged during parental leave; leave time is separate.

## 4. Notification, Scheduling & Return-to-Work

**4.1 Notification**:
- Notify People Operations and manager as soon as pregnancy, adoption, or placement is known (earlier = better for planning)
- Formal leave request is submitted to Workday at least 8 weeks before expected leave start (or as soon as feasible for unexpected circumstances)

**4.2 Leave Request Approval**:
- HR confirms eligibility and leave dates
- Manager is informed and expected to plan coverage
- No approval is required; the policy guarantees the right to leave
- Employees are not permitted to work during parental leave (exception: light email check-ins if desired, manager discretion)

**4.3 Job Guarantee**:
- Same job or equivalent position upon return
- Salary, title, benefits unchanged
- Return to same team if possible; change only for organizational restructuring

**4.4 Return-to-Work Options**:
- **Full-time return** (standard): return to full-time role after leave
- **Gradual return** (available): work 50–75% time for 2–4 weeks, then ramp to full-time (paid at reduced hours + parental leave continues to cover the gap up to 16 weeks total)
- **Flex schedule**: core hours 10am–3pm CT, with flexibility for childcare/school schedules (see Remote & Hybrid Work Policy for details)

**4.5 Benefits During Leave**:
- **Health insurance**: Company-paid portion continues (same cost-share)
- **401(k)**: Company match pauses during leave; employee resumes contributions upon return
- **PTO accrual**: Continues to accrue (1.5 days/month) during leave
- **Tenure & seniority**: Leave time counts toward service length (for vesting, advancement, etc.)

## 5. Additional Parental Circumstances

**5.1 Multiple Children**:
- Parental leave is available for each child (born or adopted)
- Same 16-week benefit per child
- Leave periods may be spaced across different years

**5.2 Surrogacy**:
- Intended parent(s) are entitled to 16 weeks paid leave (same as adoption)
- Leave begins on child's birth or placement date
- Partner of intended parent receives 16 weeks (if employee)

**5.3 Foster Care & Extended Placement**:
- Employees who formally adopt from foster care receive 16 weeks
- Kinship care (adopting relative's child) receives 16 weeks
- Informal/temporary fostering does not qualify; formal legal guardianship must be in place

**5.4 Miscarriage, Stillbirth, Neonatal Loss**:
- Employees who experience pregnancy loss are entitled to 2 weeks paid leave (bereavement) + access to EAP counseling
- No need to use PTO or short-term disability
- Compassionate leave may extend to family members caring for the grieving employee

## 6. Unpaid Leave Extensions

If employees need more than 16 weeks:
- **FMLA leave**: up to 12 weeks unpaid federal job protection (see Leave & Time-Off Policy)
- **Unpaid parental leave**: negotiate with manager and HR; job security not guaranteed beyond FMLA
- **Accrued PTO**: use remaining vacation/sick time

## 7. Financial Benefits During Leave

**7.1 Salary & Bonus**:
- 100% of base salary continues during 16-week parental leave
- Annual bonus (if applicable) is pro-rated to the time worked in the bonus period
  - Example: employee takes 16 weeks leave in Q2 (mid-year); bonus is adjusted down proportionally
  - Bonus is paid on regular schedule (e.g., Q1 bonus paid in March); parental leave does not defer bonus

**7.2 Equity (RSUs)**:
- Ongoing RSU vesting continues during parental leave
- RSUs granted before leave are vested on schedule
- If employee was due for equity refresh during leave, refresh is delayed until return (or may be granted at return)

## 8. Partner/Spouse Parental Leave

If the non-Northwind partner of a Northwind employee is on parental leave and the Northwind employee is the secondary caregiver:
- Northwind employee may request flexible schedule or part-time work (see Remote & Hybrid Work Policy)
- Not a guarantee, but manager + HR work to accommodate

## 9. Childcare Support

Northwind offers:
- **Dependent care FSA**: pre-tax savings for childcare (up to $5,250/year)
- **Childcare resource referral**: access to vetted childcare providers in local markets
- **Lactation support**: private rooms on all office floors (see Diversity, Equity & Inclusion Policy, section 7.2)
- **Emergency childcare**: subsidized emergency childcare backup for 5 days/year (via referral partner)

See Benefits Overview for enrollment details.

## 11. Leave Planning & Communication

**11.1** Manager & HR Partnership:
- Manager and employee discuss:
  - Expected return date
  - Coverage plan (temp hire, backfill, cross-training)
  - Communication during leave (email, Slack access—usually turned off)
  - Flex schedule or gradual return (if applicable)

**11.2** Team Transparency**:
- Manager informs team of parental leave and coverage plan
- Respectful language is expected; questions about personal details are not asked
- Slack status is updated to "Out - Parental Leave through [date]"

**11.3** Return Preparation**:
- 2 weeks before return, employee and manager discuss:
  - Any team or product changes
  - Workload in first week (not expected to ramping at full speed immediately)
  - Flexible schedule options

## 12. State & Local Compliance

Northwind's parental leave policy exceeds legal minimums in all states. Employees in California, New York, and other states with paid family leave are entitled to the *greater* of:
- Northwind policy (16 weeks at 100%)
- State policy (e.g., California: 16 weeks at 70%, or 8 weeks at 100%)

Employees should notify HR of state entitlements; Northwind coordinates with state programs and tops up as needed.
