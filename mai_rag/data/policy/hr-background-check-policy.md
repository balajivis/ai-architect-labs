# Background Check Policy

## 1. Purpose

Northwind Technologies conducts background checks to:
- Ensure workplace safety and security
- Protect customer and company data
- Verify representations made in applications
- Meet regulatory requirements (especially for roles accessing sensitive data or managing funds)

This policy applies to all new hires and complies with FCRA (Fair Credit Reporting Act) and relevant state/local laws.

## 2. Scope

**2.1** Background checks are required for:
- **All new full-time employees** (no exceptions)
- **Contract workers with access to AWS/Azure credentials** or sensitive data
- **Interns and temporary staff** (if duration >30 days)

**2.2** Background checks are optional for:
- Vendors and consulting partners (assessed case-by-case)
- Temporary contractors (<30 days)

## 3. Timing & Conditions

**3.1** Background checks are:
- Conditional offers: issued after successful interview and reference check, before final acceptance
- Contingent on passing background check
- Conducted by third-party vendor (currently: Checkr or Goodhire)

**3.2** Timeline**:
- Candidate receives FCRA disclosure email (clear notification they will be checked)
- Candidate signs consent form
- Background check initiated
- Results returned within 5–7 business days (sometimes up to 14 days for international records)
- Candidate has opportunity to dispute inaccuracies (see section 5)

**3.3** If candidate declines to consent, the conditional offer is withdrawn and recruitment ends. Declination does not affect future applications after 12 months.

## 4. Check Components

**4.1 Standard Check** (all new hires):
- **Criminal history**: County / state / federal records (past 7–10 years; varies by jurisdiction)
- **Sex offender registry**: Federal and state registries
- **Social Security verification**: SSN validity and work authorization status
- **Address history**: Verification of address claims

**4.2 Enhanced Check** (roles with sensitive access):
- **Driving record**: For roles requiring vehicle operation
- **Credit check** (if applicable): For roles managing budgets or finances (requires separate consent)
- **Reference verification**: Prior employers, education
- **Court records**: Civil litigation, judgments, liens

**4.3 International Checks** (for candidates outside US):
- Criminal records and work authorization verification per country
- May add 7–14 days to timeline
- International checks are not available for all countries; candidate's home country determines feasibility

## 5. Handling of Adverse Information

**5.1** If a background check reveals potentially disqualifying information:
- Candidate receives FCRA pre-adverse notice and detailed background report
- Candidate has **7 business days** to dispute inaccuracies or provide context
- HR reviews dispute and may request clarification (e.g., "explain this felony arrest from 2015")

**5.2 Disqualifying Offenses** (automatic rejection):
- Violent crimes (assault, rape, murder)
- Sexual exploitation of minors
- Fraud, embezzlement (in past 10 years)
- Multiple DUIs or reckless driving (if role-relevant)

**5.3 Case-by-Case Review** (evaluated by HR + VP People):
- Single misdemeanor (theft, DUI, assault) depending on:
  - Recency (more recent = more serious)
  - Role relevance (theft is more disqualifying for finance roles)
  - Candidate's explanation and rehabilitation evidence
  - Industry standards and legal requirements
- Gaps in employment or education (inconsistencies with resume)
- Discrepancies in stated work history

**5.4 Legal Compliance**:
- Northwind follows "ban-the-box" rules where applicable (delay criminal record inquiry until late in process)
- Criminal record alone does not automatically disqualify; nature, recency, and relevance are considered
- Age of conviction matters: convictions >7–10 years old may be disregarded depending on jurisdiction

## 6. Data Privacy & Retention

**6.1** Background check reports:
- Stored in secure HRIS system (Workday) with restricted access (HR, Legal, VP People only)
- Retained for 3 years per legal requirements
- Not shared with hiring manager or peers
- Destroyed upon employee termination (unless subpoena or legal hold)

**6.2** Medical information**:
- If background check requires medical evaluation, results are kept separately in confidential medical file
- Not shared with hiring team
- Reasonable accommodations offered if medical condition disclosed

**6.3** Candidate communication**:
- All adverse findings are communicated to candidate in writing
- Candidate may request a copy of the background report and dispute any inaccuracy

## 7. International Candidates

**7.1** Work Authorization Verification**:
- Background check confirms right to work (visa status, green card, etc.)
- Visa sponsorship (if needed) is negotiated between candidate and HR before start date
- See Hiring & Interview Process for relocation and visa details

**7.2** Delays**:
- International background checks may add 1–3 weeks to hiring timeline
- Visa processing may add additional 1–6 months (separately from background check)

## 8. Re-Checks & Ongoing Compliance

**8.1** Periodic re-checks:
- Not typically required for existing employees
- May be required if employee changes roles (especially to higher-security roles)
- Annual re-check may be required for roles with access to highly sensitive data (VP decision)

**8.2** Adherence to Security Policies**:
- Employees are expected to report any change in legal status (criminal conviction, bankruptcy) to HR
- Material changes may trigger re-evaluation and role modification

## 9. False Information on Application

If candidate knowingly provided false information on application (e.g., lied about prior employment, criminal history), and this is discovered during or after background check:
- Conditional offer is withdrawn
- If already hired, it is grounds for immediate termination for falsification of records
