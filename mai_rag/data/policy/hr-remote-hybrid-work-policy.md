# Remote & Hybrid Work Policy

## 1. Overview

Northwind Technologies operates a **flexible hybrid-first model**. Most employees are expected to work remotely with periodic in-office days for collaboration, onboarding, and team building. Some roles require consistent office presence; these are designated at hire.

This policy applies to all employees. Contractors and interns follow similar guidelines set by their engagement letter.

## 2. Work Arrangement Types

| Type | Expected Schedule | Who | Use Case |
|------|------------------|-----|----------|
| **Remote** | Work from home (or any location) full-time | Engineering, Product, Finance | Roles not requiring daily in-office presence |
| **Hybrid** | 3 office / 2 remote days per week (default) | Sales, Marketing, some Management | Roles benefiting from in-person collaboration |
| **In-Office** | 5 days/week in Austin HQ | Facilities, Reception, some Leadership | Roles requiring daily office presence |
| **Regional Hybrid** | Mix of local office + remote | Employees in secondary locations | Austin-based office access not feasible |

## 3. Remote Work Expectations

**3.1** Remote employees must:
- Maintain core overlap hours with their team (typically 10am–3pm Central Time)
- Be reachable via Slack/email during business hours (9am–6pm local time)
- Attend mandatory in-person events (quarterly all-hands, onboarding week, offsites)
- Maintain a quiet, professional workspace for video calls
- Follow Acceptable Use Policy and data security practices (see Data Classification & Retention Policy regarding working with Confidential data remotely)

**3.2** Equipment & Home Setup**:
- Company provides laptop, monitor, keyboard, mouse, and headset
- Employees are responsible for ensuring reliable broadband (minimum 25 Mbps download)
- Company reimburses ergonomic equipment (standing desk, chair): up to $1,500
- Internet reimbursement: $50/month for employees without company broadband

**3.3 VPN & MFA**: Remote access to company systems requires VPN with mandatory MFA (see COMPANY_BIBLE.md for approved MFA methods: TOTP, hardware key, SMS fallback).

## 4. Hybrid Work Expectations

**4.1** Hybrid employees are expected in office **minimum 3 days/week** (typically Mon–Wed). Schedule flexibility is negotiated with managers within this constraint.

**4.2** Office days prioritize:
- Team syncs and sprint planning (Monday)
- Cross-functional collaboration (Tuesday–Wednesday)
- 1:1s with managers

**4.3** Remote work days are for focused individual work (deep engineering, writing, analysis).

**4.4** Office attendance is tracked via Slack location ("Office" / "Remote") and calendar visibility.

## 5. Work-from-Anywhere & Extended Travel

**5.1 Temporary Remote Work** (up to 2 weeks/quarter from another location):
- Notify manager in advance
- Must maintain core overlap hours with team
- If international: ensure tax, visa, and employment law compliance (notify People Operations)

**5.2 Extended Travel / Remote Relocation** (>1 month):
- Requires manager + People Operations approval
- May affect compensation (cost-of-living adjustment)
- Tax residency and work visa requirements apply

**5.3 Travel with Confidential Data**:
- Device must be encrypted (BitLocker, FileVault)
- Avoid public Wi-Fi; use VPN for all company access
- Do not use public charging ports (risk of data exfiltration)

See Data Classification & Retention Policy for details.

## 6. Relocation

**6.1** Employees may request relocation to a different city/state. HR approval is required and depends on:
- Role location flexibility (remote vs. in-office requirement)
- Tax and employment law implications
- Compensation adjustment (see Compensation Philosophy)

**6.2** Relocation assistance is available (see Compensation Philosophy for details: moving allowance, temporary housing, tax gross-up).

**6.3** Employees relocating outside the US must have appropriate visa sponsorship; international employment agreements may differ from this policy.

## 7. Core Overlap Hours & Communication

**7.1** Core overlap hours are **10am–3pm Central Time (CT)** for US employees. International employees work with their manager to identify overlap hours.

**7.2** Asynchronous communication is encouraged (Slack, email, recorded Loom videos) to respect time zones.

**7.3** Mandatory meetings (all-hands, annual reviews, onboarding) are scheduled during core hours.

## 8. Office Space & Amenities

**8.1** Austin HQ provides:
- Open desk seating (hot-desking; sign in via Slack or Desk Finder app)
- Private phone booths for calls
- Collaboration whiteboards
- Kitchen (coffee, tea, snacks provided)
- Fitness center (additional equipment available with reimbursement)

**8.2** Regional offices and coworking spaces:
- Finance team in Dallas (1 day/week required): WeWork 77002
- Sales hubs in SF, NYC, Chicago (meet customer-dependent)

## 9. Performance & Accountability

Remote and hybrid work arrangements do not reduce expectations for:
- Meeting project deadlines
- Attending required meetings
- Code reviews, PR quality, deployment responsibilities
- Communication and collaboration
- Performance review outcomes (see Performance Review & Feedback Process)

Poor performance in remote settings is not attributable to location; it is a performance management issue.

## 10. Policy Changes & Disputes

If a manager and employee disagree on work arrangement, the matter is escalated to the VP People. Northwind reserves the right to change work arrangements with 30 days' notice if business needs change.
