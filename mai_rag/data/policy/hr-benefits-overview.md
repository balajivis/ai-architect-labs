# Benefits Overview

## 1. Health & Medical Benefits

All full-time employees are eligible for health insurance (medical, dental, vision) on the first of the month following hire (or immediately upon hire if hired on the 1st).

**1.1 Medical Plans**:
- **PPO**: In-network deductible $500 (individual) / $1,000 (family); out-of-network $2,000/$4,000
- **HMO**: In-network only; $300 deductible; requires referrals
- Company pays 75% of premium; employee pays 25%

**1.2 Dental & Vision**:
- Dental: $50 deductible; preventive 100%, basic 80%, major 50%
- Vision: $50 deductible; frames/contacts covered once per year
- Company pays 80% of premium

**1.3 Spouse/Domestic Partner & Dependent Coverage**:
- Spouse, domestic partner (same-sex or opposite-sex), and dependent children (until age 26) may be covered
- Domestic partner must meet IRS definition of tax-dependent equivalent
- Additional premium per dependent

**1.4 Mental Health & Wellness**:
- Behavioral health coverage (therapy, psychiatry): $500 annual deductible, 80% coinsurance
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP): 5 free sessions/year with licensed counselor
- Gym reimbursement: $50/month

## 2. Retirement Benefits

**2.1 401(k) Plan**:
- Employees may contribute up to IRS limit (2026: $23,500; catch-up age 50+ $7,500)
- Company match: 100% of first 3% + 50% of next 2% (vests immediately)
- Plan administrator: Fidelity; self-directed brokerage window available
- Enrollment in payroll system; changes allowed during open enrollment (Jan) or life event

**2.2 Roth 401(k)**:
- Offered as alternative or supplementary option to traditional 401(k)
- After-tax contributions; earnings grow tax-free
- Same contribution and match rules as traditional

**2.3 Financial Planning Assistance**:
- Annual financial wellness webinars (retirement, investing, student loans)
- Employees may request one-on-one session with Fidelity advisor (free)

## 3. Paid Time Off (PTO)

**3.1 PTO Accrual**:
- **1.5 days per month** (18 days/year, or 144 hours)
- **Pro-rata accrual**: mid-month start = 0.75 days for first month
- **Maximum accrual cap**: 30 days; employees with balances ≥30 must take time off or forfeit excess at fiscal year-end
- Unused PTO is forfeited upon termination (except California, which requires payout; see Termination & Offboarding Procedure)

**3.2 Usage**:
- PTO covers sick leave, personal days, vacation, and floating holidays (4/year: Juneteenth, Thanksgiving Day after, New Year's Eve, one self-selected holiday)
- Employees may take PTO in minimum 4-hour increments
- Manager approval required 2 weeks in advance (urgent PTO may be approved with shorter notice)

**3.3 Parental Leave**: See the Parental Leave Policy (Active, 16 weeks paid) for recent updates on maternity/paternity. *Note: An older superseded policy (8 weeks) may appear in archived documents but is no longer active.*

**3.4 Sick Leave Carryover**:
- Unused sick days do not carry over; available daily as needed (doctor's note required for absences >3 consecutive days)

For comprehensive PTO rules, see the Leave & Time-Off Policy.

## 4. Life & Disability Insurance

**4.1 Life Insurance**:
- Company-provided term life: 2× annual salary (up to $500K)
- Supplemental life (employee-paid): up to 5× salary available

**4.2 Disability Insurance**:
- Short-term disability: 60% of salary for up to 26 weeks (eligible after 7-day waiting period)
- Long-term disability: 60% of salary after 26 weeks, to age 65

## 5. Professional Development

**5.1 Tuition Reimbursement**:
- Employees pursuing job-related education (certification, degree, bootcamp) may receive reimbursement
- Approved courses: up to $5,000/year (employee must earn grade C or higher or equivalent pass)
- Payback agreement: 24-month employment required post-completion or prorated repayment

**5.2 Conference & Training Budgets**:
- Engineering, Product, and Sales: $2,000/year conference or training budget
- Other functions: $1,000/year
- See Learning & Development Policy for approval process

**5.3 LinkedIn Learning License**:
- All employees receive complimentary LinkedIn Learning subscription
- Enroll via HR portal

## 6. Employee Stock Purchase Plan (ESPP)

A discounted stock purchase program is available to eligible employees (pending board approval for 2026). Details and enrollment window will be communicated separately.

## 7. Time-Off for Voting, Jury Duty, and Civic Obligations

- **Voting**: Paid time off (up to 2 hours) to vote; no advance notice required
- **Jury duty**: Full pay for first 10 business days; then 80% pay thereafter
- **Military service**: See Leave & Time-Off Policy for long-term leave

## 8. Eligibility & Enrollment

Full-time employees are eligible for all benefits. Part-time employees (≥30 hours/week) are eligible for benefits on a pro-rata basis.

**Open Enrollment** occurs annually in December/January; changes are effective January 1. New hires enroll within 30 days of hire.
