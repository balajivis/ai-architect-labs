# Per Diem & Meals Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes guidelines for meal expenses during business travel and at home office locations. Meals must be business-related and documented according to receipt thresholds. Per diem allowances are capped daily; employees may not claim above the cap.

## 2. Business Meal Categories

### 2.1 Reimbursable Business Meals

**While traveling:**
- Breakfast, lunch, dinner while on approved business travel (per diem limits apply)
- Solo meals at destination (traveling employee expense)
- Team meals where all attendees are on approved business travel

**At home office:**
- Client entertainment meals (with clients present)
- Team lunches (pre-approved by department manager; budgeted event)
- Customer meetings or sales meals (business purpose documented)

**Non-travel meals NOT reimbursed:**
- Meals at personal desk during work (personal responsibility)
- Solo meals at home office (commuting meals; personal)
- Personal entertainment or recreational meals

### 2.2 Travel Per Diem Limits

**Daily meal limit while traveling:**

| Meal | Max Cost | Daily Total Cap |
|---|---|---|
| Breakfast | $15 | **$85/day** |
| Lunch | $25 | (sum of all meals) |
| Dinner | $50 | |

**Example**: If you have breakfast ($12), lunch ($20), and dinner ($48), total = $80, which is under the $85 daily cap. All reimbursed.

**Tip calculation**: Tips (15–20%) are part of the meal cost; tip does not exceed the meal category limit unless documented (e.g., forced gratuity on group meal).

### 2.3 Receipt Requirement: $75 Threshold

**Anchor fact per company bible:** Expenses over $75 require an itemized receipt.

| Situation | Receipt Required |
|---|---|
| **Single meal >$75** | Yes, itemized receipt |
| **Daily meal total >$75** | Yes, itemized receipt(s) for each meal over threshold |
| **Meal <$75** | No, estimated cost acceptable |
| **Meal $75 exactly** | Yes, itemized receipt |

**Itemized receipt** means the receipt shows individual line items (e.g., "grilled salmon $35, Caesar salad $12, water $3, tip $5" = $55 total). Credit card statement alone is insufficient for receipts over $75.

## 3. Meal Reimbursement by Scenario

### 3.1 Solo Meals While Traveling

**Allowed**: Individual traveling on business trip may expense own meals.

**Documentation**:
- Breakfast <$15: No receipt needed
- Lunch <$25: No receipt needed
- Dinner <$50: No receipt needed
- Any meal over respective category limit or >$75 total: Itemized receipt required

**Example**: Traveler spends $18 on breakfast (over $15 limit). If receipt shows individual items and total is <$75, estimated claim OK. If breakfast alone is >$75, itemized receipt required.

### 3.2 Team Meals While Traveling

**Allowed**: One person may expense a meal for the team if:
- All attendees are on approved business travel (not one person off-site, others at home office)
- Single itemized receipt shows meal for entire group
- Attendee names listed on receipt or in expense note

**Reimbursement**: Expensed by one person; others do not double-claim same meal.

**Example**: Four employees traveling to conference. Team dinner costs $180 ($45/person). One person expenses $180; others do not expense this meal. Receipt lists all 4 names and itemization.

### 3.3 Client Entertainment Meals

**Allowed**: Meals with clients, prospects, or partners are reimbursable.

**Requirements**:
- Business purpose documented (e.g., "Lunch with Acme Corp VP to discuss Q4 proposal")
- All attendees listed by name
- Attendee count and their organizations (client vs. Northwind employee)
- Itemized receipt if meal >$75
- Reasonable cost (not excessive alcohol or premium restaurant without business justification)

**Daily limit**: Client entertainment meals are NOT subject to the $85/day cap (separate line item). However, reasonableness still applies (e.g., $500 dinner for 2 people would be questioned).

**Alcohol**: Allowed as part of client meal; personal minibar use is not reimbursed.

### 3.4 Team Lunches at Home Office

**Allowed**: Department may conduct team lunch as morale event.

**Process**:
1. **Department manager pre-approves** team lunch event (budget and headcount)
2. **One person purchases** lunch (usually department manager or admin)
3. **That person expenses** total cost
4. **Itemized receipt** required if meal >$75
5. **Attendee list** and business purpose noted (e.g., "Team Q2 kickoff lunch, 8 attendees")

**Cost ceiling**: $15/person is reasonable ($120 for 8 people); exceeding this may require CFO approval.

## 4. Non-Reimbursable Meal Expenses

- **Personal meals** at desk or home office (employee's own responsibility)
- **Alcohol** outside of business meal context (solo drinking, personal minibar)
- **High-end restaurants** without business justification (e.g., Michelin-star solo lunch is personal expense)
- **Meal upgrades** (premium seating, wine pairings) beyond business meal cost
- **Dietary restrictions** accommodation beyond reasonable (e.g., vegan meal costs more; difference is employee responsibility if not disclosed in advance)

## 5. Meal Submission and Documentation

### 5.1 Expense Report Entry

1. **Open Expensify** or accounting system
2. **Create meal expense** in appropriate category:
   - Travel > Meals (while traveling)
   - Entertainment > Meals (clients)
   - Team > Meals (team lunches)
3. **Enter amount** and meal type (breakfast, lunch, dinner)
4. **Attach receipt** if >$75:
   - Photo via Expensify mobile app
   - PDF from vendor email
   - Receipt image (legible)
5. **Add notes**:
   - Location (restaurant name, city)
   - Business purpose (who, what, why)
   - Attendee names (if team or client meal)
6. **Submit to manager** for approval

### 5.2 Missing Receipt for Meal >$75

If you cannot provide receipt for a meal >$75:

1. **Submit affidavit** (signed statement) in Expensify:
   - Date of meal
   - Location and restaurant name
   - Attendees (names and organization)
   - Business purpose
   - Estimated amount
   - Reason receipt is missing (lost, no receipt provided by vendor, photo failure)

2. **Manager approves** affidavit or rejects claim

3. **Finance reviews**: Meals >$500 without receipt may be denied unless extraordinary circumstances

**Best practice**: Take photos of receipt immediately upon payment to avoid loss.

## 6. Regional and International Meal Adjustments

### 6.1 High Cost-of-Living Cities

For travel to expensive cities (NYC, SF, LA, London, Tokyo), Finance may adjust per diem limits:

| City | Adjusted Limits |
|---|---|
| NYC, SF, LA | +25% to standard limits (Dinner max $62.50) |
| London, Tokyo, Singapore | +30% (Dinner max $65) |
| Other major metros | Standard limits |

**Approval**: Department manager requests adjustment in travel approval; Finance pre-approves if city is known.

### 6.2 International Travel

**Meal documentation**: Same rules apply; use local currency on receipt; Finance converts to USD at day-of-expense rate.

**Receipts**: May be in foreign language; itemization rule still applies (must show meal items, not just total).

**Tipping**: Varies by country; reasonable local tip (not US standard 20%) is acceptable.

## 7. Policy Violations and Enforcement

### 7.1 Violation Examples
- Claiming meal expenses without receipt when >$75 threshold applies
- Double-claiming team meal (both person who expensed and attendee claim)
- Expensing personal meals at desk as business
- Falsifying attendee names or business purpose
- Exceeding daily cap without adjusting for expensive city

### 7.2 Disciplinary Escalation
- **First violation**: Expense denied (not reimbursed); verbal warning
- **Second violation**: Expense denied; written warning; expense training required
- **Third violation**: Suspension of travel authorization for 30 days; escalated to HR

## 8. Interaction with Per Diem and Per Diems vs. Actual Expenses

### 8.1 Northwind Policy: Actual Expenses (Not Per Diem)

Northwind reimburses **actual meal expenses**, not a flat daily per diem. This means:
- You expense actual meals you purchased (not a flat $85/day)
- Receipt requirement ($75 threshold) applies
- Daily cap is a safety ceiling (cannot exceed $85/day of actual meals)

**This is different from** some companies that give a flat per diem (e.g., $80/day regardless of actual spending).
