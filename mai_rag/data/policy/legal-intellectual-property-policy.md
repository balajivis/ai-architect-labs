# Intellectual Property Policy

## Purpose
This policy establishes Northwind Technologies' framework for creating, protecting, assigning, and licensing intellectual property (IP). All employees, contractors, and partners must comply with this policy to ensure Northwind retains ownership of IP created during their engagement.

## 1. IP Ownership Framework

**Northwind owns all IP** created by employees and contractors in the course of performing their duties, including:
- Software code, algorithms, and architecture
- Documentation, specifications, and technical designs
- Data processing techniques, methodologies, and processes
- Product enhancements and derivative works
- Content created for Northwind products or marketing

**IP created outside of work**, on personal time without using Northwind resources, remains the creator's property, **unless**:
- It relates to Northwind's business, products, or customers
- It was developed using Northwind intellectual property or confidential information
- The creator's employment agreement specifies otherwise

## 2. Employee & Contractor Agreements

**All employment contracts** must include an IP assignment clause. Standard language:

> "Employee hereby assigns to Northwind all right, title, and interest in any inventions, discoveries, works of authorship, software, designs, and other intellectual property created, conceived, or reduced to practice by Employee during the term of employment, whether or not created during working hours or using Northwind resources, that relate in any way to Northwind's business, products, services, research, or development."

**All contractor/consultant agreements** must include similar language with work-for-hire provisions:

> "Contractor agrees that all work product created under this Agreement shall be considered 'work made for hire' under applicable law, and shall be owned exclusively by Northwind upon creation. To the extent work made for hire does not apply, Contractor hereby assigns all IP to Northwind."

**Exceptions** are documented in the employment or contractor agreement and approved by the General Counsel.

## 3. IP Categories & Protection

| IP Type | Protection Method | Ownership | Duration | Register Where |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Software Code** | Copyright + Trade Secret | Northwind | Life + 70 years | Copyright Office (if published) |
| **Algorithms & Designs** | Trade Secret | Northwind | Indefinite (if secret) | Internal IP Register |
| **Product Names/Logos** | Trademark | Northwind | Indefinite (if renewed) | USPTO + WIPO |
| **Product Features & Specs** | Trade Secret | Northwind | Indefinite (if secret) | Internal IP Register |
| **Patents** | Patent Grant | Northwind | 20 years from filing | USPTO + International |
| **Documentation** | Copyright | Northwind | Life + 70 years | Internal IP Register |

## 4. Patent & Invention Disclosure

Employees who invent or discover patentable subject matter must file an **Invention Disclosure Form** with the General Counsel within 30 days of conception. Northwind decides within 60 days whether to:
- File a patent application (U.S. and/or international)
- Keep the invention as trade secret
- Release the invention (rare)

**Patent ownership** remains with Northwind. The inventor may be named as an inventor on the patent application (a non-transferable honor) and may receive recognition in company communications.

## 5. Open Source & Third-Party IP

**Northwind's Use of Open Source** — See **Open Source Software Use Policy** for detailed guidelines. In summary:
- Permissive licenses (MIT, Apache 2.0, BSD) are preferred and pre-approved
- GPL and copyleft licenses require VP Engineering and General Counsel approval before integration into distributed products
- All open source dependencies must be tracked in `DEPENDENCIES.md` and scanned for vulnerabilities quarterly

**Third-Party IP in Northwind Products** — Any incorporation of third-party IP (code, designs, methodologies) into Northwind deliverables requires:
1. Legal review of the license terms
2. Confirmation of Northwind's right to use and sublicense
3. Documentation of attribution and license obligations
4. MSA/contract amendments if necessary to flow down third-party terms to customers

**Northwind Licensing to Customers** — Northwind grants customers a non-exclusive, limited license to use the Northwind Cloud platform for their internal business purposes. Customers may not:
- Reverse engineer or decompile Northwind software
- Create derivative works
- Sublicense or resell access to other parties without written consent
- Use Northwind IP in competitive products

## 6. Confidentiality & Trade Secret Protection

To maintain trade secret protection for Northwind IP:
1. **Limited Access** — Access to Northwind source code, architecture, and confidential designs is restricted to employees with business justification
2. **Marked Documents** — Code repositories and design documents must be marked "Northwind Confidential" or equivalent
3. **Encryption** — Source code and design documentation in transit must be encrypted (see **Data Classification & Retention Policy**)
4. **NDA Compliance** — All employees, contractors, and partners sign NDAs covering Northwind IP (see **NDA Policy**)
5. **Non-Disclosure** — Employees may not discuss Northwind IP in public forums, conferences, or publications without VP Engineering and General Counsel approval

## 7. IP Disputes & Remedies

- **Internal Disputes** — If ownership is unclear, the General Counsel reviews employment agreements and invention records to determine ownership
- **External Disputes** — Northwind will defend its IP against infringement claims via litigation or settlement as directed by the General Counsel and CEO
- **Notification** — Any suspected infringement of Northwind IP by third parties must be reported to the General Counsel within 5 business days

## 8. License Obligations & Recordation

When Northwind licenses IP from third parties (software, patents, trademarks), the General Counsel maintains a central registry including:
- Licensor name and IP description
- License grant scope (internal use, resale, modification rights)
- Term and renewal dates
- Sublicensing rights and customer attribution requirements
- Payment obligations

Compliance with third-party license obligations is audited annually.
