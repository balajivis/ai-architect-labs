# Performance Review & Feedback Process

## 1. Purpose & Cycle

Performance reviews align individual goals with company strategy, document growth, and inform compensation decisions. Northwind Technologies conducts:

- **Continuous feedback**: informal 1:1s (bi-weekly minimum)
- **Formal reviews**: annual (typically in January) + mid-year pulse check (July)
- **90-day reviews**: for all new hires (see Employee Onboarding Guide)

All reviews are documented in the HR information system (Workday) and archived for legal compliance.

## 2. Review Components

**2.1 Goal Setting** (Q1 planning): Employee and manager collaborate on 3–5 measurable objectives aligned to role and company OKRs. Goals are tracked in Workday.

**2.2 Self-Assessment** (2 weeks before formal review): Employee completes a written reflection on accomplishments, challenges, and development areas.

**2.3 Manager Feedback**: Manager writes a documented assessment covering:
- Goal achievement (quantified where possible)
- Behavioral/competency strengths
- Areas for development
- Overall performance rating (see section 4)

**2.4 Calibration** (HR facilitated): Peer managers discuss ratings to ensure consistency and fairness across departments.

**2.5 Review Conversation**: Manager and employee meet to discuss feedback, ratings, and next-period goals. Employee may provide written rebuttal within 5 days.

**2.6 360° Feedback** (annually for managers): Upward, peer, and cross-functional feedback collected and shared to inform leadership development (see Learning & Development Policy).

## 3. Documentation & Record Keeping

All review documents—goals, self-assessment, manager feedback, and ratings—are stored in Workday with restricted access (employee, manager, HR, General Counsel). Reviews are retained for seven years per legal requirements.

Employees have the right to review their personnel file and request corrections if factual errors are found. Requests are reviewed by People Operations within 10 business days.

## 4. Performance Ratings & Distribution

| Rating | Definition | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Exceeds** | Consistently delivers beyond expectations; high impact | Eligible for bonus and accelerated raise |
| **Meets** | Meets all goals and expectations | Standard annual raise eligible |
| **Developing** | Making progress but below expectations in one or more areas | Performance improvement plan may be offered |
| **Below** | Fails to meet core job requirements | Formal PIP with 30–90-day review; may lead to termination |

## 5. Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)

An employee placed on a PIP receives:
- Clear, measurable success criteria (e.g., code review turnaround, customer satisfaction score)
- Check-in meetings with manager bi-weekly
- Support resources (training, mentoring, coaching)
- 30-, 60-, and 90-day evaluation milestones

A PIP typically lasts 30–90 days. If improvement is demonstrated, the employee may return to standard performance management. Failure to meet PIP criteria may result in termination.

## 6. Appeals & Fairness

Employees who disagree with a review rating may appeal to the VP People within 10 business days. The appeal is reviewed by HR and the manager's director. A documented decision is provided within 10 business days.

Discrimination or retaliation related to performance reviews is prohibited (see Anti-Harassment & Non-Discrimination Policy and Code of Conduct & Professional Standards).

## 7. Compensation & Promotion Decisions

Performance ratings directly influence:
- **Annual raises**: Exceeds ratings typically receive 3–5% raises; Meets 1–3%; Developing 0–1%; Below 0%
- **Bonuses**: Discretionary bonuses based on company profitability and individual ratings
- **Promotions**: Candidates must have "Meets" or "Exceeds" ratings in two consecutive reviews

See Compensation Philosophy for detail.
