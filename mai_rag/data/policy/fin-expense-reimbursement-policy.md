# Expense Reimbursement Policy (Detailed)

## 1. Overview and Scope

This policy governs the reimbursement of business expenses incurred by Northwind Technologies employees, contractors, and authorized representatives. All expenses must be business-related, reasonable, and properly documented. Employees are responsible for complying with this policy and federal tax regulations (per IRS guidelines for accountable plans).

**Policy applies to:**
- Employees (full-time, part-time, contractors on company engagements)
- Consultants with signed service agreements
- Board members on official company business

## 2. Expense Categories and Limits

### 2.1 Travel Expenses
Travel expenses are governed by the **Expense & Travel Policy** and **Travel Booking Policy**. Key anchor facts:
- Flights: Economy class domestic; VP approval required for business class on flights over 6 hours
- Hotels: Tiered by city (Major metro max $250/night; mid-size $150; small town $100, all pre-tax)
- Ground transportation: Economy rentals, rideshare base tiers, personal vehicle at IRS rate ($0.655/mile)
- **Meals while traveling**: See **Per Diem & Meals Policy** for allowances and receipt thresholds

### 2.2 Meals and Entertainment (Non-Travel)
- **Business meals** (with clients or team at home office): Reimbursable with business purpose documented
- **Solo meals at office**: Not reimbursable (meals at desk during work are personal responsibility)
- **Team lunches or morale events**: Finance pre-approves budgeted team events; individuals may expense if approved
- **Client entertainment**: Meals with clients are reimbursable with attendee names and business purpose on receipt
- **Alcohol**: Reimbursable only as part of a meal with clients/team; solo alcohol is personal expense

**Receipt requirement**: All meals/entertainment over $75 require itemized receipt.

### 2.3 Office and Supplies
- **Office supplies** (pens, paper, folders, desk accessories): Department manager approves; expenses under $50 typically bundled
- **Equipment** (monitors, keyboards, ergonomic chairs): Must be pre-approved by IT or department manager; over $200 requires Capital Expenditure approval (see **Capital Expenditure Policy**)
- **Software subscriptions**: IT must verify license compliance; reimbursed monthly/annually per Procurement
- **Books and training materials**: Job-related books and courses are reimbursable; pre-approval recommended for over $100

### 2.4 Professional Development
- **Conference registration**: Manager and VP approval required; covered under annual travel budget (see **Expense & Travel Policy**)
- **Certification exams**: Job-related certifications (AWS, Azure, security certs) are reimbursable; pre-approval recommended
- **Membership fees**: Professional memberships (IEEE, PMI, legal bar association) are reimbursable if role-related; HR pre-approves

### 2.5 Other Business Expenses
- **Client gifts**: Limited to $50/recipient/year; documented with recipient name and business purpose
- **Phone/internet**: Personal phone/broadband not reimbursable; company-issued devices are covered
- **Parking and tolls**: Business-related parking, tolls, and EZPass charges are reimbursable
- **Postage and shipping**: Business correspondence and product shipments are reimbursable

## 3. Receipt and Documentation Requirements

### 3.1 Receipt Threshold
**Expenses over $75 require an itemized receipt.** This is an anchor fact per Northwind's accounting standards.

- **Itemized receipt** shows individual line items (e.g., meal items or component costs)
- **Credit card statement alone** is insufficient for expenses over $75
- **Cash receipts**: Keep original receipt or take photo via Expensify app

### 3.2 Acceptable Receipt Formats
- Original paper receipt
- Email confirmation from vendor (e.g., Uber, flight booking)
- Photo of receipt (Expensify mobile app)
- PDF from vendor email
- Itemized credit card receipt (merchant copy)

### 3.3 Missing Receipt Process
If receipt is missing and expense exceeds $75:
1. Submit expense in Expensify with note explaining loss
2. Finance team may request **affidavit** (signed statement) confirming business purpose
3. Affidavit must include: date, location, attendees, business purpose, amount
4. Manager approval is required; expense approval still requires Finance review

Expenses over $500 without receipt are typically denied unless extraordinary circumstances (e.g., hardware failure, theft during travel).

## 4. Submission and Approval Workflow

### 4.1 Timing
- **Submit within 30 days** of expense date (preferred)
- **Absolute deadline**: 90 days after expense date
- Expenses submitted after 90 days are not reimbursed (no exceptions)

### 4.2 Submission Process
1. **Use Expensify** (corporate expense management system): Upload receipt, categorize expense, add business purpose
2. **Manager approval**: Manager reviews and approves within 3 business days
3. **Finance review**: Finance team validates receipt, amount, category, and policy compliance
4. **Reimbursement**: Processed via direct deposit within 2–3 pay periods after approval

### 4.3 Multi-Person Expenses
- One person may expense a meal for the group if all attendees are on approved business travel
- Attendee names must be listed on receipt or submission note
- The person expensing must be present (no proxy reimbursement)

## 5. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are **not reimbursable**:
- Personal entertainment (concerts, movies, personal gym membership)
- Childcare or dependent care (unless part of corporate event)
- Commuting (personal vehicle mileage to/from home office; business travel mileage is reimbursed)
- Traffic violations, parking tickets, towing fees
- Haircuts, personal grooming, dry cleaning (unless travel laundry over 5 days)
- Pet care or house sitting
- Spouse or companion travel
- Personal insurance or medical expenses
- Charitable donations (company makes donations separately)

## 6. Policy Violations and Enforcement

### 6.1 Violation Examples
- Submitting expense without receipt when receipt is required
- Falsifying receipt or business purpose
- Expensing personal entertainment as business meal
- Violating hotel nightly limits (see **Travel Booking Policy**)
- Submitting duplicate expenses

### 6.2 Disciplinary Action
- **First violation**: Expense denied; verbal warning; employee must attend expense compliance training
- **Second violation**: Expense denied; written warning; expense approval authority suspended for 30 days
- **Third violation**: Escalated to HR; suspension from expense reimbursement for 90 days; may result in termination for cause

## 7. Corporate Card Integration

Northwind employees with corporate cards must follow this same reimbursement policy. Personal card charges are reimbursed per this policy; corporate card statements are reconciled and audited by Finance monthly.

See **Corporate Card Policy** for card-specific guidelines and limits.

## 8. Tax and Accounting Compliance

All reimbursements are processed through Northwind's accounting system and reported to payroll. Reimbursements are non-taxable if they comply with IRS Section 162 (ordinary and necessary business expenses) and our accountable plan rules:
- Expenses must have business purpose
- Documentation must be adequate
- Reimbursement must be timely

Finance maintains expense records for 7 years per IRS and audit requirements.
